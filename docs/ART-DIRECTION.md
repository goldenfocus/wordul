# Word Wiki — Art Direction (LOCKED house style)

> Source of truth for every per-word AI image generated by
> `scripts/gen-word-images.mjs`. Do **not** improvise per-word. The whole point of a wiki
> with thousands of pages is that they feel like one publication, not a stock-image grab
> bag. Lock the style here; the generator appends it verbatim to every prompt.

---

## Palette (the only colors)

| Token        | Hex       | Role |
|--------------|-----------|------|
| Ultraviolet  | `#9d8bff` | Primary brand / answer-tile color, key light, hero accent |
| Gold         | `#f0c14b` | Reward / highlight / type accent, secondary light |
| Near-black   | `#15101f` | Background, the obsidian cave; deep shadow |
| Light        | `#f7f1e3` | Warm off-white; paper, fog, rim light |
| Muted        | `#bdb6c9` | Lavender-grey; mid-tones, supporting copy color |

These mirror the live brand exactly (`public/share-card.js` →
`BRAND = { bg:#15101f, gold:#f0c14b, accent:#9d8bff, fg:#f7f1e3, muted:#bdb6c9 }`).
Images must read as if lit by ultraviolet + gold against near-black. No teal, no orange,
no neon green, no generic "AI gradient" rainbow.

---

## The ONE fixed style suffix

Appended verbatim to **every** prompt by the generator (`STYLE_SUFFIX` constant). Treat as
locked — changing it re-skins the entire wiki, so change it deliberately, in one place.

```
Editorial vignette illustration, single clear subject, cinematic rim lighting in
ultraviolet (#9d8bff) and warm gold (#f0c14b) against a deep near-black (#15101f)
background, soft volumetric fog, painterly semi-realistic style with clean shapes and
generous negative space, centered composition with room to overlay type, muted lavender
mid-tones (#bdb6c9), warm off-white highlights (#f7f1e3), tasteful film grain, cohesive
poster aesthetic, high craft, no text.
```

The trailing **"no text"** is load-bearing — see the next rule.

---

## Rule: NO text, letters, or words inside the image

The model must never render letters, captions, watermarks, signage, or numerals. **All
type — the word, the definition, the wordul.com mark — is overlaid afterward in
compositing** (sharp SVG overlay, same brand frame as the OG cards in
`scripts/lib/og-card.mjs`). Why:

1. Diffusion models can't spell. Garbled letters scream "AI slop" and break trust on an
   indexed, citation-worthy page.
2. We control type for accessibility, alt-text, localization, and crisp rendering.

This is enforced two ways: `"no text"` in the style suffix **and** the negative prompt
below. Belt and suspenders.

---

## 3-slot spec

Every answer word gets exactly **3** images. Same word, three lenses. Keys in R2
(bucket `wordul-og`, reusing the OG bucket):

| Slot | R2 key                 | Depicts |
|------|------------------------|---------|
| 1 — hero / meaning-scene | `hero/<slug>.webp`     | The word's **primary meaning** as a single cinematic scene — what the word *is*, shown literally and beautifully. One subject, poster-grade. |
| 2 — mnemonic ("see it to spell it") | `mnemonic/<slug>.webp` | A vivid, slightly surreal memory hook that makes the word **stick** — an exaggerated, dreamlike association you can't un-see. Memorable over literal. |
| 3 — etymology vignette | `etymology/<slug>.webp` | A quiet historical vignette evoking the word's **origin** (the root language / era / image the word grew from). Antique, archival mood — still inside our palette. |

All three share the locked style suffix, so the trio reads as a set.

---

## Negative prompt (appended to every generation)

```
text, letters, words, captions, watermark, signature, logo, typography, numbers,
subtitles, UI, frame border, meme, lowres, blurry, jpeg artifacts, deformed, extra
limbs, extra fingers, mutated hands, oversaturated, neon rainbow, teal-and-orange,
stock-photo look, cluttered background, busy composition.
```

Flux Schnell does not take a separate negative-prompt field; the generator folds these as
an explicit `Avoid: …` clause into the prompt and relies on `"no text"` in the suffix.

## Edge cases (handled in the generator)

### Homographs — illustrate the verified primary sense
Many answer words have multiple unrelated meanings (CRANE = the bird *and* the lifting
machine; BASS = fish *and* low sound; SEWER = drain *and* one who sews). **Never let the
model guess.** The generator seeds the hero prompt with the word's verified definition
from `public/data/word-intel.js` (`def`), so the prompt anchors on the **primary,
fact-checked sense** — e.g. CRANE resolves to the long-necked wading bird because that's
what the verified `def` describes. If a word has no `def` on file, the generator skips it
(no illustration on an unverified meaning) rather than risk depicting the wrong thing.

### Sensitive / offensive words — deny-list → poster-only fallback
Some valid answer words are slurs, sexual, violent, or otherwise not something we want an
AI to illustrate on a public page. These get **no AI illustration at all**:

1. The generator reads the same exclusion source the wiki uses
   (`src/word-exclusions.ts` → `WORD_EXCLUSIONS`) **plus** an in-script `DENY_LIST` of
   sensitive-but-still-indexable words.
2. Any word on either list is rendered as a **poster-only fallback** — the plain branded
   tile/word card (the existing `og-card.mjs` frame), no diffusion call, no spend.
3. This is fail-safe: when in doubt, a word gets the clean poster, never an AI guess at
   something offensive.

Excluded words (`WORD_EXCLUSIONS`) have no public page at all, so they're skipped
entirely. The `DENY_LIST` covers the gray zone: words that *do* get an indexed page but
that we don't want diffused.

---

## Quick reference for the generator

- Style suffix: one constant, appended to all 3 prompts.
- Type: never in the pixels — always composited after.
- Homograph anchor: verified `def` from `public/data/word-intel.js`.
- Sensitive: `WORD_EXCLUSIONS` (skip) + in-script `DENY_LIST` (poster-only).
- Output: WebP, keys `hero/`, `mnemonic/`, `etymology/` in bucket `wordul-og`.
