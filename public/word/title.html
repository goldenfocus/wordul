TITLE

noun · 2 syllables · /'taɪ.təl/

TITLE

What does "TITLE" mean?

The name given to a book, film, song, or other work, or a word denoting rank or office.

Meanings

  1. The name of a book, film, artwork, or other creative work. I forgot the title of that novel, but I remember the cover.
  2. A word or phrase showing a person's rank, office, or status, such as 'Doctor' or 'Queen'. She earned the title of professor at thirty-five.
  3. A championship won in sport or another competition. The team clinched the league title in the final minute.
  4. The legal right to ownership of property, or the document proving it. The bank holds the title to the car until the loan is paid off. technical
  5. To give a name or heading to something. She titled the painting 'After the Rain'.

Did you know?

  • 'Title' and the wavy tilde mark (~) are long-lost cousins: both come from the Latin 'titulus', a label or heading written above the text.

Word origin

From Latin 'titulus', meaning 'inscription, label, or heading', via Old French 'title'; the same root gives 'titular' and lies behind the diacritic 'tilde'.
