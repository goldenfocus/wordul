HORNY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'hɔːr.ni/

HORNY

What does "HORNY" mean?

Made of, resembling, or hardened like horn; informally, sexually aroused.

Meanings

  1. Hard and rough like horn; calloused. Years of labour had left his hands horny and cracked.
  2. Having horns or horn-like projections. The horny toad is actually a lizard covered in spiny scales.
  3. Sexually aroused or eager. He sent an embarrassingly horny text and regretted it instantly. informal

Did you know?

  • The tough outermost layer of your skin is the 'stratum corneum' - literally the 'horny layer', sharing a root with horn because it is built from the same hardening protein, keratin.

Word origin

From 'horn' (Old English 'horn', a hard pointed growth) + the adjectival '-y'; the sexual sense is a later slang extension dating to the 19th century.

Remember it

HORNY = HORN + Y: the original meaning is simply 'hard as horn'; the Y just makes the horn an adjective.
