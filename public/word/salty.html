SALTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'sɔːl.ti/

SALTY

What does "SALTY" mean?

Tasting of or containing salt; informally, bitter, irritated, or resentful.

Meanings

  1. Tasting of, containing, or preserved with salt. The broth was too salty to finish.
  2. Bitter, annoyed, or resentful, especially after a loss or insult. He's still salty about losing the bet last week. informal
  3. Coarse or earthy in speech; relating to sailors or the sea. The old captain told salty tales of his years at sea. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'sealt' ('salt') plus '-y'; the slang sense of 'bitter or resentful' arose in American English, drawing on the sour sting of salt.

Remember it

SALTY = SALT + Y: both the taste and the bitter mood leave the same sting on the tongue.
