SNEAK

What does "SNEAK" mean?

To move or do something quietly and secretly to avoid being noticed.

Meanings

  1. To move quietly and furtively in order to avoid being seen or heard. He tried to sneak past the sleeping dog.
  2. To do something or take something secretly or stealthily. She sneaked a cookie before dinner.
  3. A person who behaves in a furtive, underhanded, or cowardly way. Nobody trusts him; he's a sneak who reports everything to the boss. informal
  4. Done or carried out without warning; unexpected. The army launched a sneak attack at dawn.

Did you know?

  • 'Snuck' is a linguistic latecomer: 'sneak' was historically a regular verb (sneaked), and 'snuck' only spread widely in American English during the 20th century, yet dictionaries now list both.

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Old English 'snican' (to creep or crawl); related to the same Germanic root that gives 'snake'.
