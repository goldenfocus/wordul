ADORN

verb · 2 syllables · /əˈdɔːrn/

ADORN

What does "ADORN" mean?

To make more beautiful or attractive by adding decoration.

Meanings

  1. To add beauty to something by decorating it. Garlands of flowers adorned the doorway.
  2. To lend distinction or grace to something by one's presence or qualities. Her wit adorned every conversation she entered. formal

Word origin

From Latin 'adornare' (to equip, embellish), from 'ad-' (to) plus 'ornare' (to furnish, decorate) - the same 'ornare' behind 'ornate' and 'ornament'.

Remember it

ADORN contains 'ORN' as in ORNament - to adorn is to add an ornament.
