UNFED

adjective · 2 syllables · /ʌnˈfɛd/

UNFED

What does "UNFED" mean?

Not given food; not having eaten.

Meanings

  1. Having received no food; left without nourishment. The stray dog had clearly gone unfed for days.
  2. Not supplied with what is needed to sustain or grow (figurative). An ambition left unfed will starve as surely as a body. figurative

Word origin

A compound of the negative prefix 'un-' and 'fed', the past participle of 'feed', from Old English 'fedan' (to nourish), related to 'food' and 'fodder'.

Remember it

UNFED = UN + FED: the negative prefix on the past of 'feed' - it simply means 'not fed'.
