FOGGY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈfɒɡ.i/

FOGGY

What does "FOGGY" mean?

Full of or obscured by fog; unclear or confused.

Meanings

  1. Filled with or hidden by fog. The harbor was so foggy we could not see the boats.
  2. Unclear, hazy, or confused in thought or memory. My memory of that night is still pretty foggy.
  3. Blurred or clouded, as a lens or window. He wiped his foggy glasses on his shirt.

Word origin

From 'fog' (origin uncertain, perhaps Scandinavian, related to Danish 'fog' meaning spray or drift) plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

FOGGY has two G's stuck together like droplets clumping in thick fog - the more G's, the thicker the haze.
