ITCHY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'ɪtʃ.i/

ITCHY

What does "ITCHY" mean?

Having or causing an uncomfortable feeling on the skin that makes you want to scratch.

Meanings

  1. Producing or affected by an itch; irritating to the skin. The wool sweater left my arms itchy all afternoon.
  2. Restless or impatient, eager to do or have something. After a week indoors she was itchy to get back on the road. figurative

Did you know?

  • Itch isn't just mild pain - in 2009 researchers identified nerve cells that carry itch on their own dedicated pathway, separate from the ones that signal pain.

Word origin

From 'itch' (Old English 'giccan', to itch) plus the adjective suffix '-y'; the restlessness sense extends the bodily urge to a mental one.

Remember it

ITCHY: the 'tch' is the scratch itself - say it and you can almost feel your nails on skin.
