CLOUD

noun · 1 syllable · /klaʊd/

CLOUD

What does "CLOUD" mean?

A visible mass of water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the atmosphere.

Meanings

  1. A visible mass of condensed water vapour floating in the sky. A single white cloud drifted over the valley.
  2. A network of remote servers for storing and processing data, with 'the'. All our files are backed up to the cloud.
  3. A mass of something suspended in the air, such as dust or smoke. A cloud of dust rose behind the truck.
  4. A source of gloom, worry, or suspicion. The scandal cast a cloud over his career. figurative
  5. To make or become unclear or obscured. Anger clouded his judgement.

Did you know?

  • The Latin cloud names you still hear on the forecast - cumulus, stratus, cirrus - were invented by an amateur meteorologist, Luke Howard, in 1802.
  • In Old English, 'clud' meant a rock or a hill; the word only floated up to the sky later, borrowed for the rocky-looking heaps of cumulus.

Word origin

From Old English 'clūd', which meant a rock or hill; the sense shifted to 'cloud' from the visual likeness of a billowing cumulus to a mass of rock.
