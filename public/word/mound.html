MOUND

noun · 1 syllable · /maʊnd/

MOUND

What does "MOUND" mean?

A rounded heap or raised pile of earth, stones, or other material.

Meanings

  1. A heap or bank of earth, stones, or debris, natural or made by people. Archaeologists trenched into the grassy mound to find the burial chamber.
  2. A large pile or quantity of something. A mound of unread paperwork waited on the desk. informal
  3. The slightly raised pitching area at the center of a baseball diamond. The closer walked slowly to the mound in the ninth inning. technical
  4. To form into or heap up as a rounded pile. She mounded the mashed potatoes high on the plate.

Did you know?

  • A baseball pitcher throws downhill: the rulebook puts the top of the mound exactly 10 inches above home plate, and that small slope adds real speed and bite to the pitch.

Word origin

Of uncertain origin; in early use meaning 'hedge, boundary, or protective bank', possibly from Old English 'mund', 'protection, hand', later shifting to the sense of a heap of earth.

Remember it

MOUND = a 'round' heap with an M in front; picture an M-shaped hill that's been smoothed ROUND.
