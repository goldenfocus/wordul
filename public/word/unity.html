UNITY

noun · 3 syllables · /ˈjuː.nɪ.ti/

UNITY

What does "UNITY" mean?

The state of being joined together or in agreement to form a single whole.

Meanings

  1. The condition of being united or of people working in harmony. The crisis brought a rare moment of national unity.
  2. A single thing forming a complete and harmonious whole, especially in art. Critics praised the painting's unity of color and composition.
  3. The number one. In probability, all the outcomes must sum to unity. technical

Did you know?

  • Mathematicians use 'unity' as a proper name for the number 1; the 'roots of unity' are the complex numbers that, when raised to a whole-number power, loop perfectly back to 1.

Word origin

From Latin 'unitas' ('oneness'), from 'unus' ('one'), entering English through Old French 'unite' in the 14th century.
