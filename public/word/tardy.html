TARDY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈtɑːrdi/

TARDY

What does "TARDY" mean?

Late; delayed beyond the expected or proper time.

Meanings

  1. Arriving or happening later than expected or required. Three tardy arrivals earned him a detention.
  2. Slow in action, movement, or response. The committee's tardy reply cost them the contract. formal

Did you know?

  • The tardigrade - the microscopic 'water bear' that survives outer space - shares its root with 'tardy': its name means 'slow-stepper', from Latin 'tardus' plus 'gradi' (to step).

Word origin

From Latin 'tardus' meaning 'slow, sluggish', via Old French 'tardif'; the same root gives 'retard' and 'tardigrade' (the slow-stepping water bear).

Remember it

TARDY: you're so late you 'tarry' too long - both come from the same slow Latin root.
