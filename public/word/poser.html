POSER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈpoʊ.zɚ/

POSER

What does "POSER" mean?

A person who pretends to be something they are not; also a baffling question or problem.

Meanings

  1. A person who behaves affectedly to impress others or to seem part of a group they aren't really in. He bought all the gear but never surfed - a total poser. informal
  2. A difficult or puzzling question or problem. How to fund it without raising taxes is a real poser. informal
  3. A person who poses, as for a portrait or photograph. The artist needed a steady poser for the long sitting.

Word origin

From the verb 'pose' (to take up an attitude, or to puzzle), itself from Old French 'poser' (to place, set), from Late Latin 'pausare' (to halt, rest), influenced by Latin 'ponere' (to put).

Remember it

POSER = one who POSEs: either striking a fake pose to impress, or a question that makes YOU pose, frozen, with no answer.
