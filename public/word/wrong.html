WRONG

adjective · 1 syllable · /rɔŋ/

WRONG

What does "WRONG" mean?

Not correct, true, or in accordance with what is right or required.

Meanings

  1. Not correct or factually accurate. Your answer to the third question is wrong.
  2. Morally bad or unjust; not in accordance with what is ethical. He knew it was wrong to lie, but he did it anyway.
  3. Not functioning properly; amiss. Something is wrong with the engine.
  4. An unjust, dishonest, or immoral act. She set out to right the wrongs of her past.
  5. In a mistaken or incorrect manner. You spelled my name wrong. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Wrong' literally means 'twisted' - it shares a root with 'wring', so to be in the wrong is to be bent out of straight.

Word origin

From Old English 'wrang' via Old Norse 'rangr' (awry, crooked, unjust); the root sense is 'twisted', from the same family as 'wring' - what is wrong is bent out of true.

Remember it

WRONG and WRING both start WR and both mean twisted - a wrong is a truth wrung out of shape.

What it teaches
