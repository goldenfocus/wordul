FETAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'fiː.təl/

FETAL

What does "FETAL" mean?

Relating to a fetus, the unborn offspring developing in the womb.

Meanings

  1. Of or relating to a fetus or its stage of development. The scan monitored fetal heart rate. technical
  2. Resembling the curled posture of a fetus, as in 'fetal position'. He curled into the fetal position against the cold.

Did you know?

  • 'Fetal' shares a root with 'felix', the Latin word for 'happy' - which originally meant 'fertile' or 'fruitful', tying new life and good fortune to the same ancient idea.

Word origin

From Latin 'fetus' meaning 'offspring, a bringing forth', related to 'fecundus' (fruitful) and 'felix' (happy, fertile); English added the adjective suffix '-al'.

Remember it

FETAL = FETus + AL: just add the adjective ending to the thing it describes.
