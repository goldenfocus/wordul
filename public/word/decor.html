DECOR

noun · 2 syllables · /deɪˈkɔːr/

DECOR

What does "DECOR" mean?

The style and layout of furnishing and decoration of a room or building.

Meanings

  1. The overall decorative scheme and furnishings of an interior. The cafe's warm decor of wood and brass made it feel inviting.
  2. The scenery and stage furnishings of a theatrical production. The minimalist decor left the actors alone on a bare gray stage. technical

Did you know?

  • Decor, decent, and decorum are cousins - all spring from the Latin 'decus', so a room's decor is, at root, about what is fitting and graceful, not just what looks nice.

Word origin

From French 'décor', from 'décorer' 'to decorate', from Latin 'decorare' 'to adorn', from 'decus' 'ornament, grace, honor'; related to 'decent' and 'decorum'.

Remember it

DECOR is DECORate with the '-ate' stripped off - the look that's left once you stop decorating.
