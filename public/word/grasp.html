GRASP

verb · 1 syllable · /ɡræsp/

GRASP

What does "GRASP" mean?

To seize and hold something firmly with the hand, or to understand an idea fully.

Meanings

  1. To take and hold firmly, especially with the hand. She grasped the railing as the boat lurched.
  2. To comprehend or understand fully. It took him a while to grasp the rules of the game. figurative
  3. A firm hold or grip. The rope slipped from his grasp.
  4. The ability to comprehend or attain something. Victory was suddenly within their grasp. figurative

Word origin

From Middle English 'graspen', probably a variant of 'grapsen', related to 'grope'; from a Germanic base meaning to seize.
