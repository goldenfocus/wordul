SCORE

noun · 1 syllable · /skɔr/

SCORE

What does "SCORE" mean?

The number of points gained in a game, or the written form of a piece of music.

Meanings

  1. The record of points, goals, or runs in a competition. The score was tied with a minute left.
  2. A written or printed copy of a musical composition showing all the parts. The conductor turned the next page of the score.
  3. A group or set of twenty. A score of swans drifted on the lake. archaic
  4. To gain a point, goal, or mark. She scored twice in the final ten minutes.
  5. To cut a notch or line into a surface. Score the dough so it splits cleanly in the oven.

Did you know?

  • 'Score' meant twenty because shepherds tallied sheep by cutting a notch on a stick for every twentieth animal - the same notch-and-count origin gives us both the sports 'score' and the verb 'to score' a surface.

Word origin

From Old Norse 'skor' ('notch, tally, group of twenty'); tallies were once cut as notches on a stick, which is why 'score' came to mean both a count and a cut.

Remember it

A SCORE is a notch you CORE into a tally stick - and a score is twenty, as in 'four score and seven'.
