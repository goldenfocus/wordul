WHALE

noun · 1 syllable · /weɪl/

WHALE

What does "WHALE" mean?

A very large marine mammal of the order Cetacea that breathes air and bears live young.

Meanings

  1. A large, air-breathing marine mammal of the cetacean order. A blue whale surfaced beside the boat and exhaled a tower of spray.
  2. An impressively large or excellent example of something (in 'a whale of a'). We had a whale of a time at the festival. informal
  3. A person who gambles or spends enormous sums (in casinos or games). The casino comped the suite to keep the whale at the tables. informal
  4. To beat or thrash soundly. They whaled on the door until it gave way. informal

Did you know?

  • The blue whale is the largest animal that has ever lived - bigger than any dinosaur - stretching about 30 metres and weighing up to 150 tonnes.
  • A blue whale's heart weighs roughly 180 kilograms, and its slow beat can be detected from miles away through the water.

Word origin

From Old English 'hwæl', of Germanic origin, related to Old Norse 'hvalr'; the same root may connect to a wider Indo-European word for large fish.

Remember it
