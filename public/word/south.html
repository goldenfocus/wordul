SOUTH

noun · 1 syllable · /saʊθ/

SOUTH

What does "SOUTH" mean?

The direction toward the point of the horizon opposite to north; one of the four cardinal points.

Meanings

  1. The cardinal direction opposite north, to the right of someone facing the rising sun. The geese flew south as the first frost arrived.
  2. Situated in, facing, or coming from the south. A warm south wind melted the last of the snow.
  3. Toward or in the south. Drive south until you reach the coast.
  4. To deteriorate or decline (in the idiom 'go south'). The negotiations went south within an hour. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'suth', from Proto-Germanic 'sunthaz', probably linked to the root for 'sun' since the midday sun stands in the south for observers in the northern hemisphere.

Remember it

SOUTH contains OUT: when it gets cold, birds head south and out of winter.
