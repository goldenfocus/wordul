ACTOR

noun · 2 syllables · /'æk.tər/

ACTOR

What does "ACTOR" mean?

A person who performs a role in a play, film, or other dramatic work.

Meanings

  1. A performer who portrays a character on stage or screen. The actor learned the entire script in a week.
  2. A person or entity that takes part in or influences events, especially in politics or systems. Several state actors were accused of interfering in the election. formal

Word origin

From Latin 'actor' ('doer, agent'), from 'agere' (to do, drive, act) - originally meaning simply 'one who does', before it narrowed to the stage.

Remember it

An ACTOR is one who does an ACT - the '-or' is the doer ending, as in 'sailor' and 'creator'.
