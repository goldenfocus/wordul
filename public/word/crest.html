CREST

noun · 1 syllable · /krɛst/

CREST

What does "CREST" mean?

The highest point or top ridge of something, such as a hill, wave, or bird's head.

Meanings

  1. The top or highest part of a hill, wave, or ridge. We reached the crest of the ridge as the sun set.
  2. A tuft or growth of feathers, hair, or skin on an animal's head. The cockatoo raised its yellow crest when startled.
  3. A distinctive heraldic emblem or coat of arms. The family crest was carved above the doorway.
  4. To reach the top of something, or to rise to a peak. The river crested two feet above flood stage.

Word origin

From Latin 'crista', meaning 'tuft or plume', via Old French 'creste'; the original image is the plume on a helmet or a bird's head.

Remember it

A CREST is the BEST of a wave or hill - swap the B for CR and you're standing on the top.
