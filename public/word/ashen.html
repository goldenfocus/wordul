ASHEN

adjective · 2 syllables · /'æʃ.ən/

ASHEN

What does "ASHEN" mean?

Pale grey, as if drained of color, especially from shock or fear.

Meanings

  1. Of a pale, greyish color, like ashes; drained of color. She went ashen when she read the telegram.
  2. Resembling or consisting of ashes. An ashen film coated everything downwind of the volcano.
  3. Made of or relating to the wood of the ash tree. He gripped the ashen handle of the old axe. archaic

Word origin

From Old English 'æscen'; the color sense derives from 'ash' (the residue of fire), while the wood sense comes from 'ash', the tree - two unrelated Old English words that converged in spelling.

Remember it

ASHEN = ASH + EN; turning a noun like ASH into an adjective with '-EN', just as 'wood' becomes 'wooden'.
