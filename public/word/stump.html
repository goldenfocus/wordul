STUMP

noun · 1 syllable · /stʌmp/

STUMP

What does "STUMP" mean?

The bottom part of a tree left in the ground after the trunk is cut down.

Meanings

  1. The base of a tree trunk remaining after the tree is felled. We sat on an old stump to eat lunch.
  2. The remaining part of something cut, broken, or worn down, such as a limb or pencil. The pencil was worn down to a stump.
  3. To baffle or perplex someone so they cannot answer. That last question stumped the whole class.
  4. To travel an area making political speeches; to campaign. The candidate stumped across the state for a month.

Did you know?

  • When a politician goes 'on the stump', the phrase is literal history: frontier-era American campaigners climbed onto cut tree stumps to stand tall enough for a crowd to see and hear them.

Word origin

From Middle English 'stumpe', of Germanic origin, related to Middle Low German 'stump'. The political sense comes from frontier America, where speakers stood on tree stumps to address a crowd.
