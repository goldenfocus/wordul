UNLIT

adjective · 2 syllables · /ʌnˈlɪt/

UNLIT

What does "UNLIT" mean?

Not lit; without light or not yet set alight.

Meanings

  1. Not illuminated; dark. They crept down the unlit stairwell with a phone for a torch.
  2. Not set on fire or burning, as a candle or cigarette. An unlit cigarette dangled from the corner of his mouth.

Word origin

Formed in English from the prefix 'un-' ('not') plus 'lit', the past participle of 'light', which derives from Old English 'lihtan' ('to shine, kindle').

Remember it

UN-LIT: 'un' undoes 'lit' — the switch flipped off.

A little poem

The candle waits, unlit, all wick and wax-
a held breath the dark has not learned to relax.

couplet

Wordplay

  • My friend said his birthday cake was unlit. I told him that's no way to celebrate — the party's clearly not lit.

What it teaches

An unlit lamp is not a broken one; it is only waiting for its match.
