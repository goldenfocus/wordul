TIPSY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'tɪp.si/

TIPSY

What does "TIPSY" mean?

Slightly drunk; mildly affected by alcohol.

Meanings

  1. Mildly intoxicated; cheerful and a little unsteady from drink. Two glasses of wine and she was pleasantly tipsy. informal
  2. Tilted, unsteady, or likely to topple over. The old table stood on a tipsy leg and rocked with every touch.

Word origin

From 'tip' (to tilt or topple over) plus the suffix '-sy', suggesting the unsteady, leaning gait of someone who has had a little too much to drink.

Remember it

TIPSY = you might TIP over and say 'whoopsie' - TIP + (whoop)SY.
