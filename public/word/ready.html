READY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈred.i/

READY

What does "READY" mean?

Fully prepared and able to do something or be used immediately.

Meanings

  1. Prepared for action or use; in a fit state. Dinner is ready, so wash your hands.
  2. Willing or inclined to do something. She is always ready to help a stranger.
  3. Quick or prompt in occurring or being available. He has a ready answer for every question.
  4. To prepare or make fit for action. The crew readied the ship for departure.

Word origin

From Old English 'ræde' (prepared, prompt), of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'gereed' and German 'bereit'; originally tied to the idea of being equipped for a journey or a ride.

Remember it

READY = 'read' + 'y': once you've READ the instructions, you're readY to go.
