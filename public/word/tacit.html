TACIT

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈtæsɪt/

TACIT

What does "TACIT" mean?

Understood or implied without being stated; expressed silently.

Meanings

  1. Implied or understood without being directly expressed in words. Her nod was a tacit approval of the plan.
  2. Making no sound; silent. He gave only tacit, watchful attention. formal

Word origin

From Latin 'tacitus', the past participle of 'tacēre' meaning 'to be silent'; the same root gives 'taciturn' and 'reticent'.

Remember it

TACIT hides 'TACT' inside (almost) - the silent kind of knowing that good tact relies on.
