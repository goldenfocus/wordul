EVENT

noun · 2 syllables · /ɪˈvɛnt/

EVENT

What does "EVENT" mean?

A thing that happens, especially one of importance.

Meanings

  1. Something that takes place; an occurrence. The storm was the biggest event of the summer.
  2. A planned public or social occasion. Tickets for the charity event sold out in an hour.
  3. A single contest within a larger programme, as in sport. She qualified for three events at the championships.
  4. A point specified by a time and place in spacetime. In relativity, an event is a single location at a single moment. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'eventus' (outcome, occurrence), from 'evenire' meaning 'to come out, happen', built from 'e-' (out) plus 'venire' (to come).
