CIRCA

preposition · 2 syllables · /'sɜːr.kə/

CIRCA

What does "CIRCA" mean?

Approximately; used before a date or figure that is uncertain.

Meanings

  1. Used to show that a date or amount is approximate, often abbreviated 'c.' or 'ca.' The cathedral was built circa 1250, give or take a decade.

Did you know?

  • 'Circa', 'circle', and 'circus' are all the same Latin root for 'around': a circus was originally a circular arena, and 'circa' a date means a year you can only circle, not pin down.

Word origin

Directly from Latin 'circa', 'around, about', related to 'circus' and 'circle'.

Remember it

CIRCA is a CIRCle around a date - you can't pin it exactly, so you circle it.

A little poem

Circa some lost spring-

haiku

Wordplay

  • I was born circa Tuesday. The hospital was pretty sure, but didn't want to commit.

What it teaches

History often knows the century but not the day; honest dating circles what it cannot touch.
