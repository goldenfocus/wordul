CLONE

noun · 1 syllable · /kloʊn/

CLONE

What does "CLONE" mean?

An organism or cell genetically identical to another, or an exact copy of something.

Meanings

  1. A genetically identical organism or group of cells derived from a single ancestor. Dolly the sheep was the first mammal cloned from an adult cell. technical
  2. A product or design that closely imitates another, especially in technology. The startup released a cheap clone of the popular phone.
  3. To produce a genetically identical copy of an organism or cell. Researchers cloned the gene to study its function. technical
  4. To make an exact copy of something, including data or software. Clone the repository before making changes.

Did you know?

  • Dolly the sheep, born in 1996, was the first mammal grown from an adult cell - proof that a fully specialised cell could be reset to build a whole new animal.
  • 'Clone' started life in the garden, not the lab: it comes from the Greek 'klon', a twig or cutting, and around 1903 meant a plant grown from a single slip.

Word origin

Coined around 1903 from Greek 'klon' meaning a twig or slip used for grafting; originally a botanical term for plants propagated from a single cutting.

Remember it

CLONE is 'C' + 'LONE' - ironic, since a clone is never truly alone; there's always an original.
