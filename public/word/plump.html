PLUMP

adjective · 1 syllable · /plʌmp/

PLUMP

What does "PLUMP" mean?

Having a full, rounded shape; pleasantly fat.

Meanings

  1. Full and rounded in shape, in a way that is often appealing. She chose the plumpest tomatoes on the stall.
  2. To make or become full and rounded, often by shaking or filling out. He plumped the cushions before the guests arrived.
  3. To drop or sit down heavily and suddenly. She plumped down on the sofa, exhausted. informal
  4. To decide firmly in favour of one option ('plump for'). In the end we plumped for the seaside cottage. informal

Word origin

From Middle Dutch or Middle Low German 'plomp' meaning blunt, dull, or thick; the heavy-falling sense is partly imitative of the sound of a dropping weight.
