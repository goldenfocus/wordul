LAYER

noun · 2 syllables · /'leɪ.ɚ/

LAYER

What does "LAYER" mean?

A single thickness of material spread over or under another.

Meanings

  1. A sheet or coating of a substance lying over or beneath another. A thin layer of frost covered the windshield.
  2. To arrange or build up in layers. Layer the pasta, sauce, and cheese before baking.
  3. A level in a hierarchy, system, or stack of abstractions. The network's application layer handles the user-facing requests. technical
  4. A hen kept specifically for producing eggs. These hens are good layers, giving an egg almost daily.

Word origin

From Middle English 'leyer', meaning one who or that which lays, from the verb 'lay' plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Remember it

LAYER = one who LAYs + ER: a hen that lays eggs is literally a 'layer', same word.
