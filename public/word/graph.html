GRAPH

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡræf/

GRAPH

What does "GRAPH" mean?

A diagram showing the relationship between quantities, typically plotted on axes.

Meanings

  1. A visual chart of data, such as a line, bar, or pie graph, showing how values relate. The graph showed sales climbing every quarter.
  2. In mathematics, a set of points (vertices) connected by edges, used to model networks. They modeled the subway as a graph of stations and links. technical
  3. To plot or represent something as a graph. Graph the function and find where it crosses zero.

Did you know?

  • The mathematical field of graph theory was born in 1736 when Leonhard Euler proved you cannot walk through Konigsberg crossing each of its seven bridges exactly once - the first theorem about networks.

Word origin

Shortened from 'graphic formula', from Greek 'graphein', meaning 'to write or draw' - the same root behind 'graphite', 'photograph', and 'paragraph'.

Remember it

GRAPH shares 'graph-' (to draw) with photograph and autograph - a graph is data, drawn.
