SPENT

adjective · 1 syllable · /spɛnt/

SPENT

What does "SPENT" mean?

Used up, exhausted, or having no energy or value left.

Meanings

  1. Completely used up or depleted of energy or resources. After the double shift she was utterly spent.
  2. Of fuel, ammunition, or material: already discharged or no longer usable. A spent cartridge rolled across the range floor. technical
  3. Past tense and past participle of 'spend'. They spent the inheritance in a single summer.

Word origin

Past participle of 'spend', from Old English 'spendan' via Latin 'expendere' (to pay or weigh out).

Remember it

SPENT is SPEND with the 'd' worn down to a 't' - the very thing that happens when something is used up.
