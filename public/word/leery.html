LEERY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'lɪr.i/

LEERY

What does "LEERY" mean?

Cautious or wary because of suspicion or distrust.

Meanings

  1. Suspicious and on guard; reluctant to trust. After the first scam, she was leery of online deals.

Word origin

Probably from the dialectal verb 'leer' meaning to look sidelong or askance, from Old English 'hleor' (cheek, face); to be leery is to eye something warily from the side.

Remember it

LEERY = give it the side-eye, a 'leer'; the double-E are the two narrowed eyes watching warily.
