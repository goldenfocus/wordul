SPARE

adjective · 1 syllable · /speər/

SPARE

What does "SPARE" mean?

Kept in reserve or available beyond what is currently needed.

Meanings

  1. Additional to what is required; held in reserve. Keep a spare key with a trusted neighbour.
  2. Thin or lean; sparse and unadorned. His prose was spare, with not a wasted word.
  3. To refrain from harming, or to give from one's resources. Could you spare a few minutes to help?
  4. A duplicate item kept for replacement, especially a spare tyre. The spare was flat too, so we had to walk.
  5. In bowling, knocking down all ten pins with two rolls of one frame. She picked up the spare on the last frame to tie the game. technical

Did you know?

  • In bowling a spare is worth more than the ten pins you knocked down: it adds your next single roll on top, so clearing the frame in two tries can quietly out-score sloppy strikes.

Word origin

From Old English 'sparian' (to refrain, to leave unharmed) and the related adjective 'spaer' (frugal, sparing), from a Proto-Germanic root meaning to be thrifty.

Remember it

SPARE is PARE with an S: to pare is to trim down, and spare things are the trimmed-down extras you set aside.
