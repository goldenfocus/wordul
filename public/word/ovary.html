OVARY

noun · 3 syllables · /ˈoʊ.və.ri/

OVARY

What does "OVARY" mean?

The reproductive organ that produces eggs in female animals.

Meanings

  1. In female animals, the organ that produces egg cells and reproductive hormones. Each ovary releases an egg in roughly monthly alternation.
  2. In a flowering plant, the enlarged base of the pistil that contains the ovules and becomes the fruit. After pollination, the flower's ovary swells into a ripe tomato. technical

Did you know?

  • Every fleshy fruit you eat - tomato, peach, grape - is botanically a swollen ovary, the part of the flower that ripens around its seeds after pollination.

Word origin

From Latin 'ovarium', from 'ovum' meaning 'egg'; coined in the modern scientific sense to name the egg-producing organ.

Remember it

OVARY begins with 'OV-' like 'ovum' and 'oval' - all from the Latin for egg.
