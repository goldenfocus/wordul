RETRO

adjective · 2 syllables · /'rɛt.roʊ/

RETRO

What does "RETRO" mean?

Imitating or evoking a style, fashion, or design from the recent past.

Meanings

  1. Deliberately recalling the look or feel of an earlier era. The diner had a retro look, all chrome and red vinyl booths.
  2. A retro style, item, or trend. Her whole wardrobe is retro from the seventies. informal
  3. A retrospective meeting reviewing past work, especially in software teams. We logged the bugs to raise at Friday's retro. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'retro', meaning 'backward' or 'behind'; popularized as a standalone English word in the 20th century, partly via French 'rétro'.

Remember it

RETRO points back: 'retro-' always means backward, like a retrograde planet reversing course.
