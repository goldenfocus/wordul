UPPER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈʌp.ər/

UPPER

What does "UPPER" mean?

Situated above another part; higher in position, rank, or status.

Meanings

  1. Higher in physical position than something else. She reached for a mug on the upper shelf.
  2. Higher in rank, class, or status. The deal pushed the firm into the upper tier of the market.
  3. The part of a shoe above the sole. The boot's leather upper had cracked at the toe. technical
  4. A stimulant drug, especially an amphetamine. He swore off uppers after the late shifts wrecked his sleep. informal

Word origin

From Middle English 'upper', the comparative of 'up', from Old English 'upp' ('upward'); a Germanic root shared with Dutch 'op' and German 'auf'.

Remember it

UPPER has UP at the start — literally the higher one.
