LOGIN

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈlɒɡ.ɪn/

LOGIN

What does "LOGIN" mean?

The act or credentials for gaining access to a computer system or online account.

Meanings

  1. The process or a set of credentials (username and password) used to access a system. I forgot my login for the banking site.
  2. A username or account identifier. Enter your login in the first field.

Did you know?

  • When you 'log in', you echo an age of sail: the 'log' was a wooden float thrown off a ship to gauge its speed, and the readings were written in the 'logbook'.

Word origin

A 20th-century compound from the phrasal verb 'log in', where 'log' derives from the ship's logbook in which records were kept; the noun 'login' (one word) is the computing-era spelling.

Remember it

LOGIN = LOG + IN: you write your name in the log to get in.
