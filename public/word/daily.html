DAILY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'deɪ.li/

DAILY

What does "DAILY" mean?

Happening, done, or produced every day.

Meanings

  1. Occurring or recurring every day. Her daily walk took her past the same row of plane trees.
  2. Every day; once a day. Take the medicine twice daily.
  3. A newspaper published every weekday. The story made the front page of every national daily.

Word origin

From Old English 'dæglic', formed from 'dæg' (day) plus the suffix '-lic' meaning 'like' or 'having the form of'.

Remember it

DAILY = DAY with the Y moved to the end and an I slipped in; the 'day' is still hiding right at the front.
