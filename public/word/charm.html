CHARM

noun · 1 syllable · /tʃɑːrm/

CHARM

What does "CHARM" mean?

The power to delight or attract; also a small object or spell believed to bring luck.

Meanings

  1. The quality of delighting, attracting, or fascinating others. He won the crowd over with sheer charm.
  2. A small ornament worn on a bracelet or chain. She added a tiny silver charm for every country she visited.
  3. An object, word, or act believed to have magic power; a spell. The sailor carried a charm against drowning.
  4. To delight, fascinate, or win over. The little dog charmed everyone in the waiting room.
  5. In particle physics, one of the six 'flavors' of quark. The charm quark was discovered in 1974. technical

Did you know?

  • Physicists named a building block of matter after personal magnetism: the 'charm quark' was confirmed in 1974, when two rival teams discovered the same particle, now called J/psi, in the very same week.
  • A charm was originally a song: the word descends from Latin 'carmen' ('song, verse'), because spells were meant to be sung or chanted aloud.

Word origin

From Old French 'charme' ('magic spell, incantation'), from Latin 'carmen' ('song, verse, incantation'); a charm was originally something chanted to work magic.

Remember it

A CHARM is a 'carmen', a song - the same Latin root behind any tune meant to bewitch you.
