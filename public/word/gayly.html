GAYLY

adverb · 2 syllables · /'ɡeɪ.li/

GAYLY

What does "GAYLY" mean?

In a merry, lively, or brightly colored manner; an older spelling of 'gaily'.

Meanings

  1. Cheerfully, merrily, or with bright festivity. The flags fluttered gayly above the fairground. archaic

Word origin

From 'gay' plus the adverbial '-ly', from Old French 'gai' (joyful); 'gaily' is now the standard spelling, with 'gayly' an older variant.

Remember it

GAYLY = GAY + LY, an adverb wearing its old coat; modern English swaps the Y for an I to make 'gaily'.
