INGOT

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɪŋ.ɡət/

INGOT

What does "INGOT" mean?

A block of metal cast into a convenient shape for storage or transport.

Meanings

  1. A mass of cast metal, especially gold, silver, or steel, shaped as a bar or block. The vault held row upon row of gold ingots.

Word origin

Probably from Middle English, perhaps from 'in' plus Old English 'goten' (poured, cast - past participle of 'geotan', to pour); an ingot is literally metal 'poured in' to a mold.

Remember it

An INGOT is metal poured IN and let to set - picture liquid gold poured 'in' the mold to become a solid GOT block.
