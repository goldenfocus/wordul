ANGRY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈæŋ.ɡri/

ANGRY

What does "ANGRY" mean?

Feeling or showing strong displeasure or hostility.

Meanings

  1. Feeling or showing anger. He fired off an angry email and regretted it by lunch.
  2. Inflamed and painful, of a wound or sore. The cut had turned an angry red around the edges.
  3. Suggesting turbulence or menace, of weather or the sea. Angry clouds piled up over the harbour before the storm. figurative

Word origin

From the noun 'anger' plus the adjective suffix '-y'; 'anger' itself traces to Old Norse 'angr', grief or distress.
