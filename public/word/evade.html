EVADE

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪˈveɪd/

EVADE

What does "EVADE" mean?

To escape or avoid something by cleverness, trickery, or maneuvering.

Meanings

  1. To get away from or avoid a pursuer, threat, or duty by skill or cunning. The fugitive evaded the police for months.
  2. To avoid answering or dealing with something directly. He evaded every question about the budget.
  3. To escape the grasp or understanding of someone. The melody evaded her, just out of memory's reach. figurative

Word origin

From Latin 'evadere' (to go out, escape), from 'e-' (out) plus 'vadere' (to go, to walk), the same 'vadere' root behind 'invade' and 'wade'.

Remember it

EVADE and INVADE share the root 'vadere' (to go) - one goes out (e-) to escape, the other goes in (in-) to attack.
