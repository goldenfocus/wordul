MATEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈmeɪti/

MATEY

What does "MATEY" mean?

Friendly and familiar in manner, especially in British informal speech.

Meanings

  1. Sociable, companionable, and warmly familiar with people. The new boss was instantly matey with everyone on the floor. informal
  2. A friendly term of address for a man, chiefly British and nautical. All right, matey, where to? informal

Word origin

From 'mate' (companion), from Middle Low German 'mate' (one who eats at the same table), plus the familiar suffix '-y'.

Remember it

MATEY = MATE + Y: a mate, but turned up a notch warmer - the Y is the wink.

A little poem

He calls the whole pub 'matey' by the door,
and means it less the more he says it more.
