SLUNG

What does "SLUNG" mean?

The past tense and past participle of 'sling'; threw, hurled, or suspended with force.

Meanings

  1. Past tense of 'sling': threw or flung, often carelessly. He slung his coat over the chair and left.
  2. Past participle of 'sling', used in perfect tenses or as a modifier; hung loosely. A rifle was slung across his shoulder.

Word origin

Past tense of 'sling', from Middle English 'slynge', of Scandinavian origin akin to Old Norse 'slyngva' (to fling).

Remember it

SING becomes SLUNG once the S+L grab it and throw - the past tense flung an extra L in front.
