SAUCY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'sɔː.si/

SAUCY

What does "SAUCY" mean?

Boldly impudent or cheeky, often in a lively, playful, or flirtatious way.

Meanings

  1. Impudent, cheeky, or disrespectful in a spirited manner. The parrot gave a saucy whistle every time the boss walked past. informal
  2. Suggestive or mildly risque, especially in a humorous way. The seaside postcards were famous for their saucy jokes. informal

Word origin

From 'sauce' plus '-y'; the sense shifted from 'tasting of sauce' to 'sharp, piquant in manner', i.e. someone who 'gives you sauce' (cheek).

Remember it

SAUCY is SAUCE with a Y - the person who serves you a little extra lip on the side.
