GLINT

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡlɪnt/

GLINT

What does "GLINT" mean?

A small, quick flash of reflected light.

Meanings

  1. A brief, bright sparkle or flash of light off a surface. She caught the glint of a coin in the gutter.
  2. A sudden brief sign of an emotion or quality in someone's expression. A glint of amusement crossed his face. figurative
  3. To give off small, quick flashes of light. The knife glinted as he turned it in the lamplight.

Word origin

From Middle English 'glenten' meaning 'to move quickly, dart, glance', of Scandinavian origin; the modern spelling and 'flash of light' sense were popularized by Robert Burns in the 18th century.

Remember it

GLINT is a 'glance' of 'tint' - a quick darting catch of color and light.
