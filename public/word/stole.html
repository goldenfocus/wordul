STOLE

What does "STOLE" mean?

Past tense of 'steal': took something without permission or right.

Meanings

  1. Past tense of 'steal'; took another's property dishonestly. Someone stole her bicycle from the rack.
  2. Past tense of 'steal'; moved quietly and secretly. He stole out of the room while everyone slept.
  3. A long scarf or shawl of fur or fabric worn around the shoulders. She wore a velvet stole over her gown.
  4. A long ecclesiastical vestment worn around the neck by clergy. The priest kissed his stole before placing it on. technical

Word origin

The verb is the past tense of 'steal', from Old English 'stelan'; the garment 'stole' is unrelated, from Greek 'stole' (a robe or equipment) via Latin.

Remember it

STOLE is two words wearing one coat: the past of 'steal', and the shawl - one took, one is worn.
