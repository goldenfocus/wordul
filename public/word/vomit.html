VOMIT

verb · 2 syllables · /'vɒm.ɪt/

VOMIT

What does "VOMIT" mean?

To eject the contents of the stomach forcefully through the mouth.

Meanings

  1. To bring up the contents of the stomach through the mouth. The rough crossing made several passengers vomit.
  2. The matter ejected from the stomach. He cleaned the vomit off the deck.
  3. To pour out or send forth in large amounts. The factory chimneys vomited smoke into the sky. figurative

Did you know?

  • Vomiting is mostly muscle, not stomach: the brainstem triggers the diaphragm and abdominal wall to squeeze the stomach from outside, while the stomach itself stays relatively relaxed.

Word origin

From Latin 'vomitare' (to vomit often), frequentative of 'vomere' (to discharge, spew); related to Greek 'emein' (to vomit), source of 'emetic'.
