noun · 1 syllable · /steɪdʒ/

STAGE

What does "STAGE" mean?

A raised platform on which performers act, or a distinct step in a process.

Meanings

  1. A raised floor or platform for performances or speeches. She stepped onto the stage to a wall of applause.
  2. A point, period, or step in a process or development. The disease was caught at an early stage.
  3. To organize and present a performance or event. The students staged a protest outside the dean's office.
  4. A section of a rocket that fires and then separates. The first stage fell away two minutes after liftoff. technical

Did you know?

  • The theatrical and the developmental senses share one root: Latin 'stare', to stand, gave Old French 'estage', a standing-place, which split into both 'a platform to stand on' and 'a point one stands at in a process'.

Word origin

From Old French 'estage', meaning a story of a building or a standing place, from Vulgar Latin 'staticum', a place for standing, from Latin 'stare' (to stand).
