WARTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'wɔːr.ti/

WARTY

What does "WARTY" mean?

Covered with or resembling warts; having a rough, bumpy surface.

Meanings

  1. Having or covered with warts or wart-like growths. The toad's warty skin made it hard to spot among the stones.
  2. Having a rough, lumpy, or imperfection-covered surface or character. The plan had a few warty details still to be smoothed out. figurative

Word origin

From 'wart' (Old English 'wearte', a small hard skin growth) plus the adjective suffix '-y'; the Germanic root is shared with German 'Warze'.

Remember it

WARTY is WART plus Y - the Y is the last little bump added on, just as warts pile up one more.

A little poem

Wordplay

What it teaches
