NAVEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'neɪ.vəl/

NAVEL

What does "NAVEL" mean?

The small depression in the middle of the belly where the umbilical cord was attached.

Meanings

  1. The hollow or knot at the center of the abdomen left by the umbilical cord; the belly button. The mosquito bite was right beside his navel.
  2. A central or middle point of a thing. The ancient Greeks called Delphi the navel of the world. figurative
  3. A type of seedless orange with a small secondary fruit at the blossom end resembling a navel. She peeled a navel orange and split it into segments.

Did you know?

  • Every navel orange in the world is a clone: the variety is seedless, so it can only be propagated by grafting from a single mutant tree discovered in Brazil around 1820.

Word origin

From Old English 'nafela', from a Proto-Germanic root related to 'nave', the hub of a wheel - the central point.

Remember it

NAVEL has an 'E' for the body, unlike NAVAL with an 'A' for the armada. E for your bellybutton in the middle.
