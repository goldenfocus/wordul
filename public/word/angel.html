ANGEL

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈeɪndʒəl/

ANGEL

What does "ANGEL" mean?

A spiritual being believed to act as a messenger or servant of God.

Meanings

  1. A heavenly messenger or attendant of God in Jewish, Christian, and Islamic belief, often depicted with wings. In the painting an angel descends with a single lily.
  2. A person of exceptional kindness, beauty, or virtue. You're an angel for staying up to help me finish. informal
  3. A private investor who funds a startup or theatrical production in its early stage. An angel put up the seed money before any bank would listen.

Did you know?

  • An 'angel' is, at root, just a 'messenger': the Greek 'angelos' meant any messenger, and the same root sits inside 'evangelist' - literally a bringer of good (eu-) news (angel).

Word origin

From Greek 'angelos' (messenger), used in the Greek Bible to translate Hebrew 'mal'ak' (messenger); it passed through Latin 'angelus' and Old English 'engel' to modern 'angel'.

Remember it

ANGEL = ANGE + L, and it hides ANGLE rearranged - an angel folds its wings at a holy angle.
