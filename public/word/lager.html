noun · 2 syllables · /ˈlɑː.ɡər/

LAGER

What does "LAGER" mean?

A pale, crisp beer made by slow fermentation and cold storage with bottom-fermenting yeast.

Meanings

  1. A type of beer fermented and conditioned at low temperatures, typically light, clear, and crisp. He ordered a cold lager to go with the spicy food.

Did you know?

  • 'Lager' literally means 'storehouse' in German: the beer is named not for a flavor but for the months it spent stored cold in cellars to mature.
  • What separates a lager from an ale is mostly the yeast - lagers ferment cool with bottom-fermenting yeast, ales ferment warm with top-fermenting yeast.

Word origin

From German 'Lagerbier', meaning 'beer for storing', from 'Lager' (storehouse, store) plus 'Bier' (beer), reflecting the long cold storage the style requires.

Remember it

LAGER lags behind: the beer that must lag in cold storage before it is ready to drink.
