UNDID

What does "UNDID" mean?

The past tense of 'undo': reversed, untied, or cancelled something done.

Meanings

  1. Past tense of 'undo' - unfastened or untied something. She undid the top button and let out a breath.
  2. Past tense of 'undo' - reversed or cancelled the effect of an action. One careless email undid months of careful diplomacy.
  3. Past tense of 'undo' - ruined or destroyed (someone or their fortunes). Pride undid him long before his rivals could. formal

Word origin

Past tense of 'undo', from Old English 'undon' (to open, unfasten), combining the reversive prefix 'un-' with 'don' (to do); 'did' is the inherited strong past of 'do'.

Remember it

UNDID = UN + DID: the prefix that reverses, glued to the past of 'do' - it literally un-did what was done.
