RISER

noun · 2 syllables · /'raɪ.zər/

RISER

What does "RISER" mean?

The vertical part of a stair step, or a person who rises, especially from bed.

Meanings

  1. The upright face of a stair step, between one tread and the next. Each riser was exactly seven inches tall. technical
  2. A person considered in terms of how early or late they get up. She's an early riser, up before the sun.
  3. A raised platform, often a tiered set of them, for a choir or band. The choir climbed onto the risers for the final number.
  4. A vertical pipe or duct carrying water, gas, or wiring up through a building. The plumber traced the leak to the main riser. technical

Word origin

From 'rise' plus the agent suffix '-er'; the stair sense names the part that 'rises' vertically, while the personal sense names one who rises.

Remember it

On a staircase, the RISER is the part that rises (goes up); the tread is the part you tread on.
