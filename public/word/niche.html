noun · 1 syllable · /niːʃ/

NICHE

What does "NICHE" mean?

A specialized segment, role, or position particularly well suited to a person or thing.

Meanings

  1. A comfortable or suitable position in life or employment. After years of drifting, she finally found her niche in restoration carpentry.
  2. A specialized but profitable corner of a market. The brand thrived by serving a niche that bigger companies ignored.
  3. A shallow recess in a wall, often holding a statue or vase. A small saint stood in the niche above the chapel door.
  4. The role and position a species occupies within its ecosystem. Two species rarely share an identical ecological niche for long. technical
  5. Appealing to a narrow, specialized audience. It's a niche hobby, but its fans are fiercely devoted.

Did you know?

  • The word's ecological sense rests on a hard rule: Georgy Gause's 1934 work gave us the competitive exclusion principle, which holds that two species filling the identical niche cannot stably coexist - one always edges the other out.

Word origin

From French 'niche' (a recess for a statue), from Old French 'nichier' (to nest), ultimately from Latin 'nidus' meaning nest.

Remember it

A niche is a NICH-e in the wall - a little nest carved out just for you, echoing its Latin root 'nidus', nest.
