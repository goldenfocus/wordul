VINYL

noun · 2 syllables · /'vaɪ.nəl/

VINYL

What does "VINYL" mean?

A tough synthetic plastic, and the phonograph records pressed from it.

Meanings

  1. A durable synthetic plastic (polyvinyl chloride) used for flooring, upholstery, records, and more. The kitchen floor was easy-clean vinyl.
  2. Phonograph records as a format, especially as distinct from digital music. He still buys his favorite albums on vinyl. informal
  3. Made of or relating to vinyl plastic. She wore a shiny vinyl jacket.
  4. In chemistry, the unsaturated group CH2=CH- derived from ethylene. Vinyl groups react readily in polymerization. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'vinyl' traces to Latin 'vinum', wine - chemists named the chemical group after its kinship with the ethyl alcohol that makes wine alcoholic.
  • In 2022, vinyl records earned more U.S. revenue than CDs for the first time since 1987, completing one of the strangest format comebacks in music history.

Word origin

Coined in chemistry from Latin 'vinum' (wine) plus '-yl', because the group was seen as related to ethyl alcohol (the alcohol in wine).

Remember it

VINYL holds your favorite 'VINYL' grooves - and hides 'VINY', like grapevines, hinting at its wine-rooted name.
