DWELL

verb · 1 syllable · /dwɛl/

DWELL

What does "DWELL" mean?

To live in or occupy a place as one's home.

Meanings

  1. To reside or have one's home in a particular place. For decades they dwelt in a stone cottage at the edge of the moor. formal
  2. To linger over, think, or speak about something at length. Try not to dwell on the mistake; finish the report instead.

Word origin

From Old English 'dwellan' meaning to lead astray, hinder, or delay; the sense shifted through Middle English 'to linger' to the modern 'to reside'.

Remember it

DWELL hides WELL: you dwell where you feel well.
