RAPID

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈræp.ɪd/

RAPID

What does "RAPID" mean?

Happening or moving with great speed; very fast.

Meanings

  1. Marked by quickness of movement or change. The patient made a rapid recovery after surgery.
  2. A fast, turbulent stretch of a river where the water flows over rocks (usually plural). Their raft bounced wildly through the rapids of the canyon.

Word origin

From Latin 'rapidus' (seizing, swift), from 'rapere' (to seize or snatch); the river sense reflects water that 'snatches' a boat away.

Remember it

RAPID shares its 'rap' root with 'rapture' and 'raptor' - all from Latin 'rapere', to seize swiftly.
