CRESS

What does "CRESS" mean?

A small, fast-growing leafy plant with a peppery taste, eaten in salads and garnishes.

Meanings

  1. Any of several pungent-leaved plants of the cabbage family, such as watercress or garden cress, used in salads. She topped the egg sandwich with a layer of fresh cress.

Word origin

From Old English 'cresse' or 'cærse', from a Germanic root linked to a verb meaning 'to eat' or 'to nibble'; related to German 'Kresse'.

Remember it

CRESS rhymes with 'press' - press the peppery leaves and the green is everywhere, double-S for the spray of sprouts.
