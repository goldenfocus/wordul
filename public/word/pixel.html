PIXEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'pɪk.səl/

PIXEL

What does "PIXEL" mean?

The smallest controllable element of a digital image on a screen.

Meanings

  1. A single point in a raster image, the smallest addressable element of a display. Zoom in far enough and the photo dissolves into a grid of coloured pixels.
  2. A tiny invisible tracking image embedded in an email or webpage. Marketers drop a tracking pixel in the email to know when you open it. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'pixel' was coined in 1965 by JPL engineer Frederic C. Billingsley to describe the picture elements of images beamed back from spacecraft - it was born in spaceflight, not entertainment.

Word origin

A 1965 blend of 'pix' (slang for pictures) and 'el' from 'element', coined by engineers working on early image processing.

Remember it

PIXEL = PIX (pictures) + EL (element): the smallest element of a picture.
