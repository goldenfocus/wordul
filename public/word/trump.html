TRUMP

noun · 1 syllable · /trʌmp/

TRUMP

What does "TRUMP" mean?

In card games, a suit ranked above all others for the duration of a hand.

Meanings

  1. A playing card of the suit chosen to outrank all other suits. She slapped down a trump and took the trick.
  2. To beat a card by playing a trump; figuratively, to outdo or override. His clever budget trumped every objection.
  3. A decisive advantage held in reserve, as in 'trump card'. The witness was the prosecutor's trump card. figurative
  4. The blast of a trumpet, especially in the phrase 'the last trump'. They sang of the dead rising at the last trump. archaic

Did you know?

  • The 'trump' that beats every other card is a worn-down spelling of 'triumph' - and Triumph was itself the name of a card game played in Tudor England.

Word origin

The card sense is a corruption of 'triumph', from Latin 'triumphus' (a victory procession); the trumpet sense comes separately from Old French 'trompe' (a horn).

Remember it

TRUMP is TRiUMPh with the 'iuh' squeezed out - the winning suit keeps the win baked in.
