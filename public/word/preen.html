PREEN

verb · 1 syllable · /priːn/

PREEN

What does "PREEN" mean?

To clean and arrange one's feathers with the beak, or to groom oneself with obvious vanity.

Meanings

  1. Of a bird, to clean, smooth, and oil its feathers using the beak. The heron stood on one leg, preening its long wing feathers.
  2. To tidy or admire one's own appearance with self-satisfaction. He preened in front of the mirror before the interview.
  3. To take obvious pride in oneself or one's achievements. She preened at the praise from her professor. figurative

Did you know?

  • When a bird preens, it is often spreading oil from the uropygial gland at the base of its tail - a built-in waterproofing kit it works through its feathers with its beak.

Word origin

From Middle English 'preinen', probably a blend of 'proinen' (to preen, from Old French 'proignier', to trim) and 'prenen' (to pierce, from Old English 'preon', a pin), reflecting the beak's needle-like action.
