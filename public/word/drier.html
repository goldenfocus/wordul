DRIER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'draɪ.ər/

DRIER

What does "DRIER" mean?

Comparative form of 'dry'; more dry.

Meanings

  1. More dry; having less moisture than something else. The desert air is far drier than the coast.
  2. A machine or substance that removes moisture (variant spelling of 'dryer'). The paint needs a chemical drier to set faster. technical

Word origin

Comparative of 'dry', from Old English 'dryge'; the 'y' shifts to 'i' before the '-er' comparative ending.

Remember it

DRIER vs DRYER: 'drier' (with an i) means MORE dry; the machine is usually a 'dryer' (with a y).

A little poem

Wordplay

What it teaches

Comparatives are quiet bullies: there is always a drier desert, a colder year, a harder day.
