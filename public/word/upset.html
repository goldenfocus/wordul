UPSET

verb · 2 syllables · /ʌpˈsɛt/

UPSET

What does "UPSET" mean?

To make someone unhappy or distressed; to knock over or disrupt; or an unexpected result.

Meanings

  1. To cause someone to feel troubled, unhappy, or distressed. The bad news upset her for the rest of the day.
  2. To knock or tip something over. The cat upset the vase as it leapt off the table.
  3. To disturb the normal working or order of something. The strike upset the airline's entire schedule.
  4. An unexpected result in which a favorite is defeated. The unranked team pulled off a stunning upset in the final.
  5. Distressed, unhappy, or (of a stomach) feeling sick. He went home early with an upset stomach.

Did you know?

  • A folk story claims the sporting term 'upset' was born when a horse literally named Upset beat the unbeaten Man o' War in 1919 — but Merriam-Webster notes the word was already used for surprise defeats years earlier, making it a tidy coincidence rather than the true origin.

Word origin

From Middle English 'upsetten' ('to set up'), later shifting to mean 'overturn'; formed from 'up' plus 'set'. The sense 'distressed' arose in the 19th century.
