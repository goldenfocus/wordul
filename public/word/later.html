LATER

adverb · 2 syllables · /'leɪ.tɚ/

LATER

What does "LATER" mean?

At a time after the present or after a stated point; afterward.

Meanings

  1. At some unspecified time in the future; afterward. I'll call you later when I'm off work.
  2. Coming after another in time; more recent or further along. Her later novels are darker than the early ones.
  3. A casual goodbye, short for 'see you later'. Thanks for the ride - later! informal

Word origin

The comparative form of 'late', from Old English 'lætra', the comparative of 'læt' meaning slow or tardy.

Remember it

LATER is LATE with an -R: the comparative, meaning even further past the time.

A little poem

We say it like a door we mean to use:
'Later.' The hour nods and walks away,
and later, somehow, never is today.
