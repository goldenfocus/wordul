adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈɪn.ər/

INNER

What does "INNER" mean?

Located inside or further in; relating to the mind or spirit.

Meanings

  1. Situated inside or closer to the center. The inner courtyard was hidden from the street.
  2. Relating to one's mind, feelings, or spirit. She finally found a kind of inner peace.
  3. Private, exclusive, or close to a center of power. Only the inner circle knew the real plan.

Did you know?

  • 'Inner' is a fossil comparative: like 'former' or 'utter', it was built as a comparative of Old English 'inne' (within), though we no longer feel it as 'more in'.

Word origin

From Old English 'innera', a comparative form of 'inne' (within); a relative of 'in', 'inn', and the German 'inner'.

Remember it

INNER has the word IN twice over in spirit - it points to what is most 'in'.
