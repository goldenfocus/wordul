TESTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'tes.ti/

TESTY

What does "TESTY" mean?

Easily irritated; impatient and quick to show annoyance.

Meanings

  1. Irritable, touchy, and short-tempered. He gets testy when he hasn't eaten.

Did you know?

  • TESTY traces back to Latin 'testa', meaning 'clay pot.' Roman slang used 'pot' for 'head', which became French 'tête'; a testy person is literally 'headstrong.'

Word origin

From Anglo-Norman 'testif' (headstrong), from Old French 'teste' (head), from Latin 'testa' (an earthen pot, later slang for 'head'); a testy person is literally 'headstrong'.

Remember it

TESTY = a TEST + Y: nobody is at their most patient mid-test, and the questions make everyone a little testy.
