QUEST

noun · 1 syllable · /kwɛst/

QUEST

What does "QUEST" mean?

A long, determined search for or pursuit of something difficult to attain.

Meanings

  1. A long or arduous search for something of value or meaning. His quest for the truth lasted a decade.
  2. A task or mission undertaken by a character, especially in a game or romance. The knight set out on a quest to recover the stolen relic.
  3. To search or seek determinedly. Scholars still quest after the meaning of the lost script. formal

Did you know?

  • A 'quest' and a 'question' are the same impulse in different clothes: both grow from Latin 'quaerere', to seek - one searches the world for a thing, the other searches a mind for an answer.

Word origin

From Old French 'queste' meaning 'search, hunt', from Latin 'quaesita', past participle of 'quaerere' meaning 'to seek, ask' - the same root as 'question' and 'query'.

Remember it

QUEST = a QUEry put into your feet - a question you go out and walk toward.
