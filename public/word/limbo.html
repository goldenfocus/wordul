LIMBO

noun · 2 syllables · /'lɪm.boʊ/

LIMBO

What does "LIMBO" mean?

A state of uncertain waiting, neither one thing nor another.

Meanings

  1. An uncertain or in-between state where one is stuck waiting for a decision or resolution. Her visa application left her in limbo for months.
  2. In some Christian theology, a region on the border of hell for souls barred from heaven through no fault of their own. Medieval theologians placed unbaptized infants in limbo. technical
  3. A West Indian dance in which dancers bend backward to pass under a low bar that is steadily lowered. At the party everyone tried the limbo until the stick was barely a foot off the floor.

Did you know?

  • The two meanings of 'limbo' come from different sources: the theological 'edge of hell' is from Latin 'limbus' (border), while the Caribbean dance, which arose in Trinidad, is tied to 'limber' and the idea of getting low.

Word origin

From Medieval Latin 'in limbo', the ablative of 'limbus', meaning 'edge' or 'border'; the Caribbean dance name is separately influenced by 'limber'.
