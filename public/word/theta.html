What does "THETA" mean?

The eighth letter of the Greek alphabet (Θ, θ), often used to denote an angle.

Meanings

  1. The eighth letter of the Greek alphabet, written Θ (uppercase) or θ (lowercase). She labeled the unknown angle theta on the diagram.
  2. In mathematics and physics, a symbol commonly standing for an angle or an angular coordinate. Convert the polar point to Cartesian using r and theta. technical
  3. In neuroscience, a band of brain waves of roughly 4 to 8 Hz linked to drowsiness and memory. Theta rhythms surged in the hippocampus during the maze task. technical
  4. In options trading, the rate at which an option loses value as time passes. With expiry near, theta was eating into the premium daily. technical

Did you know?

  • The 'theta' your brain hums at while drifting off is a measurable 4 to 8 Hz rhythm, the same Greek letter physicists use for the angle of a falling pendulum.

Word origin

From Greek 'thêta', the name of the letter, ultimately borrowed from the Phoenician letter 'teth' meaning roughly 'wheel'.

Remember it

THETA sounds like 'the-ta', and its symbol θ is a circle with a line through it - a tiny wheel, which is exactly what its Phoenician ancestor meant.
