EVOKE

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪˈvoʊk/

EVOKE

What does "EVOKE" mean?

To bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind.

Meanings

  1. To call up or produce a memory, emotion, or response. The smell of pine evokes my grandmother's house.
  2. To summon or call forth a spirit or presence. The ritual was meant to evoke the dead. formal
  3. To elicit a reaction or specified response. Her speech evoked loud applause.

Word origin

From Latin 'evocare' meaning 'to call out, summon', from 'e-' (out) plus 'vocare' (to call), which is related to 'vox', the Latin for voice.

Remember it

EVOKE = E + VO(ice) + KE: a voice calls something out of memory.
