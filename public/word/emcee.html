EMCEE

noun · 2 syllables · /'ɛm.siː/

EMCEE

What does "EMCEE" mean?

A person who hosts and introduces the acts or speakers at an event.

Meanings

  1. The host who announces performers and keeps an event moving. The emcee kept the crowd laughing between acts.
  2. A rapper or vocalist who leads and energizes a hip-hop performance. The emcee traded verses with the DJ all night. informal
  3. To act as the host of an event. She agreed to emcee the awards ceremony.

Did you know?

  • Emcee is one of the rare English words that is just an abbreviation spelled out loud - it writes the letters M-C as you say them, short for 'master of ceremonies'.

Word origin

A spelled-out pronunciation of the abbreviation 'MC', which stands for 'master of ceremonies'; the word literally writes out the two letters 'M' and 'C'.

Remember it

EMCEE = say the letters M-C: the host is literally the 'M.C.' written down.

What it teaches

The best host disappears into the event: your job is the room's momentum, not your own spotlight.
