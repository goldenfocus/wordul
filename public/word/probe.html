PROBE

verb · 1 syllable · /proʊb/

PROBE

What does "PROBE" mean?

To explore or examine something closely, or an instrument or craft used to do so.

Meanings

  1. To investigate or examine something thoroughly. Investigators probed the cause of the collapse.
  2. To physically explore a surface or cavity with a tool or finger. The dentist probed the tooth for soft spots.
  3. A blunt surgical or scientific instrument used to explore a wound or measure something. She slid the probe along the patient's gumline. technical
  4. An unmanned spacecraft sent to gather data about distant bodies. The space probe sent back the first images of Pluto.
  5. A thorough investigation or inquiry. The corruption probe lasted two years.

Did you know?

  • Launched in 1977, NASA's Voyager 1 probe crossed into interstellar space in 2012 - the first human-made object ever to leave the Sun's bubble entirely.

Word origin

From Medieval Latin 'proba' (examination), from Latin 'probare' (to test, prove); the same root gives English 'prove', 'probable', and 'probation'.

Remember it

PROBE shares its root with PROVE - both come from Latin 'probare', to test, whether you're testing a wound or a planet.
