CLIMB

verb · 1 syllable · /klaɪm/

CLIMB

What does "CLIMB" mean?

To go up or move toward a higher position, typically using the hands and feet.

Meanings

  1. To go upward, especially by gripping with hands and feet. They climbed the rope to the top of the wall.
  2. To rise or increase gradually. Temperatures climbed throughout the afternoon.
  3. To move into or out of something with effort. She climbed into bed and pulled up the covers.
  4. An act of climbing; an ascent. The climb to the summit took six hours.

Did you know?

  • The silent 'b' in 'climb' is a fossil: speakers once sounded it, and the word still shares its root with 'clamber'.

Word origin

From Old English 'climban', from a West Germanic root related to 'clamber' and 'cleave'; the silent 'b' was once pronounced, surviving from the original consonant cluster.

Remember it
