GIVEN

adjective · 2 syllables · /'ɡɪv.ən/

GIVEN

What does "GIVEN" mean?

Specified, assumed, or granted as a basis for reasoning; the past participle of give.

Meanings

  1. Specified or particular; already fixed or named. On any given day the office is half empty.
  2. Taking into account; considering. Given the weather, we cancelled the hike.
  3. Something assumed or accepted as fact without proof. In this town, rain in November is a given.
  4. Past participle of 'give': to have handed over or granted. She had given him every chance.

Did you know?

  • When mathematicians say 'a given', they're translating Latin 'datum' - literally 'a thing given' - which is also the root of the word 'data'.

Word origin

Past participle of 'give', from Old English 'giefan' (to give); the logical and mathematical sense of 'a given' is a translation of Latin 'datum', literally 'something given'.

Remember it

GIVEN = GIVE + N; it's the form of 'give' you reach for after 'have', as in 'have given'.

A little poem

We argue hard from premises we've never weighed-

What it teaches
