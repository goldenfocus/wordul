VAPOR

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈveɪpər/

VAPOR

What does "VAPOR" mean?

A substance diffused or suspended in the air, especially one normally liquid or solid.

Meanings

  1. The gaseous phase of a substance that is liquid or solid at ordinary temperatures, such as water vapor. Water vapor condenses on the cold glass.
  2. A visible suspension of fine particles or droplets in air, such as mist, fog, or fumes. Vapor rose off the hot road after the rain.
  3. To rise or pass off as vapor; to evaporate. The puddle vapored away by noon.

Did you know?

  • Water vapor - not carbon dioxide - is Earth's most abundant greenhouse gas, doing about half the warming work that keeps the planet from freezing solid.

Word origin

From Latin 'vapor' meaning 'steam, heat, exhalation', via Old French 'vapour'; the British spelling keeps the 'u' as 'vapour'.

Remember it

VAPOR sounds like 'vape-or' - the rising plume of a vape is literally vapor leaving the liquid behind.
