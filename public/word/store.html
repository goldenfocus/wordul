STORE

noun · 1 syllable · /stɔːr/

STORE

What does "STORE" mean?

A place where goods are sold to the public.

Meanings

  1. A retail establishment where merchandise is sold. The corner store stays open until midnight.
  2. A supply or stock of something kept for future use. The squirrels built up a store of acorns for winter.
  3. To keep or accumulate something for future use. We store the firewood under a tarp behind the shed.
  4. To retain data in a computer's memory or on a storage device. The app stores your settings locally on the phone. technical

Did you know?

  • When you 'set great store by' something, you are using the oldest English sense of store - a stock or hoard of value - a usage running back to the 1400s, long before store meant a shop.

Word origin

From Old French 'estor', meaning provisions or supplies, from the verb 'estorer' (to build, furnish), from Latin 'instaurare', to restore or renew.

Remember it
