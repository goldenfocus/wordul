SMALL

adjective · 1 syllable · /smɔːl/

SMALL

What does "SMALL" mean?

Of a size, amount, or degree that is less than normal or average.

Meanings

  1. Little in size, quantity, or extent. They live in a small apartment near the river.
  2. Minor or unimportant in significance. It was a small mistake, easily fixed.
  3. Operating on a modest scale; not large or powerful. She runs a small business from home.
  4. The narrowest part of something, especially the lower back. He felt a twinge in the small of his back.
  5. Into small pieces; on a small scale. Chop the onions small. informal

Did you know?

  • For much of English history 'small' meant narrow or slender, not little - a 'small' waist or a 'small' stream meant a thin one, and the modern size-based sense only fully took over later.

Word origin

From Old English 'smæl', meaning slender or narrow, from Proto-Germanic 'smalaz'; related to German 'schmal' (narrow).
