STIFF

adjective · 1 syllable · /stɪf/

STIFF

What does "STIFF" mean?

Not easily bent; rigid and resistant to movement.

Meanings

  1. Firm and difficult to bend or move. The new boots were stiff and rubbed his heels.
  2. Aching and lacking suppleness, as muscles after exertion. My neck is stiff after sleeping at a bad angle.
  3. Formal, awkward, or lacking warmth in manner. His speech was polite but stiff.
  4. Severe, strong, or difficult in degree. They faced stiff competition and a stiff penalty.
  5. To cheat someone of money owed, especially a tip or payment. The table left and stiffed the waiter. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'stif' meaning rigid or unbending, from a Proto-Germanic root shared with Dutch 'stijf' and German 'steif'.
