DENSE

adjective · 1 syllable · /dɛns/

DENSE

What does "DENSE" mean?

Closely packed together; having a high mass per unit of volume.

Meanings

  1. Having parts crowded closely together; thick or compact. The hikers lost the trail in the dense undergrowth.
  2. Having a high mass relative to volume; heavy for its size. Lead is so dense that a small brick of it is surprisingly hard to lift. technical
  3. Hard to understand because it is so packed with meaning or detail. The opening chapter is dense, but it rewards a second reading.
  4. Slow to understand; stupid. Sorry, I'm being dense - can you explain that again? informal

Word origin

From Latin 'densus' meaning thick or crowded, via French 'dense' into English in the 16th century.
