adjective · 2 syllables · /'mɔːr.əl/

MORAL

What does "MORAL" mean?

Concerned with the principles of right and wrong behavior and the goodness of character.

Meanings

  1. Relating to the distinction between right and wrong conduct. She faced a moral dilemma between loyalty and the truth.
  2. Holding or showing high standards of good behavior. He was a deeply moral man who never broke a promise.
  3. A practical lesson taught by a story or experience. The moral of the fable is that slow and steady wins the race.
  4. Standards of conduct or principles of right and wrong (usually plural, 'morals'). The scandal raised questions about the company's morals.

Did you know?

  • The word 'moral' was custom-built by Cicero: he forged Latin 'moralis' specifically to translate the Greek 'ethikos', so 'moral' and 'ethical' began life as twins in two languages.

Word origin

From Latin 'moralis', coined by Cicero to translate Greek 'ethikos', from 'mos' (plural 'mores'), meaning 'custom, manner'.
