LEAKY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'liː.ki/

LEAKY

What does "LEAKY" mean?

Allowing liquid, gas, or information to escape through a hole or crack.

Meanings

  1. Letting fluid or gas pass through unintentionally. A leaky faucet kept us awake all night.
  2. Prone to letting secret information escape. The campaign had a leaky inner circle. figurative
  3. In computing, losing resources such as memory over time. The app got slower and slower because of a leaky process. technical

Word origin

From 'leak', probably from Old Norse 'leka' meaning to drip or leak, plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

LEAKY = LEAK + Y: the Y is the drip falling off the end.

Wordplay

What it teaches
