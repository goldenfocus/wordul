THROB

verb · 1 syllable · /θrɒb/

THROB

What does "THROB" mean?

To beat or pulsate with a strong, steady, often painful rhythm.

Meanings

  1. To pulsate or beat strongly and rhythmically, as the heart or an aching wound. Her temples throbbed after the long flight.
  2. To vibrate or resonate with a deep, rhythmic intensity, as music or an engine. The bass throbbed through the floor.
  3. A strong, rhythmic beat or pulsation. He felt the throb of pain return.

Word origin

Middle English 'throbben', probably of imitative origin, echoing the dull, repeated beat it names.

Remember it

THROB sounds like what it means - a heavy, rounded 'throb' you can feel land twice in your chest.
