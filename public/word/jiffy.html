noun · 2 syllables · /'dʒɪf.i/

JIFFY

What does "JIFFY" mean?

A very short moment of time; an instant.

Meanings

  1. A brief moment, especially in the phrase 'in a jiffy'. Hang on, I'll be back in a jiffy. informal
  2. In physics and computing, a defined small unit of time (varying by field, sometimes the time light travels one centimetre). In some physics texts a jiffy is the time light takes to cross a centimetre. technical

Did you know?

  • A 'jiffy' is a real scientific unit, not just a figure of speech: chemist Gilbert N. Lewis defined one as the time light takes to travel a single centimetre - about 33.4 trillionths of a second.

Word origin

Of unknown origin, recorded from the late 1700s in the sense of 'a moment'; possibly thieves' slang. Later borrowed by physicists as a playful name for a precise tiny interval.
