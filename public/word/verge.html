VERGE

noun · 1 syllable · /vɜːrdʒ/

VERGE

What does "VERGE" mean?

An edge, border, or point beyond which something happens.

Meanings

  1. The point at which something is about to happen; the brink. She was on the verge of tears.
  2. A grassy edge or border, especially along a road or path. Wildflowers grew on the grass verge by the lane.
  3. To border on or approach a condition or quality. His confidence verged on arrogance.

Did you know?

  • The 'verge' you stand on the edge of and the 'verger' who carries a rod in church processions are the same word: both come from Latin 'virga', a staff - the rod once marked the boundary of authority.

Word origin

From Latin 'virga' meaning 'rod, staff, or wand', via Old French 'verge'; an English usher once carried a 'verge' of office, and the staff's reach marked the bounds of authority - hence the sense of an edge or border.

Remember it

VERGE = the EDGE you can't quite reach; both are thin lines you stand right at, and they even rhyme.
