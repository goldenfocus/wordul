BLURT

verb · 1 syllable · /blɜːrt/

BLURT

What does "BLURT" mean?

To say something suddenly and without thinking, often by accident.

Meanings

  1. To utter words abruptly and impulsively, usually followed by 'out'. She blurted out the secret before she could stop herself.

Word origin

First recorded in the late 16th century, probably imitative in origin - the sound suggesting a sudden, forceful release of breath and speech.

Remember it

BLURT sounds like what it means - a short BLUR of speech that escapes before the brain can catch it.
