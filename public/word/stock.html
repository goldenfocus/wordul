STOCK

noun · 1 syllable · /stɒk/

STOCK

What does "STOCK" mean?

A supply of goods kept on hand for sale or use.

Meanings

  1. The goods or merchandise kept available by a business. The store ran out of stock before the holidays.
  2. A share in the ownership of a company; capital raised by issuing such shares. She bought stock in a renewable-energy firm.
  3. A savoury liquid made by simmering bones, meat, or vegetables, used as a cooking base. Add a cup of chicken stock to the risotto.
  4. Farm animals raised for use or profit; livestock. The drought left little water for the stock.
  5. To supply or fill with goods or a supply of something. We stocked the cabin with firewood before winter.
  6. Kept regularly in supply; standard or commonly used. He gave the same stock answer to every question.

Did you know?

  • The whole idea of buying stock traces to 1602, when the Dutch East India Company became the first business to sell tradable shares to the general public on an open exchange.

Word origin

From Old English 'stocc' meaning a tree trunk or log; the sense widened to a fixed store, then to a fund or capital, and finally to company shares.

Remember it

STOCK began as a wooden STOCK - a tree trunk - which is why your wealth can still be 'rooted' in shares.
