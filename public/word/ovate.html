OVATE

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈoʊ.veɪt/

OVATE

What does "OVATE" mean?

Egg-shaped, especially of a leaf broader at the base than the tip.

Meanings

  1. Shaped like the outline of an egg, typically with the wider end at the bottom; used especially to describe leaves. The shrub has glossy ovate leaves tapering to a point. technical
  2. A member of one of the three grades within a Welsh-influenced druidic order. He was initiated as an ovate before progressing to bard. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'ovatus', meaning egg-shaped, from 'ovum' meaning egg; the botanical sense was adopted to describe leaf outlines.

Remember it

OVATE = 'OVal' shape with a tidy ending; both start with the Latin 'ovum', egg.
