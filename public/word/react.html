REACT

verb · 2 syllables · /riˈækt/

REACT

What does "REACT" mean?

To respond or act in answer to something that has happened or been done.

Meanings

  1. To respond to a stimulus, action, or situation. How did she react when she heard the news?
  2. To undergo a chemical change when in contact with another substance. Sodium reacts violently with water. technical
  3. To act in opposition to something, as a counter-influence (often as 'react against'). His minimalism was a reaction against his parents' cluttered home.

Word origin

From Latin 're-' (back, again) plus 'agere' (to do or drive), via medieval Latin 'reagere' (to act back); the same 'agere' root gives 'act', 'agent', and 'agile'.

Remember it

REACT is just 'act' with 're-' in front: to act back. The cause acts, you re-act.
