OFTEN

adverb · 2 syllables · /'ɔ.fən/

OFTEN

What does "OFTEN" mean?

Frequently; many times or in many cases.

Meanings

  1. On many occasions; frequently. He often walks to work when the weather is clear.

Did you know?

  • The 't' in 'often' was silent for generations, like the 't' in 'soften' and 'listen'; today's spoken 't' is a partial comeback driven by people pronouncing the word the way it is spelled.

Word origin

From Middle English 'often', an extended form of older 'oft' (Old English 'oft', meaning frequently); the final '-en' was likely added by analogy with 'seldom'.

Remember it

OFTEN is OFT + EN; the old word 'oft' still hides inside it, the way it once did in poetry.

What it teaches

How often we do a thing, more than how well, is what quietly becomes our character.
