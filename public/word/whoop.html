WHOOP

noun · 1 syllable · /wuːp/

WHOOP

What does "WHOOP" mean?

A loud cry of excitement, joy, or encouragement.

Meanings

  1. A loud shout or cry, especially of joy or triumph. A whoop went up from the crowd when the goal was scored.
  2. To utter a loud cry of joy, excitement, or encouragement. The children whooped as the rollercoaster plunged.
  3. The convulsive gasp that follows a fit of coughing in whooping cough. The doctor recognized the disease by the distinctive whoop. technical

Did you know?

  • Whooping cough gets its name from the strained 'whoop' a sufferer makes sucking in air after a violent coughing fit - the disease is named after a noise, not a symptom.

Word origin

From Old French 'houper' meaning 'to call out, shout', of imitative origin echoing the sound of the cry itself.
