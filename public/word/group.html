GROUP

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡruːp/

GROUP

What does "GROUP" mean?

A number of people or things gathered, classified, or considered together.

Meanings

  1. A set of people or things regarded as a unit. A small group of hikers waited at the trailhead.
  2. To place or come together in a set. She grouped the photos by the year they were taken.
  3. A musical ensemble or band. The group released its first album in the spring.
  4. In mathematics, a set with an operation satisfying closure, associativity, identity, and inverses. The integers under addition form a group. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'group' anchors a whole branch of math: group theory, developed from Evariste Galois's work around 1832, proves no general formula can solve every fifth-degree equation by radicals.

Word origin

From French 'groupe', borrowed from Italian 'gruppo' meaning a cluster or knot, likely of Germanic origin related to a rounded mass.
