TANGO

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈtæŋɡoʊ/

TANGO

What does "TANGO" mean?

A passionate Argentine ballroom dance in 2/4 time, danced in close embrace.

Meanings

  1. A South American ballroom dance, marked by long gliding steps and abrupt pauses, danced by a couple in close hold. They learned the tango for their first dance.
  2. The music written for or in the style of this dance. A mournful tango drifted out of the cafe.
  3. To dance the tango. We tangoed badly but laughed the whole time.
  4. The code word for the letter 'T' in the NATO phonetic alphabet. Tango, Alpha, November - that spells the call sign. technical

Did you know?

  • UNESCO added the tango to its list of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2009, recognizing the dance born in the riverside slums of Buenos Aires and Montevideo as a shared human treasure.

Word origin

From Argentine Spanish 'tango', the dance born in the working-class barrios of Buenos Aires and Montevideo in the late 19th century; the word's deeper origin is debated, possibly African.

Remember it
