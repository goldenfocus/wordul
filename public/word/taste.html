TASTE

noun · 1 syllable · /teɪst/

TASTE

What does "TASTE" mean?

The sensation of flavor perceived in the mouth, or one's sense of what is good.

Meanings

  1. The sensation of flavor detected by the tongue and palate. The soup had a smoky, peppery taste.
  2. To perceive the flavor of food or drink; to sample. Taste this and tell me if it needs salt.
  3. A person's judgment of what is beautiful, fashionable, or proper. The room was decorated with impeccable taste.
  4. A small amount or brief experience of something. The trip gave her a taste of true wilderness. figurative

Did you know?

  • Your tongue detects only five basic tastes - sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami; everything else you 'taste' is actually smell, which is why food turns bland when your nose is blocked.

Word origin

From Old French 'taster' meaning 'to touch, try, sample', from Vulgar Latin 'taxitare', a frequentative of Latin 'taxare', 'to touch sharply, assess'.

Remember it

TASTE began as 'to touch and try' - the tongue is just the mouth reaching out to test.
