TRIBE

noun · 1 syllable · /traɪb/

TRIBE

What does "TRIBE" mean?

A social group united by kinship, culture, language, or common ancestry.

Meanings

  1. A community bound together by shared ancestry, customs, or leadership. The treaty was signed by elders representing each tribe along the river.
  2. A group of people sharing an interest, identity, or way of life. At the festival she finally found her tribe. informal
  3. A taxonomic rank in biology between family and genus. Cattle and bison belong to the same tribe, Bovini. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'tribe' goes back to the Latin 'tribus', which first named the three original divisions of Rome's people.

Word origin

From Latin 'tribus', one of the original divisions of the Roman people; into Old French and then English.
