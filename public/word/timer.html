TIMER

noun · 2 syllables · /'taɪ.mər/

TIMER

What does "TIMER" mean?

A device that measures or counts down a set period of time and signals when it ends.

Meanings

  1. An automatic mechanism that controls an action after a set interval, or measures elapsed time. She set the timer for twelve minutes so the eggs wouldn't overcook.
  2. A person who records the time taken in a race or event. The official timer clicked the stopwatch as the runner crossed the line.

Word origin

From 'time' (Old English 'tīma', a stretch or period) plus the agent suffix '-er' meaning 'a thing that does'; literally 'a thing that times'.

Remember it

TIMER = TIME + R; the R is the 'ring' it makes when time is up.
