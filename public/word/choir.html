CHOIR

noun · 1 syllable · /'kwaɪər/

CHOIR

What does "CHOIR" mean?

An organized group of singers, especially one that performs in a church.

Meanings

  1. A group of singers who perform together. The school choir sang carols in the town square.
  2. The part of a church, typically east of the nave, where the choir and clergy sit. Light streamed through the windows above the choir. technical
  3. A grouping of instruments of the same family in an orchestra (e.g. the brass choir). The brass choir opened the symphony's final movement. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Choir' used to be spelled 'quire', matching how it sounds; 17th-century scholars deliberately respelled it to resemble the Latin 'chorus', which is why the word now looks nothing like its pronunciation.

Word origin

From Old French 'cuer', from Latin 'chorus', from Greek 'khoros' (a band of dancers and singers); the spelling was deliberately re-Latinized to 'choir' in the 17th century, which is why it looks nothing like it sounds.

Remember it

CHOIR is the rare word where 'CH' sounds like 'K' and 'OIR' sounds like 'wire' - K-wire, a choir of voices on a wire of melody.
