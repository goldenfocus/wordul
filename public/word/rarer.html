RARER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈreə.rər/

RARER

What does "RARER" mean?

More uncommon or infrequent than something else; the comparative form of rare.

Meanings

  1. Occurring less often or found in smaller numbers (comparative of rare, meaning uncommon). Snow leopards are rarer than tigers in the wild.
  2. Cooked for a shorter time, leaving more red interior (comparative of rare, of meat). He likes his steak even rarer than I do, barely past raw.
  3. Of a gas or substance, less dense or thinner (comparative of rare, meaning rarefied). The air grows rarer the higher the climbers ascend. technical

Word origin

Comparative of 'rare', from Latin 'rarus' (loose, sparse, scattered, hence uncommon); the meat sense comes from a separate Old English root 'hrere' (lightly cooked).

What it teaches

Scarcity sharpens value: the rarer the thing, the harder we look at it.
