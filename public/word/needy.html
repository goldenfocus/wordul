NEEDY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'niː.di/

NEEDY

What does "NEEDY" mean?

Wanting a great deal of attention, affection, or reassurance; or lacking basic necessities.

Meanings

  1. Demanding more emotional support or attention than is reasonable. He grew clingy and needy whenever she traveled for work.
  2. Lacking the basic necessities of life; poor. The charity distributes meals to needy families each winter.

Word origin

From Old English 'nedig', from 'ned', 'need', plus the adjective suffix '-ig' (modern '-y').

Remember it

NEEDY = NEED + Y; the double E is two open eyes always looking for more.
