CROWN

noun · 1 syllable · /kraʊn/

CROWN

What does "CROWN" mean?

An ornamental headdress worn by a monarch as a symbol of authority.

Meanings

  1. A circular ornamental headdress worn by a king or queen. The jeweled crown was kept under guard in the tower.
  2. The monarchy or sovereign power of a country, considered as an institution. The land belonged to the Crown for three centuries.
  3. The top of the head or of a hat. He set the hat squarely on the crown of his head.
  4. An artificial cap fitted over a damaged or decayed tooth. The dentist fitted a porcelain crown over the cracked molar. technical
  5. To place a crown on, or to formally make someone a monarch. She was crowned queen at the age of twenty-five.
  6. To be the finest or final part of something. A spectacular sunset crowned the perfect day. figurative

Did you know?

  • The crown on a king and the corona around the sun share one Latin root: 'corona', a wreath - so the sun wears the same word a monarch does.

Word origin

From Latin 'corona' (wreath, garland), via Old French 'corone' and Anglo-Norman 'coroune'; the original sense was a circular wreath of honor, not a metal headdress.
