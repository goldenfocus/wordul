URBAN

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈɜːr.bən/

URBAN

What does "URBAN" mean?

Relating to, located in, or characteristic of a city or town.

Meanings

  1. Of or relating to a city or densely built-up area. Urban traffic slowed to a crawl during the morning rush.
  2. Characteristic of modern city culture, especially in music and style. The festival showcased urban dance and street art.

Did you know?

  • Around 2007, humanity quietly crossed a threshold it had never crossed before: for the first time, more than half of all people on Earth lived in urban areas rather than the countryside.

Word origin

From Latin 'urbanus' ('of the city'), from 'urbs' ('city'); the related 'urbane' (meaning polished and sophisticated) shares the same root.

Remember it

URBAN shares its root with 'urbane' — both come from Latin 'urbs', the city.
