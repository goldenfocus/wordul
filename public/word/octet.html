noun · 2 syllables · /ɑk'tɛt/

OCTET

What does "OCTET" mean?

A group of eight, especially eight musicians, a piece for them, or a unit of eight bits in computing.

Meanings

  1. A group of eight musicians, or a musical composition written for eight performers. Schubert's octet is scored for strings and winds and runs nearly an hour.
  2. A unit of eight bits, used in computing to mean unambiguously what 'byte' usually means. Network standards specify packet sizes in octets to avoid confusion about byte length. technical
  3. Any set or group of eight people or things. An octet of rowers strained against the current.

Did you know?

  • Networking engineers say 'octet' instead of 'byte' on purpose: early computers used bytes of 6, 7, or 9 bits, so 'octet' was adopted to mean exactly eight bits with zero ambiguity.

Word origin

From Latin 'octo' (eight), shaped on the model of 'duet' and 'quartet' to name a group of eight.

Remember it

OCTET = OCT (eight) + ET, the same '-et' that makes a quartet four; an octet is simply twice as crowded.

Wordplay

  • An octet walked into a bar. The bartender said, 'Sorry, we don't serve groups of eight bits.' They left, byte by byte.

What it teaches

The same eight can be a chord or a byte; what a group means depends on the language you count it in.
