GONER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɡɔː.nər/

GONER

What does "GONER" mean?

A person or thing that is doomed, beyond rescue, or as good as dead.

Meanings

  1. Someone or something past saving - certain to die, fail, or be lost. Once the engine caught fire, the old truck was a goner. informal

Word origin

An American colloquialism from the mid-1800s, built straightforwardly from the verb 'gone' plus the agent suffix '-er' - literally 'one who is gone'.

Remember it

GONER = 'gone' + 'er': add two letters to 'gone' and you've named the one already lost.

A little poem

The doctor sighed and shook his head;
a goner walks but counts as dead.

couplet

What it teaches

We write people off the moment we stop imagining their rescue.
