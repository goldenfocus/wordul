HEFTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'hef.ti/

HEFTY

What does "HEFTY" mean?

Large, heavy, or substantial in size or amount.

Meanings

  1. Large and heavy; solidly built. He swung a hefty axe at the log. informal
  2. Substantial in amount, especially of money. The repair came with a hefty bill. informal

Word origin

From 'heft' ('weight, the feel of weight when lifted'), a noun built from 'heave' by analogy with words like 'theft'; '-y' adds the adjectival sense.

Remember it

HEFTY shares its heft with 'heave' - both about real, liftable weight; hear the effort in the 'hef'.

A little poem

The invoice landed with a thud-
hefty in ink, if not in mud.

couplet

Wordplay

  • The dictionary gave 'hefty' a hefty definition. Both senses applied: it was long, and it cost me a heavy late fee.

What it teaches

Hefty is honest about its weight; beware the things that are heavy but pretend to be light.
