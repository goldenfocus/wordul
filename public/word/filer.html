FILER

noun · 2 syllables · /'faɪ.lər/

FILER

What does "FILER" mean?

A person who files documents or claims, especially in an official process.

Meanings

  1. A person who submits a formal document, such as a tax return or legal claim. Early filers usually get their tax refunds first.
  2. A person or device that arranges documents in order for storage. She was a meticulous filer who never lost a single contract.
  3. One who uses a file tool to smooth or shape a surface. The metal filer worked the burr off the edge.

Did you know?

  • A 'filer' is named after thread: the office word 'file' comes from Latin 'filum', because documents were once literally strung on a wire or string to keep them in sequence.

Word origin

An agent noun from the verb 'file', from Old French 'filer' (to spin thread) and Latin 'filum' (thread), originally referring to stringing documents on a thread or wire.

Remember it

FILER = one who FILEs; add '-r' to the verb, like a baker bakes and a filer files.
