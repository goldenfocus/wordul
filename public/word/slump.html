SLUMP

verb · 1 syllable · /slʌmp/

SLUMP

What does "SLUMP" mean?

To sink, drop, or decline heavily and suddenly.

Meanings

  1. To fall or sink down heavily, especially in posture. Exhausted, she slumped into the armchair.
  2. A marked, prolonged decline in economic activity, prices, or performance. The housing market entered a deep slump.
  3. A period of poor form, especially in sport. The striker is mired in a goal-scoring slump. informal

Word origin

Probably of imitative or Scandinavian origin, akin to Norwegian and Danish 'slumpe' (to fall into, happen by chance); first recorded in the late 17th century.
