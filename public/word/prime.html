PRIME

adjective · 1 syllable · /praɪm/

PRIME

What does "PRIME" mean?

Of first importance or highest quality; or a number divisible only by 1 and itself.

Meanings

  1. Of the greatest importance or the best quality. Customer trust is our prime concern.
  2. The stage of greatest vigor, success, or perfection. The athlete was in her prime at twenty-six.
  3. A whole number greater than 1 with no positive divisors other than 1 and itself. Seven is a prime, but nine is not. technical
  4. To prepare something for use or action. Prime the wall before painting it.
  5. To make ready by supplying information or influence beforehand. The lawyer primed the witness on what to expect.

Did you know?

  • Around 300 BC Euclid proved there are infinitely many prime numbers - a result so durable it is still the proof students meet first in number theory today.

Word origin

From Latin 'primus' (first); the mathematical sense reflects numbers that are 'first' in the sense of indivisible building blocks of all integers.

Remember it

PRIME starts like 'primary' and 'primal' - all from Latin 'primus', first.
