UNTIE

verb · 2 syllables · /ʌnˈtaɪ/

UNTIE

What does "UNTIE" mean?

To undo or loosen a knot, bond, or fastening.

Meanings

  1. To loosen or unfasten something that is tied. He knelt to untie his daughter's tangled shoelace.
  2. To free a person or animal that is bound. She hurried to untie the horse from the post before the storm.
  3. To resolve or release something difficult, as a problem or restriction. The new ruling helped untie a decades-old legal tangle. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'untīgan', combining the reversing prefix 'un-' with 'tīgan' ('to tie'), itself from the noun 'tēag' ('a tie, rope').

Remember it

UNTIE is an anagram of UNITE — the same letters tie people together or take a knot apart.
