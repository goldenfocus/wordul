BREAD

noun · 1 syllable · /brɛd/

BREAD

What does "BREAD" mean?

A staple food made from flour, water, and usually yeast, baked into a loaf.

Meanings

  1. A baked food made from a kneaded dough of flour and water, typically leavened. She tore off a piece of warm bread and dipped it in oil.
  2. The means of staying alive; one's livelihood or food in general. Teaching was the bread that kept the family fed. figurative
  3. Money. He took the night shift just to earn a little bread. informal
  4. To coat food with breadcrumbs before cooking. Bread the cutlets and fry them until golden.

Did you know?

  • The word 'lord' literally means 'loaf-keeper' - from Old English 'hlafweard' - while 'lady' meant 'loaf-kneader,' so power in early England was measured in bread.

Word origin

From Old English 'bread', originally meaning a morsel or piece; it gradually replaced the older word 'hlaf' (the source of 'loaf') as the general term.
