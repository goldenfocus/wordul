CARRY

verb · 2 syllables · /'kær.i/

CARRY

What does "CARRY" mean?

To hold and move something from one place to another.

Meanings

  1. To support and transport something while moving. She carried the groceries up three flights of stairs.
  2. To have or hold (a quality, consequence, or meaning). The crime carries a heavy sentence.
  3. To support the weight of, or to bear responsibility for a group's success. He practically carried the whole team that season. informal
  4. To transfer a digit to the next column when adding numbers. Add the units, then carry the one. technical

Word origin

From Anglo-Norman 'carier' ('to transport in a vehicle'), from Late Latin 'carricare' ('to load a wagon'), from Latin 'carrus' ('a wheeled vehicle'); the same root behind 'cargo', 'car', and 'charge'.

Remember it

CARRY has two R's because it's heavy - it takes a double effort to carry a load.
