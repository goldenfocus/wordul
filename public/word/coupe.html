noun · 1 syllable · /kuːp/

COUPE

What does "COUPE" mean?

A two-door car with a fixed roof and a sloping rear, typically sportier than a sedan.

Meanings

  1. A closed two-door automobile, usually with a sloping back and a sporty profile. He traded the family sedan for a sleek black coupe.
  2. A shallow, broad-bowled stemmed glass, traditionally used for champagne or cocktails. The bartender poured the daiquiri into a frosted coupe.

Did you know?

  • The car name comes from the French 'carrosse coupe' - literally a 'cut carriage' - a horse-drawn coach shortened to hold a single row of seats.

Word origin

From French 'coupe' (carriage cut short), past participle of 'couper' (to cut), originally a 'carrosse coupe', a shortened carriage with one row of seats.

Remember it

COUPE is French for 'cut' - the car is a sedan with the back seats cut off, and the glass is a bowl cut wide and shallow.
