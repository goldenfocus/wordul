CHUTE

noun · 1 syllable · /ʃuːt/

CHUTE

What does "CHUTE" mean?

A sloping channel or tube down which things slide or fall.

Meanings

  1. A steep sloped passage for conveying things to a lower level. The mail dropped down the chute to the lobby five floors below.
  2. A rapid descent or sloping fall of water in a stream or river. The kayakers braced as the river narrowed into a foaming chute.
  3. A short, informal term for a parachute. He pulled the cord and the chute snapped open above him. informal

Word origin

From French 'chute', 'a fall', from Old French 'cheoite', past participle of 'cheoir', 'to fall'; in America blended with English 'shoot'.

Remember it

CHUTE is pronounced like 'shoot' but spelled with the French 'CHU' - things shoot down a chute.
