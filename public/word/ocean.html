OCEAN

noun · 2 syllables · /'oʊ.ʃən/

OCEAN

What does "OCEAN" mean?

A vast continuous body of salt water covering most of Earth's surface.

Meanings

  1. The continuous mass of salt water covering about 71% of Earth's surface. Two thirds of the planet is ocean, yet most of it has never been seen.
  2. A very great quantity or expanse of something. After the layoffs he had oceans of time. figurative

Did you know?

  • The ocean makes more than half the oxygen you breathe - most of it from microscopic drifting phytoplankton, not from trees.
"We are tied to the ocean. And when we go back to the sea, we are going back from whence we came."— John F. Kennedy

Word origin

From Greek 'Okeanos', the great river the Greeks believed encircled the Earth; into Latin 'oceanus', Old French 'occean', then English.

Remember it

O-C-E-A-N: the C in the middle is the 'sea' the word is named for.
