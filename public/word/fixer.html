FIXER

noun · 2 syllables · /'fɪk.sər/

FIXER

What does "FIXER" mean?

A person who arranges things, often discreetly resolving problems for others.

Meanings

  1. Someone who makes arrangements and solves problems for others, sometimes by questionable means. The studio kept a fixer on retainer to keep scandals out of the papers. informal
  2. A person who repairs or mends things. He's the family fixer; nothing broken stays broken for long.
  3. A chemical solution that makes a photographic image permanent by removing unexposed silver halides. After the developer, the print goes into the fixer to stop it fading. technical

Word origin

From the verb 'fix' (via Latin 'fixus', past participle of 'figere', 'to fasten') plus the agent suffix '-er', meaning 'one who makes fast or sets right'.

Remember it
