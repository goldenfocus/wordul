SHAPE

noun · 1 syllable · /ʃeɪp/

SHAPE

What does "SHAPE" mean?

The external form or outline of something; also, to give form to something.

Meanings

  1. The outer form, outline, or configuration of an object. The cloud drifted into the shape of a horse.
  2. The condition or state of something, especially of health or readiness. After months of training she was in great shape.
  3. To give a particular form to something, physically or figuratively. Childhood experiences shape how we see the world.

Word origin

From Old English 'gesceap', meaning creation, form, or condition, from the verb 'scieppan' (to create); related to the '-ship' in words like 'friendship'.

Remember it

SHAPE = SH + APE: even an ape can recognize a familiar shape.
