DEPTH

noun · 1 syllable · /dɛpθ/

DEPTH

What does "DEPTH" mean?

The distance from the top or surface of something down to its bottom or back.

Meanings

  1. The measurement of how deep something is, from surface to bottom. Divers measured the depth of the wreck at sixty metres.
  2. Intensity, complexity, or seriousness of thought, feeling, or knowledge. Her essay showed a depth of insight rare in someone so young. figurative
  3. The deepest or most extreme part of something, often a time or place. In the depths of winter the lake froze solid enough to walk on.

Did you know?

  • The greatest depth on Earth is the Challenger Deep, roughly 10,935 metres down - deeper than Mount Everest is tall, with kilometres of water to spare.

Word origin

Formed in Middle English from 'deep' plus the noun-forming suffix '-th', on the model of 'length' from 'long'.

Remember it

DEPTH = DEEP + TH, the same way LONG becomes LENGTH and STRONG becomes STRENGTH.
