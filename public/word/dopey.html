DOPEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'doʊ.pi/

DOPEY

What does "DOPEY" mean?

Sluggish and muddled, as if drugged or half-asleep; also, silly or foolish.

Meanings

  1. Dazed, drowsy, or stupefied, as if from a drug or lack of sleep. The medication left him dopey for most of the afternoon. informal
  2. Silly, foolish, or not very clever. It was a dopey idea, but it made everyone laugh. informal

Did you know?

  • In Disney's 1937 'Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs', Dopey is the only dwarf who never says a word - in the story, he simply has never tried to talk.

Word origin

From 'dope' (originally a thick liquid or drug preparation, from Dutch 'doop', 'sauce') plus '-y'; the sense moved from 'drugged' to 'stupefied' to 'foolish'.

Remember it

DOPEY sounds like 'dope' + 'y' - picture someone moving in slow motion, dopey from a long, lazy nap.
