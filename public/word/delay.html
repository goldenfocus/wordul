DELAY

verb · 2 syllables · /dɪˈleɪ/

DELAY

What does "DELAY" mean?

To make something happen later than planned, or the period by which it is postponed.

Meanings

  1. To postpone or put off to a later time. We had to delay the launch by two weeks.
  2. To cause to be late or slow; to hold up. Heavy snow delayed every flight out of the airport.
  3. A period of time by which something is late or postponed. There was a long delay before the show finally began.

Word origin

From Old French 'delaier' 'to put off', from 'de-' plus 'laier' 'to leave, let', a verb of uncertain origin possibly blending Latin 'laxare' 'to loosen' and Frankish roots.

Remember it

DELAY ends in 'lay' - to delay is to let a task lay around untouched a while longer.
