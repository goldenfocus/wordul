THOSE

determiner · 1 syllable · /ðoʊz/

THOSE

What does "THOSE" mean?

The plural of 'that', used for things farther away in space, time, or thought.

Meanings

  1. Used to identify specific plural things at a distance from the speaker. Those mountains are farther than they look.
  2. The distant or previously mentioned plural things. Those were the days.

Word origin

From Middle English 'thos', 'thas', plural forms of 'that' (Old English 'þā'), later reshaped on the model of 'those' versus the nearer 'these'.

Remember it

THOSE is THESE pushed away: same shape, but the 'o' rounds your mouth toward the distance.

A little poem

Those - the things I point to far,
a hilltop light, a faded star,
too distant now to say what they are.

What it teaches

We say 'those' for what we've set at arm's length; some distances we choose, and call them grammar.
