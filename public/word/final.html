FINAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'faɪ.nəl/

FINAL

What does "FINAL" mean?

Coming at the end; last in a series or process.

Meanings

  1. Coming at the end; last. The final chapter tied every loose thread together.
  2. Allowing no further change; conclusive. The referee's decision is final.
  3. The last and deciding game or contest in a tournament. They reached the final but lost on penalties.
  4. A concluding examination at the end of a course or term. She stayed up all night studying for her chemistry final. informal

Did you know?

  • A 'fine' you pay and the 'final' whistle share a root: both come from Latin 'finis' (end), since a fine was originally a settlement payment that brought a dispute to an end.

Word origin

From Latin 'finalis' (of or relating to an end), from 'finis' (end, boundary, limit), via Old French 'final'.

Remember it

FINAL contains 'fin' - the French and Latin word for 'end' you see at the close of old films.
