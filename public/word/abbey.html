ABBEY

noun · 2 syllables · /'æb.i/

ABBEY

What does "ABBEY" mean?

A monastery or convent under the rule of an abbot or abbess, or the church belonging to it.

Meanings

  1. A building or complex housing monks or nuns, governed by an abbot or abbess. The monks brewed beer in the abbey for nine centuries.
  2. A church that was once part of, or is named for, such a monastery. Coronations and royal weddings are still held in the abbey.

Word origin

From Old French 'abbeie', from Late Latin 'abbatia', from 'abbas' (abbot), itself from Aramaic 'abba' meaning 'father'.

Remember it

ABBEY starts with ABBA, Aramaic for 'father' - the root that also gave us 'abbot', the abbey's head.
