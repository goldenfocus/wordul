RACER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈreɪ.sər/

RACER

What does "RACER" mean?

A person, animal, or vehicle that competes in races, especially at high speed.

Meanings

  1. A person who competes in races. The young racer overtook two rivals on the final lap.
  2. A vehicle, animal, or boat built or bred for racing. The new racer cut three seconds off the track record.
  3. A slender, fast-moving North American snake of the genus Coluber. A black racer slid through the grass and vanished under the porch. technical

Word origin

From 'race', from Old Norse 'rás' (a running, a rush), plus the agent suffix '-er' marking the one who does the action.

Remember it

RACER contains RACE, with the runner ('-er') tacked on the end - the one who does the racing.
