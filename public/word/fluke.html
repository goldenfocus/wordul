FLUKE

noun · 1 syllable · /fluːk/

FLUKE

What does "FLUKE" mean?

An unexpected stroke of luck or a chance success.

Meanings

  1. A lucky or surprising result achieved by chance rather than skill. Winning on his first try was a complete fluke.
  2. Either of the two lobes of a whale's tail. The humpback raised its flukes and slid beneath the surface.
  3. A flat parasitic worm, such as the liver fluke. The sheep were treated for liver fluke after the wet summer. technical
  4. The broad triangular blade at the end of an anchor's arm that digs into the seabed. The anchor's fluke caught firmly in the sand. technical

Did you know?

  • The 'lucky break' meaning of fluke was born at the billiards table: in the 1800s a fluke was a pocketed shot the player never aimed for, and the slang spread from the green baize into everyday English.

Word origin

The 'lucky chance' sense arose in 19th-century billiards slang for an accidental winning shot; the worm and whale-tail senses come from older, separate words, including Old English 'floc' for a flatfish.

Remember it

FLUKE rhymes with 'luck' stretched out - and a fluke is luck you didn't earn.
