adjective · 1 syllable · /naɪnθ/

NINTH

What does "NINTH" mean?

Coming after the eighth in a sequence; the ordinal corresponding to the number nine.

Meanings

  1. Occupying position nine in a counted order. She lives on the ninth floor, just below the roof garden.
  2. One of nine equal parts of a whole. Each heir received a ninth of the estate.
  3. A musical interval spanning an octave plus a second. The jazz chord leaned on a tense, hovering ninth. technical

Did you know?

  • When people say 'the Ninth', they usually mean one piece: Beethoven completed his Ninth Symphony in 1824, breaking with tradition by adding a full chorus in the final movement.

Word origin

From Old English 'nigoþa', later regularized to 'ninth' by analogy with 'nine' (Old English 'nigon'), from Proto-Germanic 'newun', from Proto-Indo-European 'newn'.

Remember it

NINTH is NINE with its 'e' traded for 'th' - the same swap every ordinal makes when it grows a tail.
