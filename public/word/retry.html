RETRY

verb · 2 syllables · /ri'traɪ/

RETRY

What does "RETRY" mean?

To attempt something again after a failure or rejection.

Meanings

  1. To try again, especially after an unsuccessful attempt. If the upload fails, the app will retry automatically.
  2. To try a legal case a second time. The court ordered the case to be retried before a new jury. technical
  3. An instance of trying again, especially an automated reattempt. The connection succeeded on the third retry. technical

Word origin

Formed in English from the prefix 're-' ('again', from Latin) plus 'try', which came through Old French 'trier', to pick out or sift.

Remember it

RE + TRY: 'try' with the 're-' that means 'again', exactly what the word does.
