TRAIN

noun · 1 syllable · /treɪn/

TRAIN

What does "TRAIN" mean?

A connected line of railway carriages, or the act of teaching a skill through practice.

Meanings

  1. A series of connected railway cars pulled by a locomotive. We caught the last train back to the city.
  2. To teach a skill or behavior through instruction and repeated practice. She trains rescue dogs for mountain work.
  3. To prepare oneself physically for a sport or feat through exercise. He trained for two years before the marathon.
  4. A long trailing part of a gown or robe. Pageboys carried the bride's train down the aisle.

Did you know?

  • A railway 'train' and a wedding gown's 'train' are the same word: both come from the sense of something dragged along behind - the railway just dragged carriages instead of cloth.

Word origin

From Old French 'train', 'a dragging or trailing', from 'trainer', 'to pull or drag', from Vulgar Latin 'traginare', related to Latin 'trahere', 'to draw'.

Remember it

All senses of TRAIN trail behind: a gown's train drags, a railway train pulls a line of cars, and to train is to draw a skill along through practice.
