SCARY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'skɛr.i/

SCARY

What does "SCARY" mean?

Causing fear or alarm; frightening.

Meanings

  1. Frightening or alarming. The basement stairs make a scary creak in the dark.
  2. Surprisingly impressive or uncannily accurate, used as informal praise. Her memory for names is scary good. informal

Word origin

Formed in English in the 16th century from the verb 'scare' (from Old Norse 'skirra', 'to frighten') plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

SCARY is SCAR plus Y - a scar is the mark fear leaves behind.
