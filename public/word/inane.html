INANE

adjective · 2 syllables · /ɪˈneɪn/

INANE

What does "INANE" mean?

Silly, empty, or lacking sense or meaning.

Meanings

  1. Silly, pointless, or devoid of substance. The meeting dissolved into inane chatter about parking spaces.
  2. Empty or void; without contents. He stared into the inane dark of the empty hangar. archaic

Did you know?

  • 'Inane' originally meant simply 'empty' (Latin 'inanis'); the modern sense of 'silly' is literally being empty of sense or content.

Word origin

From Latin 'inanis' (empty, void, worthless); the same root gives 'inanity' and the noun 'inane' used for empty space or the void.

Remember it

INANE is 'IN A vacuum' - empty space, no substance, just air.

What it teaches

What we call inane is rarely harmless; empty talk still fills the hour you can't get back.
