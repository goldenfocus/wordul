noun · 1 syllable · /ɡleɪd/

GLADE

What does "GLADE" mean?

An open, often grassy clearing within a forest or wood.

Meanings

  1. An open space surrounded by trees in a wood or forest. Sunlight pooled in the glade where the deer had stopped to graze.
  2. An everglade or marshy open tract, chiefly in regional and place-name use. The trail wound past a wet glade thick with sawgrass.

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, recorded in English from the 16th century; commonly linked to the word 'glad' in an older sense of 'bright, shining', a glade being a bright open spot in dark woods.
