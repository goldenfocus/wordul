TONAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'toʊ.nəl/

TONAL

What does "TONAL" mean?

Relating to tone, especially the system of musical keys or the use of pitch to distinguish meaning.

Meanings

  1. Relating to the musical system of keys built around a central note, the tonic. Most pop songs are firmly tonal, centered on a single key. technical
  2. Of a language in which pitch changes the meaning of a word. Mandarin is a tonal language, so the same syllable can mean four different things. technical
  3. Relating to color tone, shading, or overall quality of sound or mood. The film's tonal shift from comedy to grief caught viewers off guard.

Word origin

From 'tone' (Greek 'tonos', a stretching, pitch, or tension of a string) plus the adjective suffix '-al'; the root reflects the tightening of a string to raise its pitch.

Remember it

TONAL = TONE + AL; it's all about the TONE, whether in a song's key or a word's pitch.
