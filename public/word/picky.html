PICKY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'pɪk.i/

PICKY

What does "PICKY" mean?

Excessively fussy or hard to please, especially about small details.

Meanings

  1. Fussy and selective; difficult to satisfy. My nephew is a picky eater who refuses anything green. informal

Word origin

An American English formation from the verb 'pick' (to choose, single out) plus the adjective suffix '-y'; first recorded in the late 19th century in the sense of fussy choosing.

Remember it

A PICKY person can't stop picking - the verb 'pick' sits right inside the word, choosing and rejecting.
