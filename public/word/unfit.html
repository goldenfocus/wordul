UNFIT

adjective · 2 syllables · /ʌnˈfɪt/

UNFIT

What does "UNFIT" mean?

Not suitable, qualified, or in good enough condition for a purpose.

Meanings

  1. Not of the right quality or condition for a particular use. Inspectors declared the building unfit for human habitation.
  2. Not physically healthy or in good shape. After a winter indoors he felt badly unfit on the first climb.
  3. Not competent or proper for a role or responsibility. The court ruled him unfit to manage his own affairs. formal

Word origin

A compound of the negative prefix 'un-' and 'fit', an adjective of uncertain Middle English origin meaning 'suited' or 'proper'.

Remember it

UNFIT = UN + FIT: whatever 'fit' means - suited, healthy, proper - the 'un-' simply flips it to its opposite.

A little poem

Wordplay

What it teaches

Being unfit for one thing is rarely a verdict on your worth - only on the match.
