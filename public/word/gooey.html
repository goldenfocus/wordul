GOOEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈɡuː.i/

GOOEY

What does "GOOEY" mean?

Soft, sticky, and semi-liquid in texture.

Meanings

  1. Having a soft, thick, sticky consistency. The center of the brownie was warm and gooey. informal
  2. Excessively sentimental or mawkish. The card got a little gooey toward the end. figurative

Word origin

From 'goo', a 19th-century American word of uncertain origin (possibly imitative of a sticky sound), plus the adjectival '-ey' suffix.

Remember it

GOOEY: double-O for the puddle of goo, and the 'EY' is your fingers pulling apart in a sticky string.
