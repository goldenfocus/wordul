ARSON

noun · 2 syllables · /'ɑːr.sən/

ARSON

What does "ARSON" mean?

The crime of deliberately setting fire to property.

Meanings

  1. The intentional and unlawful burning of buildings or property. Investigators ruled the warehouse blaze an act of arson.

Did you know?

  • Under old English common law, 'arson' meant only the malicious burning of someone else's inhabited home - torching your own house didn't legally count.

Word origin

From Anglo-French 'arson' from Late Latin 'arsionem' ('a burning'), from Latin 'ardere' ('to burn') - the same root as 'ardor' and 'arid'.

Remember it

ARSON shares its burning root with ARDOR and ARID - all trace back to Latin 'ardere', 'to burn'. Hot AR- words.
