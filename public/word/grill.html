GRILL

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡrɪl/

GRILL

What does "GRILL" mean?

A frame of metal bars for cooking food over heat, or the act of cooking on one.

Meanings

  1. A device with parallel metal bars for cooking food over a heat source. He flipped the burgers on the grill.
  2. To cook food on a grill or under direct heat. We grilled vegetables and fish for dinner.
  3. To question someone intensely and persistently. Reporters grilled the senator about the scandal. informal
  4. A restaurant that serves grilled food, or its menu section. We met for lunch at the bar and grill.

Word origin

From French 'grille' (grating), from Latin 'craticula', a small gridiron — the diminutive of 'cratis' (wickerwork), the same root behind 'grate'.

Remember it

GRILL has a double-L like two parallel bars of a grilling grate.
