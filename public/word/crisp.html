CRISP

adjective · 1 syllable · /krɪsp/

CRISP

What does "CRISP" mean?

Firm, dry, and brittle in a fresh and pleasing way.

Meanings

  1. Firm and brittle so that it breaks or snaps cleanly, as fresh toast or a new bill. The apple was so crisp it cracked when she bit it.
  2. Cool, dry, and bracing, said of air or weather. We walked out into a crisp October morning.
  3. Brisk, clear, and decisive in manner or expression. Her report was short and crisp - no wasted words.
  4. A thin slice of fried potato eaten cold; the British term for a chip. He opened a bag of salt-and-vinegar crisps. informal
  5. A baked dessert of fruit under a crumbly topping. Grandma's apple crisp came out bubbling at the edges.

Did you know?

  • For centuries 'crisp' meant curly, not crunchy: it comes from Latin 'crispus' (curled), and English only later borrowed the word for the crackle of dried, brittle things.

Word origin

From Latin 'crispus' (curled), borrowed into Old English; the early English sense was 'curly', and the modern 'brittle' meaning grew from the crackling texture of curled, dried things.

Remember it

CRISP sounds like the snap it describes - say it sharply and you can almost hear the toast break.
