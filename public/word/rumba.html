RUMBA

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈrʌm.bə/

RUMBA

What does "RUMBA" mean?

A rhythmic Cuban dance and its accompanying music, marked by sensual hip movement.

Meanings

  1. An Afro-Cuban dance and music genre, also adapted into ballroom dance. They swayed through a slow rumba under the string lights.
  2. To dance the rumba. We rumbaed until the band packed up. informal

Did you know?

  • In 2016, UNESCO inscribed Cuban rumba on its list of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, recognizing it as a festive blend of music, dance, and song.

Word origin

From Cuban Spanish 'rumba', with roots in Afro-Cuban culture; the Spanish word originally suggested a spree, party, or carousing.
