HUMOR

noun · 2 syllables · /'hju.mɚ/

HUMOR

What does "HUMOR" mean?

The quality of being amusing, or the ability to find and express what is funny.

Meanings

  1. The quality of being comical or amusing. Her speech balanced sharp arguments with real humor.
  2. A particular mood or temporary state of mind. He was in no humor for jokes after the long flight. formal
  3. In old medicine, one of four bodily fluids thought to govern temperament. Medieval doctors blamed melancholy on an excess of the black humor. archaic
  4. To comply with the wishes of someone in order to keep them content. Just humor him and admire the model train set.

Did you know?

  • 'Humor' began as a medical term for bodily fluid - the four humors (blood, phlegm, yellow and black bile) were believed to set your temperament, which is how a word for liquid came to mean a sense of comedy.

Word origin

From Latin 'humor' (fluid, moisture); medieval medicine held that four bodily 'humors' set a person's mood, and the word drifted from 'bodily fluid' to 'temperament' to 'wit'.
