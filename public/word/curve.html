CURVE

noun · 1 syllable · /kɜːrv/

CURVE

What does "CURVE" mean?

A line that bends smoothly and continuously, without sharp angles.

Meanings

  1. A smoothly bending line or shape. The road followed the curve of the river.
  2. A line on a graph showing how one quantity varies with another. The infection curve finally began to flatten. technical
  3. To bend or cause to bend in a smooth, continuous arc. The pitcher could make the ball curve away at the last instant.

Word origin

From Latin 'curvus', meaning bent or crooked, entering English first as a verb in the 16th century.
