DIZZY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'dɪz.i/

DIZZY

What does "DIZZY" mean?

Feeling unsteady, as if everything is spinning and you might lose your balance.

Meanings

  1. Having a whirling sensation in the head with a tendency to lose balance. She stood up too fast and felt suddenly dizzy.
  2. Causing or producing a giddy, disorienting sensation, as of great speed or height. Stock prices climbed to dizzy heights before the crash. figurative
  3. Foolish, scatterbrained, or frivolous. He played the dizzy sidekick for laughs. informal

Did you know?

  • For most of its life 'dizzy' meant 'foolish', not 'spinning': the Old English 'dysig' described a stupid person, and only later drifted toward the whirling head we mean today.

Word origin

From Old English 'dysig' ('foolish, stupid'); the sense shifted over centuries from 'silly' to 'giddy' to the modern 'lightheaded and spinning'.

Remember it
