POWER

noun · 2 syllables · /'paʊ.ər/

POWER

What does "POWER" mean?

The ability to do something or to control people, events, or outcomes.

Meanings

  1. The capacity or authority to act, direct, or influence others. The committee held the power to overturn the ruling.
  2. Physical energy or force that does work, especially electricity. The storm knocked out power to half the city.
  3. In physics, the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts. The engine delivers more power at higher revolutions. technical
  4. The result of multiplying a number by itself a given number of times. Two to the third power is eight. technical
  5. To supply with energy or drive the operation of something. Solar panels power the whole house in summer.

Did you know?

  • The unit of power, the watt, honors James Watt - but it only became an official SI base-derived unit in 1960, nearly 140 years after his death.

Word origin

From Old French 'poeir' (to be able), from Vulgar Latin 'potere', a reshaping of Latin 'posse' (to be able); the noun came to mean 'ability' then 'authority'.

Remember it
