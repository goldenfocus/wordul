CURVY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'kɜːr.vi/

CURVY

What does "CURVY" mean?

Having many curves; of a body, having a shapely, full figure.

Meanings

  1. Marked by smooth bends or curves rather than straight lines. The car hugged the curvy mountain pass.
  2. Of a person, having a full, shapely figure. The brand designs denim for curvy figures. informal

Word origin

From 'curve' (Latin 'curvus', bent) plus the adjectival suffix '-y'; an informal coinage of the 20th century.

Remember it

CURVY is CURVE with a Y - swap the straight E for a forked Y and even the spelling bends.
