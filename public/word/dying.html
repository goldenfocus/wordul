DYING

adjective · 2 syllables · /'daɪ.ɪŋ/

DYING

What does "DYING" mean?

Ceasing to live; approaching the end of life or existence.

Meanings

  1. On the point of death; failing. She held the dying candle close to read the last page.
  2. Present participle of die: ceasing to live or function. The signal kept dying every time the train entered a tunnel.
  3. Wanting something intensely. I'm dying for a cup of coffee. informal
  4. The process or moment of death. Hospice workers ease the dying of those in their care. formal

Word origin

Present participle of 'die', from Old Norse 'deyja' (to die), related to Old English 'diegan'; English kept the verb but mostly borrowed the noun 'death' from native roots.

Remember it

DYING is DYE + ING: the color drains, the cloth fades.
