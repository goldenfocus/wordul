SOLVE

verb · 1 syllable · /sɒlv/

SOLVE

What does "SOLVE" mean?

To find the answer to a problem or explanation for something.

Meanings

  1. To find an answer to or way of dealing with a problem or difficulty. The detectives finally solved the case.
  2. To find the value or values that satisfy a mathematical equation or problem. She solved the equation for x. technical

Did you know?

  • To 'solve' literally means to 'untie': it comes from Latin 'solvere' (to loosen), the same root behind 'dissolve', 'absolve', and 'resolve' - every problem imagined as a knot.

Word origin

From Latin 'solvere', 'to loosen, untie, release'; the idea of solving a problem is literally 'untying' a knot, which is also the root of 'dissolve' and 'absolve'.

Remember it

To SOLVE is to loosen a knot — its Latin root 'solvere' lives on in dis-SOLVE and ab-SOLVE.

A little poem

The tangled string yields not to force but thought-
you loosen one end, and the whole comes taut.

couplet

Wordplay

  • Why did the rope make a great detective? It knew that to solve anything, you just untie the knot.

What it teaches

Most problems are knots, not walls: you don't break through, you find the loose end and pull.
