OWING

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈoʊ.ɪŋ/

OWING

What does "OWING" mean?

Yet to be paid; still owed.

Meanings

  1. Remaining to be paid or due as a debt. There is still a balance owing on the account.
  2. Because of; as a result of, in the phrase 'owing to'. The match was cancelled owing to heavy rain.

Word origin

The present participle of 'owe', from Old English 'agan' meaning 'to own' or 'to have'; debt began as something you 'had' on another's behalf.

Remember it

OWING is 'OWE' plus '-ing' - if it's owing, someone still has to pay.
