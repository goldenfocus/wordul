DATUM

noun · 2 syllables · /'deɪ.təm/

DATUM

What does "DATUM" mean?

A single piece of information; the grammatically singular form of 'data'.

Meanings

  1. An individual item of information or a single fact. Each datum was logged with a precise timestamp. formal
  2. A fixed reference point, line, or surface from which measurements are taken. Elevations on the chart are given relative to a tidal datum. technical

Did you know?

  • A single fact is, strictly, a 'datum': the everyday word 'data' is its Latin plural, and both come from 'dare', to give, so data is literally 'the things that have been given'.

Word origin

From Latin 'datum', meaning 'something given', the neuter past participle of 'dare', to give; its Latin plural is 'data'.

Remember it

DATUM = DATA with the final A swapped for UM; one item, singular, so it ends in the lonely 'um'.
