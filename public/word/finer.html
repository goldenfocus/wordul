FINER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'faɪ.nər/

FINER

What does "FINER" mean?

The comparative form of 'fine': of higher quality, thinner, or more refined.

Meanings

  1. Of better quality or more excellent than something else. This is a finer wine than the one we had last night.
  2. Thinner, sharper, or composed of smaller particles. Sift the flour through a finer mesh for a smoother batter.
  3. More subtle or delicate in distinction. Only an expert can tell the finer differences between the two pressings.

Word origin

Comparative of 'fine', from Old French 'fin' ('perfected, of high quality'), from Latin 'finis' ('end, limit'), with the sense of something brought to its finish or peak.
