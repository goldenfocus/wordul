COMIC

adjective · 2 syllables · /'kɒm.ɪk/

COMIC

What does "COMIC" mean?

Causing or relating to laughter and amusement.

Meanings

  1. Funny; intended to or tending to cause laughter. The play's comic timing kept the whole room laughing.
  2. A comedian; a performer who tells jokes. The young comic killed during his five-minute set.
  3. A magazine or strip telling a story through sequential drawings. He spent his allowance on a stack of old comics.

Did you know?

  • 'Comic' traces back to the Greek 'komos' - a wild, wine-fueled revel for the god Dionysus - which is also the buried root of 'comedy.'

Word origin

From Greek 'komikos' (relating to comedy), from 'komos' (a revel or merrymaking), via Latin 'comicus' into English.

COMIC starts like 'come in' - the invitation to a laugh; and it shares a root with 'comedy.'
