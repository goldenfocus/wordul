BOWEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'baʊ.əl/

BOWEL

What does "BOWEL" mean?

The intestine, especially the lower part where waste is processed.

Meanings

  1. The part of the digestive tract below the stomach; the intestine, often used in the plural. The doctor checked his bowel for any sign of inflammation.
  2. The innermost or deepest part of something (chiefly in the plural, 'bowels'). The boiler room sat in the bowels of the ship. figurative

Did you know?

  • 'Bowel' literally means 'little sausage' in Latin (botellus) - the same word that gives us 'botulism', the disease first traced to bad sausages.

Word origin

From Old French 'boel', from Latin 'botellus' meaning 'little sausage', a diminutive of 'botulus' ('sausage') - the intestine being likened to a sausage casing.
