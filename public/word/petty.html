PETTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'pɛt.i/

PETTY

What does "PETTY" mean?

Concerned with trivial matters, or mean-spirited over small things.

Meanings

  1. Of little importance; trivial or minor. Let's not waste the meeting on petty details.
  2. Spiteful or small-minded about insignificant things. It was petty of him to bring up a mistake she made years ago.
  3. Of low or subordinate rank, as in 'petty officer'. He was promoted to petty officer in his second year. formal

Did you know?

  • 'Petty' is just the French word 'petit' (small) wearing an English coat: a 'petty officer' is literally a 'small officer', and 'petty cash' is simply 'small cash'.

Word origin

From Old French 'petit' (small), anglicized in spelling to reflect its English pronunciation; the same root gives 'petite' and 'petit four'.

Remember it

PETTY hoards two T's the way a petty person hoards small grievances - twice as many as it needs.

A little poem

couplet

Wordplay

  • I gave the small officer the smallest budget I had. He took it personally - which tells you he was petty in both senses.

What it teaches

Smallness of subject is forgivable; smallness of spirit is the choice that makes you petty.
