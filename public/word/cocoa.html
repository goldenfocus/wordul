COCOA

noun · 2 syllables · /'koʊ.koʊ/

COCOA

What does "COCOA" mean?

A powder made from roasted, ground cacao seeds, or a hot drink made from it.

Meanings

  1. The brown powder produced from roasted and ground cacao beans, used to make chocolate. The brownie recipe needs half a cup of unsweetened cocoa.
  2. A hot drink made by mixing cocoa powder with milk or water and sugar. We came in from the snow and made cocoa.

Did you know?

  • 'Cocoa' is essentially a typo that stuck: the original word was 'cacao,' but it got tangled with 'coco' (the coconut) and the misspelling became standard English.

Word origin

An alteration of 'cacao,' from Spanish, ultimately from the Nahuatl 'cacahuatl'; the spelling 'cocoa' arose from confusion with the unrelated 'coco' (coconut).
