STYLE

noun · 1 syllable · /staɪl/

STYLE

What does "STYLE" mean?

A distinctive manner of doing, expressing, or presenting something.

Meanings

  1. A particular way in which something is done, made, or expressed. Her writing style is spare and precise.
  2. Elegance and sophistication in appearance or behavior. He handled the loss with real style.
  3. A fashionable mode of dress or design at a given time. Wide lapels are back in style this year.
  4. The slender stalk of a flower's pistil, connecting the stigma to the ovary. Pollen travels down the style to reach the ovary. technical
  5. To design, arrange, or name in a particular way. She styled the model's hair for the shoot.

Did you know?

  • Style began as a physical object: the Latin 'stilus' was the pointed tool used to scratch words into wax. From 'how you held the pen' came 'your manner of writing', and finally 'your flair in everything'.

Word origin

From Latin 'stylus', a pointed instrument for writing on wax tablets; the meaning shifted from the writing tool to the manner of writing, and then to manner in general.

Remember it

STYLE holds 'sty' + 'le' - but think 'stylus': the pen-point root of how you do things with flair.
