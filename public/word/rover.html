ROVER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈroʊ.vər/

ROVER

What does "ROVER" mean?

A person who wanders from place to place; also a vehicle for exploring terrain, especially on another planet.

Meanings

  1. A wanderer; one who roams without a fixed destination. A restless rover, he never stayed in one town a season.
  2. A wheeled robotic vehicle used to explore a planet or moon's surface. The rover sent back its first images of the crater floor. technical
  3. A common name for a pet dog. She whistled and Rover came bounding across the yard. informal

Did you know?

  • The word 'rover' once meant a sea-roving pirate, but NASA gave it new life: the Perseverance rover touched down in Mars's Jezero Crater on February 18, 2021, to wander a world no human has stood on.

Word origin

From Middle English 'roven' (to wander), possibly influenced by Middle Dutch 'rover' (robber, pirate) and the verb 'rove'.
