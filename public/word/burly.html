BURLY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'bɜːr.li/

BURLY

What does "BURLY" mean?

Large, strong, and heavily built.

Meanings

  1. Stout and muscular in build; physically powerful. A burly dockworker hoisted the crate onto his shoulder without a grunt.

Word origin

From Middle English 'borlich' (stately, excellent), likely from an unrecorded Old English root meaning 'fit for the bower or hall'; the sense narrowed over time to 'large and sturdy of body'.

Remember it

BURLY sounds like a 'burly' bear that's a bit 'surly' - both big and gruff.
