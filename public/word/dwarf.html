noun · 1 syllable · /dwɔːrf/

DWARF

What does "DWARF" mean?

A mythical short, stocky humanoid; also a small star or anything unusually small for its kind.

Meanings

  1. A small, often skilled and underground-dwelling humanoid of folklore and fantasy. In the legend, a dwarf forged the king's enchanted sword.
  2. A star of relatively small size and low luminosity, such as the Sun. Our Sun is a yellow dwarf about halfway through its life. technical
  3. A plant or animal much smaller than the usual size for its kind. The garden was lined with dwarf apple trees.
  4. To make something seem small by comparison; to tower over. The new skyscraper dwarfs every building around it.

Did you know?

  • Our Sun is technically a dwarf: astronomers classify it as a yellow dwarf, an ordinary main-sequence star rather than a giant.

Word origin

From Old English 'dweorg', a mythical small being, from a Proto-Germanic root shared with Old Norse 'dvergr'; the astronomical and 'tiny variety' senses are later extensions.
