MEANT

verb · 1 syllable · /mɛnt/

MEANT

What does "MEANT" mean?

The past tense and past participle of 'mean': to have intended or signified.

Meanings

  1. Intended to convey or express something. I meant it as a compliment.
  2. Signified or had as a meaning. Back then a handshake meant a binding deal.
  3. Was intended or destined for a particular purpose (often 'meant to' or 'meant for'). That seat was meant for the guest of honor.

Word origin

Past form of 'mean', from Old English 'mænan' (to intend, signify), related to Old High German 'meinen' (to have in mind).

Remember it

MEANT is MEAN with a T - the same word, just sent back in time to the past tense.

What it teaches
