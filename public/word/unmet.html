UNMET

adjective · 2 syllables · /ʌnˈmɛt/

UNMET

What does "UNMET" mean?

Not satisfied, fulfilled, or attained; (of a person) never encountered.

Meanings

  1. Not fulfilled or satisfied, as a need, demand, or standard. The report highlighted the unmet needs of rural patients.
  2. Not yet encountered or come face to face with. She wrote to an as-yet-unmet pen pal across the ocean.

Word origin

Formed in English from the prefix 'un-' ('not') plus 'met', the past participle of 'meet', from Old English 'metan' ('to find, encounter').

Remember it

UN-MET: the goal you have not 'met' yet — the bar untouched.
