CRUMP

verb · 1 syllable · /krʌmp/

CRUMP

What does "CRUMP" mean?

To make or be a dull, heavy, crunching or exploding sound.

Meanings

  1. To make a muffled thudding or crunching sound, as boots in snow or a distant shell. Their boots crumped through the frozen crust.
  2. A dull, heavy thud or the sound of an exploding shell. A crump sounded somewhere over the ridge.
  3. To perform krump, an energetic, expressive style of street dance (often spelled 'krump'). The dancers crumped in a tight circle on the sidewalk. informal

Word origin

Of imitative origin, echoing a dull crunching or thudding sound; related to the older English 'crump' meaning crooked or bent.

Remember it

CRUMP is CRUNCH plus a thumP - the word lands like the heavy, muffled sound it names.
