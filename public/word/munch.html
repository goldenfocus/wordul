MUNCH

verb · 1 syllable · /mʌntʃ/

MUNCH

What does "MUNCH" mean?

To chew food steadily and audibly, especially something crisp.

Meanings

  1. To eat with a steady, often noisy, chewing motion. The rabbits munched their way through the lettuce patch.
  2. A snack or something to eat; an act of snacking. Let's grab a quick munch before the film. informal

Word origin

From Middle English 'mocchen', probably imitative of the sound of chewing, perhaps influenced by Old French 'mangier', 'to eat'; an echoic word like 'crunch'.

Remember it

MUNCH rhymes with 'crunch' and 'lunch' - the perfect trio: you crunch your lunch when you munch.
