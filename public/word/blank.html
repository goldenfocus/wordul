BLANK

adjective · 1 syllable · /blæŋk/

BLANK

What does "BLANK" mean?

Bearing no marks, content, or expression; empty or vacant.

Meanings

  1. Not written or printed on; unmarked. He stared at the blank page for an hour.
  2. Showing no understanding, emotion, or expression. She met the question with a blank look.
  3. An empty space to be filled in, as on a form. Sign your name in the blank.
  4. A cartridge that contains powder but no bullet. The starter pistol was loaded with blanks.
  5. To prevent an opponent from scoring, or to fail to recall. I blanked on her name the second she walked in. informal

Word origin

From Old French 'blanc' meaning 'white', from a Frankish/Germanic root '*blank-' ('to shine, gleam') — so a 'blank' page is literally a 'white' one, white and unwritten.

Remember it

BLANK shares its root with French 'blanc' (white): a blank page is just a white one waiting for ink.
