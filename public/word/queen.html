QUEEN

What does "QUEEN" mean?

A female sovereign ruler, or the wife of a king.

Meanings

  1. A woman who reigns over a country in her own right. The queen addressed the nation on the eve of war.
  2. The wife or widow of a king. She became queen the day she married the king.
  3. The most powerful piece in chess, able to move any number of squares in a straight or diagonal line. He sacrificed his queen to force checkmate.
  4. The single reproductive female in a colony of bees, ants, or termites. The whole hive serves a single queen.
  5. A playing card ranking between the jack and the king. She drew the queen of hearts.

Did you know?

  • The chess queen used to be the board's weakest fighter, creeping one diagonal square at a time, until rule changes around the 1470s turned her into the most powerful piece in the game - a transformation sometimes called 'mad queen' chess.

Word origin

From Old English 'cwen' meaning 'woman, wife, queen', from a Germanic root related to Greek 'gyne' (woman) - the same ancient root behind 'gynecology'.
