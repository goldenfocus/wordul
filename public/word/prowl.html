PROWL

verb · 1 syllable · /praʊl/

PROWL

What does "PROWL" mean?

To move about stealthily, as if hunting or searching for prey.

Meanings

  1. To move quietly and secretly, as a predator does when hunting. The leopard prowled the edge of the clearing at dusk.
  2. To roam restlessly through a place, often looking for something. He prowled the kitchen at midnight hunting for leftovers.
  3. An act of moving about stealthily; a roving search. The cat was out on its nightly prowl.

Word origin

From Middle English 'prollen' ('to move about searching, to rove'), of uncertain origin; the modern spelling with -w- emerged later.
