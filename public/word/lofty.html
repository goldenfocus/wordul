LOFTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈlɒf.ti/

LOFTY

What does "LOFTY" mean?

Of imposing height; or noble and elevated in character, sometimes haughtily so.

Meanings

  1. Towering; of great height. Lofty pines lined the ridge above the valley.
  2. Elevated or noble in thought, aim, or feeling. She had lofty ideals about reforming the whole system.
  3. Haughtily superior; arrogant. He dismissed our suggestion with a lofty wave of the hand.

Word origin

From 'loft' (an upper room or sky), from Old Norse 'lopt' (air, sky, upper room), plus the suffix '-y'; the figurative 'elevated' senses grew from the literal idea of great height.

Remember it

LOFTY = like a LOFT, the high room up under the roof; both are about being up high.

A little poem

His aims were lofty as the mountain air-

Wordplay

  • His goals were so lofty he needed a ladder - and so was his apartment, technically a loft.

What it teaches

Aim high, but remember: the same height that ennobles can also make you look down on people.
