TWIRL

verb · 1 syllable · /twɜːrl/

TWIRL

What does "TWIRL" mean?

To spin or rotate quickly and lightly, often in a circle.

Meanings

  1. To turn or spin around rapidly, especially in a graceful or playful way. She twirled in her new dress to make the skirt flare out.
  2. To twist or curl something between the fingers. He twirled the ends of his moustache while he thought.
  3. A quick spinning movement or a curling flourish. The dancer ended the routine with a final twirl.

Word origin

Recorded from the late 1500s, probably a blend of 'twist' and 'whirl' or an alteration of dialectal 'tirl' (to turn); the exact origin is uncertain.
