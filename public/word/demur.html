DEMUR

verb · 2 syllables · /dɪˈmɜːr/

DEMUR

What does "DEMUR" mean?

To raise an objection or show reluctance, especially politely or mildly.

Meanings

  1. To express mild or hesitant disagreement; to object. When asked to lead the project, she demurred, citing her workload. formal
  2. The act of objecting or hesitating. He accepted the terms without demur. formal
  3. In law, to file a plea that even if the facts are true, they give no legal cause. The defense chose to demur rather than contest the evidence. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'demorer' 'to linger, delay', from Latin 'demorari' 'to delay', from 'de-' plus 'morari' 'to tarry'; the sense of hesitating shaded into mild objection.

Remember it

To DEMUR is to murmur a 'no' - a quiet, polite objection rather than a flat refusal.
