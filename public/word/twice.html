TWICE

adverb · 1 syllable · /twaɪs/

TWICE

What does "TWICE" mean?

On two occasions; two times.

Meanings

  1. Two times; on two separate occasions. I knocked twice before anyone came to the door.
  2. In double the amount or degree. This rope is twice as strong as the old one.

Word origin

From Middle English 'twies', from Old English 'twiga' (twice) with an added adverbial '-s'; ultimately from the same Germanic root as 'two'.

Remember it

TWICE keeps the 'tw-' of TWO and TWIN - all three start the same because all three count from two.
