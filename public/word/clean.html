CLEAN

adjective · 1 syllable · /kliːn/

CLEAN

What does "CLEAN" mean?

Free from dirt, marks, or impurities; not soiled.

Meanings

  1. Free from dirt, stains, or contamination. Put on a clean shirt before the interview.
  2. Morally pure, honest, or free from wrongdoing. His record is completely clean.
  3. Smooth, neat, and precise in form or execution. The diver entered the water with a clean line.
  4. To remove dirt or unwanted matter from something. I need to clean the kitchen before they arrive.
  5. Completely or entirely. The ball sailed clean over the fence. informal

Did you know?

  • In Olympic weightlifting the 'clean' is a named move - the explosive pull that brings the bar to the shoulders before the overhead jerk.

Word origin

From Old English 'clǣne' meaning clear, pure, or unencumbered, from a West Germanic root; related to German 'klein', which originally meant 'fine' or 'small' before drifting in meaning.
