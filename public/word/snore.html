SNORE

verb · 1 syllable · /snɔːr/

SNORE

What does "SNORE" mean?

To breathe with a harsh, rough noise while asleep.

Meanings

  1. To make a snorting or grunting sound while breathing during sleep. He snored so loudly that the neighbors complained.
  2. The act or sound of snoring. A rumbling snore drifted from the next room.

Word origin

Middle English 'snoren', of imitative origin echoing the rough breathing sound; related to 'snort' and Dutch 'snorken'.

Remember it

SNORE keeps SNORT's sound but trades the sharp 't' for a long, lazy 'e' — the noise of someone fast asleep.
