SCENE

noun · 1 syllable · /sin/

SCENE

What does "SCENE" mean?

A place where an event happens, or a single continuous section of a play or film.

Meanings

  1. The place where an action or event occurs. Detectives roped off the scene before dawn.
  2. A subdivision of a play or film, usually a single setting and time. The duel happens in the final scene of Act Five.
  3. A view or landscape as one takes it in. From the ridge the whole valley opened into a green scene.
  4. A public display of strong, usually angry, emotion. Please don't make a scene at the restaurant. informal
  5. A particular cultural milieu or sphere of activity. She knows everyone in the local jazz scene. informal

Did you know?

  • The English word 'scene' traces back to the Greek 'skene' - literally the changing tent behind a stage - and the same root gives us 'proscenium', the arch that frames a theatre stage.

Word origin

From Greek 'skene', the tent or hut behind the stage where actors changed; into Latin 'scaena' ('stage'), then French 'scene' and English.

Remember it

SCENE shares its opening with 'scenery' and 'scenic' - all the stage-set words start SC-E.
