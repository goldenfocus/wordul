RELIC

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈrɛlɪk/

RELIC

What does "RELIC" mean?

An object surviving from the past, especially one kept for historical or sacred value.

Meanings

  1. An object or custom surviving from an earlier time. The rotary phone is a charming relic of another era.
  2. A part of a holy person's body or belongings, kept and venerated after their death. Pilgrims traveled for weeks to see the saint's relic. religious
  3. Something or someone outdated and out of place in the present. He felt like a relic at the all-digital newsroom. figurative

Word origin

From Latin 'reliquiae' ('remains, remnants'), from 'relinquere' ('to leave behind'), from 're-' plus 'linquere' ('to leave'); entered English via Old French 'relique'.

Remember it

RELIC = what's LEFT (Latin 'relinquere', to leave behind) after time moves on.
