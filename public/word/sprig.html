SPRIG

noun · 1 syllable · /sprɪɡ/

SPRIG

What does "SPRIG" mean?

A small shoot or twig of a plant, often bearing leaves or flowers.

Meanings

  1. A small stem, shoot, or twig with leaves, flowers, or buds. She garnished the plate with a sprig of mint.
  2. A small headless nail or brad, used in glazing and framing. The framer tapped a few sprigs to hold the glass. technical
  3. To decorate fabric or ceramics with a small leaf or flower motif. The muslin was sprigged with tiny blue flowers. archaic

Word origin

From Middle English 'sprigge', a small shoot, probably of Low German or Scandinavian origin and related to words meaning a twig or spray.
