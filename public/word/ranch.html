RANCH

noun · 1 syllable · /ræntʃ/

RANCH

What does "RANCH" mean?

A large farm where cattle, sheep, or horses are raised on open grazing land.

Meanings

  1. A large establishment for raising livestock such as cattle or horses. The family ran a cattle ranch in the Texas hill country.
  2. A single-story house, typically long and low; a ranch house. They bought a 1960s ranch with a sprawling backyard.
  3. A creamy, herb-flavored salad dressing. She dunked the carrots in ranch and called it dinner. informal
  4. To run or work on a ranch raising livestock. He has ranched the same land for forty years.

Did you know?

  • Ranch dressing was invented in the 1950s by plumber Steve Henson at his Hidden Valley Ranch in California; the dressing was named after the ranch, and it has long been the best-selling salad dressing in the United States.

Word origin

From Mexican Spanish 'rancho', meaning a small farm or a group who eat together, from Spanish 'ranchear' (to lodge); entered American English through the Southwest.

Remember it

RANCH sounds like its Spanish parent 'rancho' - drop the O and you have the wide American spread.
