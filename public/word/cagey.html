CAGEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'keɪ.dʒi/

CAGEY

What does "CAGEY" mean?

Reluctant to give information; cautious, wary, or evasive.

Meanings

  1. Unwilling to share details; guarded and evasive in a careful way. He got cagey whenever anyone asked how much the deal was worth. informal

Word origin

American English, early 20th century, of uncertain origin; sometimes linked to the idea of being hard to trap (as if reluctant to enter a cage), though the connection is unconfirmed.

Remember it

Picture a bird that won't talk because it fears the CAGE - someone CAGEY keeps their answers locked in.

Wordplay

What it teaches
