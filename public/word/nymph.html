NYMPH

What does "NYMPH" mean?

A minor female nature deity of Greek myth, imagined as a beautiful maiden of woods, water, or mountains.

Meanings

  1. A minor female deity personifying a natural feature such as a spring, tree, or mountain in Greek and Roman mythology. The poets said a nymph lived in every stream that ran clear enough to see her.
  2. The immature, wingless stage of certain insects that resembles the adult, as in dragonflies or grasshoppers. The dragonfly nymph hunted underwater for a year before climbing a reed to molt. technical
  3. A beautiful young woman. He wrote of her as a nymph too lovely for the ordinary world. archaic

Did you know?

  • Biologists borrowed 'nymph' from myth for a sober purpose: it is the official name for the young of insects like dragonflies and grasshoppers, which hatch as smaller, wingless versions of the adult rather than as grubs.

Word origin

From Greek 'nymphe', meaning both 'bride' and 'nature spirit', via Latin 'nympha' and Old French 'nimphe' into English.
