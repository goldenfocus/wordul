FATTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'fæt.i/

FATTY

What does "FATTY" mean?

Containing or consisting of a large amount of fat.

Meanings

  1. Rich in or composed of fat. Salmon is a fatty fish high in omega-3s.
  2. Relating to or derived from fat, as in chemistry. Fatty acids are the building blocks of many lipids. technical
  3. An offensive informal term for an overweight person. Schoolyard taunts of 'fatty' left a lasting mark. informal

Did you know?

  • Some fatty acids are called 'essential' because your body literally cannot build them from scratch - omega-3 and omega-6 must come from your diet or you slowly run out.

Word origin

From 'fat', from Old English 'fætt', the past participle of an old verb meaning 'to cram or stuff', plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'.

Remember it

FATTY = FAT + TY: it just doubles down on 'fat' with a doubled T.
