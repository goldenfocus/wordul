CONDO

noun · 2 syllables · /'kɒn.doʊ/

CONDO

What does "CONDO" mean?

An individually owned unit in a building, with shared ownership of common areas.

Meanings

  1. A privately owned apartment in a complex where residents jointly own shared spaces. They sold the house and bought a condo near the beach. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Condo' shortens 'condominium' - Latin for 'joint ownership' - a term that first described territory ruled jointly by two nations before it ever meant a place to live.

Word origin

A shortening of 'condominium,' from Latin 'con-' (together) + 'dominium' (ownership, lordship); literally 'joint ownership.'

Remember it

A CONDO is ownership held 'con' (with) others - the Latin 'con-' means together, which is the whole idea of shared property.
