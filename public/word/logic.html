LOGIC

What does "LOGIC" mean?

The systematic study of valid reasoning and inference; sound or coherent reasoning itself.

Meanings

  1. The branch of philosophy and mathematics dealing with the principles of valid inference. She took a course in formal logic to sharpen her proofs.
  2. A particular system or chain of reasoning. I couldn't follow the logic of his argument.
  3. The principles governing the operations of a computer or circuit. The chip's logic handles millions of operations per second. technical

Did you know?

  • The syllogism Aristotle described over 2,300 years ago - 'all men are mortal, Socrates is a man, therefore Socrates is mortal' - remained the dominant model of logic in the West until the 19th century.

Word origin

From Greek 'logike (techne)', the art of reasoning, from 'logos' (word, reason, account), via Latin 'logica' and Old French into English.

Remember it

LOGIC shares its root with 'logos' (Greek for reason) - LOG-IC is the IC of reasoning.
