PLUMB

noun · 1 syllable · /plʌm/

PLUMB

What does "PLUMB" mean?

A weight on a line used to test verticality, or the act of measuring depth or truth.

Meanings

  1. A lead weight hung from a cord to find true vertical or to sound depth. The mason dropped a plumb to check the wall was straight.
  2. Exactly vertical or true. Make sure the post is plumb before you set it.
  3. Exactly, directly; or, informally, completely. The arrow landed plumb in the centre. informal
  4. To explore or understand something fully and deeply. Scientists are still plumbing the depths of the trench. figurative

Did you know?

  • A plumb line, a plumber, and the chemical symbol Pb for lead all trace to one Latin word, 'plumbum' - because each began with the soft heavy metal that pulls a string straight down.

Word origin

From Latin 'plumbum', meaning lead - the metal the weight was made of; the same root gives 'plumber' (originally a worker in lead) and the element symbol Pb.

Remember it

PLUMB hides the silent B of 'plumber' and 'bomb' - both heavy with lead; 'plumbum' is Latin for lead.

A little poem

A lead weight, hung, asks only one mute thing:
which way is down? And gravity replies
the single honest answer it can bring.

tercet

Wordplay

  • Why trust a plumb line? It always tells you the truth, straight down.

What it teaches

To find what is true, hang a weight and let it settle; honesty, like gravity, only ever points one way.
