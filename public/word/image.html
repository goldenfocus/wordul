noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɪm.ɪdʒ/

IMAGE

What does "IMAGE" mean?

A visual representation or mental picture of something.

Meanings

  1. A visible representation of a person or thing, as in a picture, photograph, or reflection. Her image stared back from the dark shop window.
  2. A mental picture or impression formed in the mind. I still carry an image of that beach at dawn.
  3. The general impression a person, company, or thing presents to the public. The brand spent millions polishing its image.
  4. A figure of speech, such as a metaphor or simile, that evokes a picture in the mind. The poem turns on a single image of melting ice. technical
  5. To form a representation of, especially by scanning or capturing. The scanner images the brain in thin cross-sections. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Image' and 'imitate' are kin: both trace to the Latin 'imago/imitari', the root idea that an image is fundamentally a copy of something else.

Word origin

From Latin 'imago' (a copy, likeness, statue, ghost), related to 'imitari' (to imitate); into Old French 'image' and then English around 1200.
