noun · 2 syllables · /ˈdɛl.tə/

DELTA

What does "DELTA" mean?

A triangular landform of sediment where a river splits and empties into a sea or lake.

Meanings

  1. The fan-shaped deposit of silt and channels at a river's mouth. The Nile delta spreads across the coast in a broad green fan.
  2. The fourth letter of the Greek alphabet, written as a triangle. She labeled the third vertex of the triangle with a delta.
  3. A difference or change in a quantity, especially in math, science, or finance. The report tracked the delta between forecast and actual sales. technical

Did you know?

  • The word for every river delta on Earth comes from a Greek letter: the historian Herodotus saw the Nile's triangular mouth and called it a 'delta' (Δ), and the shape kept the name worldwide.

Word origin

From Greek 'delta', the triangular fourth letter; Herodotus applied the name to the Nile's mouth because its shape resembled the letter, and the geographic sense spread from there.

Remember it

A capital Greek delta is a triangle - the same triangle a river spreads into as it meets the sea.
