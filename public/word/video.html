VIDEO

noun · 3 syllables · /ˈvɪdioʊ/

VIDEO

What does "VIDEO" mean?

The recording, reproducing, or broadcasting of moving visual images.

Meanings

  1. A recording of moving visual images, or the medium of such recordings. She uploaded a video of the concert.
  2. The visual component of a broadcast or recording, as opposed to audio. The audio is fine but the video keeps freezing. technical
  3. To record on video. He videoed the whole graduation ceremony.
  4. Relating to the recording or broadcasting of moving images. They set up a video call for the meeting.

Did you know?

  • 'Video' is simply Latin for 'I see' - it was coined in the 1930s as the seeing counterpart to 'audio', which is Latin for 'I hear'; the two words are a matched grammatical pair.

Word origin

From Latin 'video' meaning 'I see', the first-person singular of 'videre' ('to see'); coined in the 1930s on the model of 'audio' ('I hear'), to name the visual part of broadcast signals.

Remember it

VIDEO = Latin 'I see' (from 'videre'), the same root as 'vision' and 'evident' - what is evident is what you can see.
