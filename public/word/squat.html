SQUAT

verb · 1 syllable · /skwɒt/

SQUAT

What does "SQUAT" mean?

To crouch down low with the knees bent and heels close to the buttocks.

Meanings

  1. To lower the body by bending the knees, keeping the weight on the feet. He squatted to tie his shoe and study the cracked pavement.
  2. To occupy an empty building or land without legal right. Artists squatted in the abandoned factory for years.
  3. Short and thick; low and broad. A squat little teapot sat on the stove.
  4. A strength exercise of bending the knees and rising while bearing weight. Her squat max climbed by twenty pounds this month.

Word origin

From Old French 'esquatir', meaning to crush or flatten, from 'es-' (intensive) plus 'quatir' (to press down), from Latin 'coactare' (to constrain).
