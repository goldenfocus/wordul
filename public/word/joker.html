JOKER

noun · 2 syllables · /'dʒoʊ.kər/

JOKER

What does "JOKER" mean?

A person who jokes a lot, or a special extra card in a standard deck.

Meanings

  1. Someone who makes jokes or behaves playfully, sometimes to a fault. He's the office joker - always good for a laugh, rarely on time.
  2. An extra playing card, usually showing a jester, used as a wild card in many games. She drew the joker and used it to complete her run.
  3. A hidden clause or factor that can unexpectedly change a situation. The new tariff was the joker in the deal that nobody saw coming. figurative

Did you know?

  • The joker is the only playing card invented in America: it was added in the 1860s as a top trump card for the game euchre, then spread into decks worldwide.

Word origin

From 'joke' (from Latin 'jocus', a jest) plus '-er'; the card sense is an American addition from the 1860s, likely tied to the trump card of the game euchre.

Remember it

JOKER = JOKE + R: the one who tells the joke, and the card that's always in on it.
