PATTY

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈpæt.i/

PATTY

What does "PATTY" mean?

A small flat round cake of minced or chopped food, such as meat.

Meanings

  1. A small, flattened, rounded portion of food, especially minced meat shaped for cooking. She flipped the burger patty and waited for the sizzle.
  2. A small flat sweet, especially a chocolate-coated peppermint one. He kept peppermint patties in his coat pocket.
  3. In Caribbean cuisine, a folded pastry turnover filled with spiced meat. The bakery sold beef patties wrapped in flaky golden crust.

Word origin

From French 'paté' (a pie or pastry), itself from Old French 'pasté'; the spelling shifted to 'patty' in English by the early 1700s, influenced by 'pat'.
