RAZOR

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈreɪ.zər/

RAZOR

What does "RAZOR" mean?

A sharp-bladed instrument used to shave hair from the skin.

Meanings

  1. A tool with a keen blade for shaving or cutting hair. He nicked his chin with the razor while hurrying to work.
  2. A principle that strips away unnecessary assumptions in reasoning, as in Occam's razor. By Occam's razor, the simplest explanation that fits the facts is usually best. technical
  3. To cut, shave, or shape with or as if with a razor. The stylist razored the ends to give the cut more movement.

Did you know?

  • Philosophers call rules of thumb that trim away unneeded assumptions 'razors' - Occam's razor, named for the 14th-century friar William of Ockham, is the most famous, but Hanlon's and Hitchens's razors follow the same cutting metaphor.

Word origin

From Old French 'rasor', from 'raser' (to scrape or shave), from Latin 'radere' (to scrape); the philosophical sense uses 'razor' figuratively for a principle that cuts away the superfluous.
