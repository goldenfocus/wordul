SNIPE

noun · 1 syllable · /snaɪp/

SNIPE

What does "SNIPE" mean?

A long-billed wading bird, or to shoot at people from a hidden position.

Meanings

  1. A wading bird with a long straight bill and camouflaged plumage, found in marshes. A snipe burst from the reeds in a zigzag flight.
  2. To shoot at exposed individuals from a concealed or distant position. Enemy soldiers sniped at anyone who crossed the open square.
  3. To make sly, critical, or carping remarks about someone. Online commenters sniped at the new policy from every angle. figurative
  4. To win an online auction by placing a bid in the final seconds. He sniped the rare card with two seconds left. informal

Did you know?

  • A 'sniper' is named after a bird: British soldiers in 18th-century India who could hit the fast, zigzagging snipe were called snipers, and the term came to mean any expert concealed shooter.

Word origin

The bird name comes from Middle English 'snype', of Scandinavian origin; the verb 'snipe' (to shoot from hiding) and 'sniper' arose from the difficulty of shooting the fast, erratic-flying snipe bird.

Remember it

A SNIPE is hard to hit — so the hunter who could was a sniper. The bird gave the marksman his name.
