RERUN

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈriːrʌn/

RERUN

What does "RERUN" mean?

A repeat broadcast of a program, or to run or show something again.

Meanings

  1. A repeated showing of a television program or film. On rainy Sundays we watch old sitcom reruns.
  2. To show, broadcast, or perform something again. The channel reran the finale by popular demand.
  3. To execute a process, test, or race over again. Engineers had to rerun the experiment after the power dip.
  4. A tediously familiar repeat of a past situation. Their argument was a rerun of last year's fight. figurative

Word origin

A 20th-century compound of 're-' ('again') and 'run', from Old English 'rinnan' ('to flow, run'); popularized by the broadcast television era.

Remember it

RE + RUN: run the tape again. The double R even looks like a loop starting over.

What it teaches

We rewatch the familiar not to be surprised but to be safe; sometimes that is exactly what we need.
