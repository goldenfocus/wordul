HABIT

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈhæb.ɪt/

HABIT

What does "HABIT" mean?

A settled tendency or practice, especially one that is hard to give up.

Meanings

  1. An acquired behavior pattern followed regularly, often unconsciously. Checking his phone first thing in the morning had become a habit he couldn't shake.
  2. A distinctive long garment worn by members of a religious order. The nuns moved across the courtyard in their black habits.
  3. The characteristic mode of growth or form of a plant or crystal. The mineral has a cubic crystal habit. technical
  4. An addiction, especially to a drug. He spent a year fighting a heroin habit. informal

Did you know?

  • The popular '21 days to a habit' rule is a myth: a 2010 study by Phillippa Lally found it took a median of 66 days for a behavior to become automatic, ranging from 18 to 254 days.

Word origin

From Latin 'habitus' (condition, appearance, dress), from 'habere' (to have, hold); the clothing and behavior senses both descend from this idea of how one 'holds' oneself.

Remember it

A habit is hard 'to ABIT' - the abandon part is the hard part; the word literally has 'a bit' you do over and over.
