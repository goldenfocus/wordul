BLESS

verb · 1 syllable · /blɛs/

BLESS

What does "BLESS" mean?

To make holy, ask divine favor on, or wish good fortune to someone or something.

Meanings

  1. To consecrate or invoke divine favor upon someone or something. The priest blessed the congregation.
  2. To endow with good fortune, talent, or grace. She was blessed with a quick wit.
  3. To express good wishes or thanks toward someone. Bless you for remembering my birthday. informal

Did you know?

  • To 'bless' originally meant to hallow something with blood: the Old English word came from 'blōd' and described marking a pagan altar with sacrificial blood, long before it took on its gentle Christian sense.

Word origin

From Old English 'blētsian', earlier 'blōdisian', meaning 'to consecrate with blood' — rooted in 'blōd' (blood), referring to marking an altar with sacrificial blood; later reshaped by association with 'bliss' and used to translate Latin 'benedicere'.

Remember it

BLESS = B + LESS; a blessing often asks for LESS for yourself so others have more — and notice it almost spells 'bliss'.
