DETOX

noun · 2 syllables · /'diː.tɒks/

DETOX

What does "DETOX" mean?

A process or period of removing toxic or harmful substances from the body.

Meanings

  1. A treatment intended to rid the body of addictive or poisonous substances. He spent a week in detox before starting the recovery program.
  2. A period of avoiding unhealthy foods, habits, or influences to improve wellbeing. She went on a social-media detox for the whole of January. informal
  3. To undergo or carry out a process of removing harmful substances or habits. The clinic helps patients detox safely under medical supervision.

Did you know?

  • The 'tox' in detox comes from the Greek 'toxikon' - poison meant for arrows - so to detox is, by ancient roots, to draw out the arrow-venom.

Word origin

A 20th-century shortening of 'detoxification', from 'de-' (removal) plus 'toxic', ultimately from Greek 'toxikon', poison for arrows.

Remember it

DETOX = DE (remove) + TOX (toxins); you are taking the poison back out.
