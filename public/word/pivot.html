PIVOT

noun · 2 syllables · /'pɪv.ət/

PIVOT

What does "PIVOT" mean?

A central point or pin on which something turns, or the act of turning on it.

Meanings

  1. A fixed point or shaft on which a mechanism turns or oscillates. The compass needle spins freely on its tiny brass pivot.
  2. A person or thing on which something else depends or revolves. She became the pivot around which the whole campaign turned. figurative
  3. To turn as if on a central point. He pivoted on his heel and walked out.
  4. To change strategy or direction sharply, especially in business. The startup pivoted from selling games to selling the engine that ran them. informal

Word origin

From French 'pivot', a hinge-pin or pin on which something turns, of uncertain earlier origin; entered English in the 14th century.

Remember it

A PIVOT is a point you turn on - picture the 'i' standing upright as the pin and the word spinning around it.
