noun · 2 syllables · /'haɪ.mən/

HYMEN

What does "HYMEN" mean?

A thin membrane that partly covers the opening of the vagina.

Meanings

  1. A fold of mucous membrane at the external vaginal opening. Anatomy texts note that the hymen varies widely in shape and thickness. technical
  2. In Greek mythology, Hymen, the god of marriage and wedding feasts. The wedding hymn invoked Hymen to bless the union. archaic

Word origin

From Greek 'hymen' (a membrane or thin skin), traced to a Proto-Indo-European root meaning 'to bind, sew'; the modern anatomical sense was fixed by the anatomist Vesalius in the 1500s.

Remember it

HYMEN and HYMN sit just one letter apart - picture the thin 'membrane' of an N slipped between the M and the silence.
