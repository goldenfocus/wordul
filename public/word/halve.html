HALVE

verb · 1 syllable · /hæv/

HALVE

What does "HALVE" mean?

To divide something into two equal parts, or to reduce it by half.

Meanings

  1. To cut or separate into two equal portions. Halve the avocado and remove the pit.
  2. To reduce something to half its previous amount. The new engine halves the car's fuel consumption.
  3. In golf, to play a hole or match in the same number of strokes as one's opponent. They halved the final hole and the match ended all square. technical

Word origin

From Old English 'healfian', from 'healf' (half), of Germanic origin; the silent 'l' and the 'v' spelling mirror the noun 'half' becoming the verb 'halve'.

Remember it

HALVE is HALF with the F flipped to V and an E added - same word, just the verb wearing different shoes.
