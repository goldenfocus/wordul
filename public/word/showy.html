SHOWY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'ʃoʊ.i/

SHOWY

What does "SHOWY" mean?

Making a striking or ostentatious display, often more flashy than tasteful.

Meanings

  1. Brightly attractive or conspicuous in a way meant to be noticed. The garden was full of showy peonies in deep red and pink.
  2. Ostentatious; flashy to the point of being in poor taste. He drove a showy sports car he could not really afford.

Word origin

Formed in English from the noun 'show' plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'; first recorded in the early 18th century.

Remember it

SHOWY = 'show' + y: a thing so eager to SHOW off it added a letter to brag.
