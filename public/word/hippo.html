HIPPO

noun · 2 syllables · /'hɪp.oʊ/

HIPPO

What does "HIPPO" mean?

A large, mostly aquatic African mammal with a barrel-shaped body and enormous jaws; short for hippopotamus.

Meanings

  1. A massive semi-aquatic mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa, the hippopotamus. A pod of hippos surfaced in the muddy river just after dawn. informal

Did you know?

  • The hippo's pinkish 'blood sweat' isn't blood or sweat at all - it's an oily secretion that screens ultraviolet light and fights bacteria, a built-in sunscreen.
  • A hippo can't actually swim: too dense to float, it walks and bounds along the riverbed and surfaces by pushing off the bottom.

Word origin

Clipping of 'hippopotamus', from Greek 'hippopotamos', literally 'river horse', from 'hippos' (horse) + 'potamos' (river).

Remember it

HIPPO hides a HIP and a O - picture the round 'O' of a hippo's wide-open mouth.
