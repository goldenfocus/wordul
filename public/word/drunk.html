DRUNK

adjective · 1 syllable · /drʌŋk/

DRUNK

What does "DRUNK" mean?

Affected by alcohol to the point of losing control of one's faculties.

Meanings

  1. Intoxicated by alcohol so that physical or mental control is impaired. He was too drunk to find his own front door.
  2. Overwhelmed or exhilarated by a strong feeling. They were drunk on the thrill of the win. figurative
  3. Past participle of 'drink'. By midnight they had drunk every bottle in the house.
  4. A person who is habitually or currently intoxicated. A cheerful drunk was singing on the corner. informal

Word origin

Past participle of 'drink', from Old English 'druncen', the past participle of 'drincan'; the adjective and noun senses for intoxication grew from it.

Remember it

DRUNK = what you become after too much DRINK; just swap the I for a U and stumble.
