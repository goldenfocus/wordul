TOKEN

noun · 2 syllables · /'toʊ.kən/

TOKEN

What does "TOKEN" mean?

A thing serving as a visible sign or symbol of a fact, feeling, or value.

Meanings

  1. An object or gesture representing a feeling, fact, or quality. He gave her a ring as a token of his love.
  2. A small stamped disc or voucher used in place of money in machines or games. You buy tokens at the counter to play the arcade games.
  3. A unit of data or a digital asset, such as a security credential or a cryptocurrency unit. The app issues a fresh login token every time you sign in. technical
  4. Done as a minimal symbolic gesture rather than a real effort. They made only a token attempt to fix the problem.

Did you know?

  • 'Token' and 'teach' are distant cousins from the same Germanic root meaning 'to show' - a token shows a fact, and to teach is to show someone the truth of it.

Word origin

From Old English 'tācen', meaning 'sign, symbol, or evidence', from the same Germanic root as 'teach' - both involve showing or making something known.

Remember it

TOKEN = 'TOKE-N', a small sign you can hold; it 'tokens' (shows) something bigger than itself.
