PLATE

noun · 1 syllable · /pleɪt/

PLATE

What does "PLATE" mean?

A flat, usually round dish from which food is eaten or served.

Meanings

  1. A shallow dish for holding or serving food. He cleared his plate and asked for seconds.
  2. A thin flat sheet of metal, glass, or other rigid material. The ship's hull was riveted from steel plates.
  3. One of the rigid sections of Earth's crust that move slowly over the mantle. Two tectonic plates grind past each other along the fault. technical
  4. An illustration printed on special paper in a book. The atlas opened to a colour plate of the coastline. formal
  5. To coat a metal object with a thin layer of another metal. The ring was plated with gold.

Word origin

From Old French 'plate', a thin flat piece of metal, from Vulgar Latin 'plattus' (flat), from Greek 'platys' (broad, flat).

Remember it

PLATE shares Greek 'platys' (flat, broad) with 'plateau' and 'platypus' (flat-foot) - all things spread out flat.
