CLUNG

verb · 1 syllable · /klʌŋ/

CLUNG

What does "CLUNG" mean?

Past tense and past participle of 'cling,' to have held on tightly to something.

Meanings

  1. Held firmly to something, refusing to let go. The child clung to her mother's coat in the crowd.
  2. Stuck closely to a surface or remained close. Mist clung to the valley until late morning.
  3. Held stubbornly to a belief, hope, or habit. He clung to the idea that things would soon improve. figurative

Word origin

Past tense of 'cling,' from Old English 'clingan' (to shrink, stick together), of Germanic origin, following the same vowel-shift pattern as sing-sang-sung.

Remember it

CLUNG rhymes with SUNG and HUNG - all are 'u' past tenses of '-ing' verbs (cling, sing, hang).
