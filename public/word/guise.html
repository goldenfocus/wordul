GUISE

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡaɪz/

GUISE

What does "GUISE" mean?

An outward appearance or pretense, often one that conceals the true nature of something.

Meanings

  1. A false or assumed appearance used to deceive or present something. He entered the palace in the guise of a merchant.
  2. An external form or manner in which something is presented. The old tax returned under the guise of a service fee.

Did you know?

  • The '-wise' in 'likewise' and 'clockwise' is a cousin of 'guise' - both mean 'manner' - and 'disguise' is just 'guise' with a 'dis-' bolted to the front.

Word origin

From Old French 'guise' meaning manner or fashion, of Germanic origin and related to 'wise' as in 'likewise' and to the suffix '-wise'; 'disguise' adds the prefix 'dis-' to it.

Remember it

GUISE = 'guy's' disguise minus the 'dis' - it's the mask before you strip the prefix off.

A little poem

couplet

Wordplay

  • The con man wore a costume and a new name. Under that guise, he was hiding in plain disguise.

What it teaches

A guise asks one question of you: do you read the coat, or the body underneath it?
