THING

noun · 1 syllable · /θɪŋ/

THING

What does "THING" mean?

An object, idea, action, or matter whose name is unknown, unspecified, or general.

Meanings

  1. An inanimate material object. Pack only the things you really need.
  2. An unspecified action, event, idea, or matter. The strange thing is, nobody noticed.
  3. A current trend, fashion, or recognized practice. Cold plunges are a thing now. informal
  4. One's personal interest, talent, or preference. Late nights just aren't my thing. informal
  5. Personal belongings or possessions. Gather your things, we're leaving.

Did you know?

  • Before 'thing' meant any object, it meant a public assembly - which is why Iceland's parliament is still called the Althing, the 'all-thing.'

Word origin

From Old English 'þing', originally meaning a public assembly or legal case; the sense drifted from 'the matter discussed' to 'any matter' to 'any object'. Norse 'Thing' assemblies share the same root.

Remember it

THING is the word you reach for when the real name won't come - it points at everything and names nothing.
