ENTER

verb · 2 syllables · /ˈɛn.tər/

ENTER

What does "ENTER" mean?

To come or go into a place, group, or activity.

Meanings

  1. To move into or onto a place. She entered the room without knocking.
  2. To become a member of, or begin participation in, something. He entered the army at nineteen.
  3. To record or key in information. Enter your password and press the key.
  4. The key on a keyboard that confirms input or starts a new line. Type the command, then hit Enter. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'entrer', from Latin 'intrare' (to go in), from 'intra' (within), itself from 'inter' (between, among).

Remember it

ENTER starts and ends the same way 'in' and 'out' do not - it begins with EN (in) and you slip through the middle.
