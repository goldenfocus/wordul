DONOR

noun · 2 syllables · /'doʊ.nɚ/

DONOR

What does "DONOR" mean?

A person or thing that gives something, such as money, blood, or an organ.

Meanings

  1. A person who gives money or goods to a cause, organization, or person in need. An anonymous donor funded the new wing of the library.
  2. A person who gives blood, an organ, or tissue for use in another person's body. A single organ donor can save several lives. technical
  3. In chemistry, an atom or molecule that supplies a pair of electrons or a particle to another. The nitrogen acts as an electron-pair donor in the bond. technical

Did you know?

  • People with O-negative blood are called universal donors because their red cells carry no A, B, or Rh markers, so they can be transfused into a patient of any blood type.

Word origin

From Anglo-French 'donour' and Latin 'donator' ('giver'), from 'donare' ('to give'), from 'donum' ('gift'); the same root as 'donate' and 'pardon'.
