JUMPY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'dʒʌm.pi/

JUMPY

What does "JUMPY" mean?

Nervous and easily startled; on edge.

Meanings

  1. Anxious and likely to flinch or react sharply to small stimuli. Ever since the break-in, she's been jumpy at every creak of the floor.
  2. Moving in sudden, jerky, or irregular shifts. The old film was so jumpy it was hard to follow the action.

Word origin

Formed in English from the verb 'jump' (of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative of the sound of feet landing) plus the suffix '-y'.
