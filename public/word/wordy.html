WORDY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'wɝ.di/

WORDY

What does "WORDY" mean?

Using or containing too many words; verbose.

Meanings

  1. Expressed in or using more words than are necessary; long-winded. His emails were so wordy that no one ever reached the point at the bottom.

Word origin

From 'word' plus the adjective suffix '-y'; Old English had a corresponding 'wordig' (wordy, talkative).

Remember it

WORDY is just WORD plus a Y - a word that won't stop, asking 'whY use one when you can use ten?'

Wordplay

What it teaches
