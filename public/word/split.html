SPLIT

verb · 1 syllable · /splɪt/

SPLIT

What does "SPLIT" mean?

To break or divide something into separate parts.

Meanings

  1. To break or cut something into two or more parts, especially lengthwise. He split the log with a single clean swing.
  2. To divide or share something among people. Let's split the bill four ways.
  3. To end a relationship or to leave a place (informal). The band split after their second album. informal
  4. A division, crack, or rift between parts or people. A deep split formed within the committee.
  5. A gymnastic position with the legs extended fully in opposite directions. After months of stretching she finally did the splits.

Word origin

From Middle Dutch or Middle Low German 'splitten' (to split, cleave), entering English in the late 16th century, where it was first used of a ship breaking apart.

Remember it
