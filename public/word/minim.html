MINIM

noun · 2 syllables · /'mɪn.ɪm/

MINIM

What does "MINIM" mean?

A musical note equal to two beats (a half note); also a tiny unit of liquid measure.

Meanings

  1. A musical note with the time value of two beats, also called a half note. Hold the minim for two full counts. technical
  2. A unit of liquid volume equal to about one drop, or 1/60 of a fluid dram. The recipe called for ten minims of the tincture. technical
  3. A single short vertical stroke in handwriting, as in the letters i, n, m, and u. A row of minims can be hard to read in old script. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'minim' is a famous trap in old handwriting: written in blackletter, the strokes of m-i-n-i-m become a near-identical picket fence of vertical lines, so a reader can barely tell where one letter ends and the next begins.

Word origin

From Latin 'minimus' (smallest), the superlative of 'parvus' (small); the same root behind 'minimum' and 'minimal'.

Remember it

MINIM is the MINIMum: the smallest dose, the smallest drop, the smallest stroke - all from Latin for 'least'.
