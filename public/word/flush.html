FLUSH

verb · 1 syllable · /flʌʃ/

FLUSH

What does "FLUSH" mean?

To cleanse or empty something by a sudden rapid flow of water.

Meanings

  1. To clean or clear out by a quick rush of water. Remember to flush the toilet before you leave.
  2. To redden suddenly in the face from heat, exertion, or emotion. She flushed with pride when her name was read aloud.
  3. A hot reddening of the skin, especially of the cheeks. A feverish flush crept up his neck.
  4. In card games, a hand of cards all of the same suit. He won the pot with a flush of hearts.
  5. Level or even with an adjacent surface. Sand the trim until it sits flush with the wall. technical
  6. Having plenty of money on hand. After the bonus he was flush for the first time in months. informal

Word origin

Probably from Middle English 'flusshen' (to fly up suddenly, of birds), later blending with senses of 'flow' and 'flash'; the 'level/even' sense may relate to French 'fluche'.
