noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɡoʊ.næd/

GONAD

What does "GONAD" mean?

A primary reproductive organ that produces gametes, such as a testis or an ovary.

Meanings

  1. An organ - the testis in males, the ovary in females - that produces reproductive cells and sex hormones. In many fish the gonad changes function with the seasons. technical

Did you know?

  • Every human embryo starts with the same 'indifferent gonad' - an identical organ that only later becomes a testis or an ovary depending on which genes switch on.

Word origin

Coined in modern scientific Latin from Greek 'gonē', meaning 'seed' or 'generation', from the root 'gen-' (to produce) - the same root behind 'gene' and 'genesis'.

Remember it

GONAD shares the 'gon-' of 'generation' - it's the organ where new generations are seeded.
