CRIMP

verb · 1 syllable · /krɪmp/

CRIMP

What does "CRIMP" mean?

To press or pinch something into small regular folds, waves, or ridges.

Meanings

  1. To form into small ridges or waves, as with hair, fabric, or pastry. She crimped the edge of the pie crust with a fork.
  2. To squeeze or bend a piece of metal so it grips, as on a wire connector or shotgun shell. Crimp the terminal onto the bare wire before you plug it in. technical
  3. A fold, wave, or ridge made by crimping. Her hair still held the crimps from the night before.
  4. Something that limits or hampers; chiefly in 'put a crimp in'. The rain put a crimp in our weekend plans. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'gecrympan' (to curl), related to 'cramp'; the Germanic root carries the sense of bending or contracting into folds.

What it teaches
