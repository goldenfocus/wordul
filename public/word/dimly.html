DIMLY

adverb · 2 syllables · /'dɪm.li/

DIMLY

What does "DIMLY" mean?

In a way that is faint, weak, or hard to see or remember clearly.

Meanings

  1. With little light; faintly illuminated. A single candle lit the room dimly.
  2. In a faint or unclear way, as of perception or memory. He could dimly recall the song his grandmother used to hum. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'dimlice', from 'dim' (dark, obscure) plus the adverb-forming suffix '-ly'.

Remember it

DIMLY is DIM + LY - the light, or the memory, is turned low.
