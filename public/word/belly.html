BELLY

noun · 2 syllables · /'bɛl.i/

BELLY

What does "BELLY" mean?

The front part of the human body below the chest, containing the stomach and bowels.

Meanings

  1. The front of the human trunk below the ribs; the abdomen. He patted his full belly after the feast.
  2. The stomach as the organ of digestion or hunger. An empty belly makes a sharp tongue. informal
  3. The underside or rounded interior of an object, like a ship or aircraft. Cargo was loaded into the belly of the plane.
  4. To swell or bulge outward. The sails bellied in the freshening wind.

Word origin

From Old English 'belg' or 'bælig' meaning 'bag, purse, leather sack', from Proto-Germanic 'balgiz'; the sense shifted from 'bag' to the body part that holds food.

Remember it

BELLY holds a double-L like a full pouch with two folds; the old word meant 'bag', and your belly is the bag that holds your food.
