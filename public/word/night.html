noun · 1 syllable · /naɪt/

NIGHT

What does "NIGHT" mean?

The period of darkness between sunset and sunrise when one part of Earth faces away from the sun.

Meanings

  1. The dark hours from dusk to dawn. The desert turns bitterly cold the moment night falls.
  2. An evening set aside for a particular activity or event. Friday is trivia night at the pub down the road.
  3. A state of darkness, ignorance, or adversity. He spoke of the long night the country had finally come through. figurative

Did you know?

  • 'Night' is one of the most conserved words in the Indo-European family: Latin 'nox', Greek 'nyx', German 'Nacht', and Sanskrit 'nakti' all trace back to the same ancient root 'nokt-'.
"Do not go gentle into that good night."— Dylan Thomas

Word origin

From Old English 'niht', from Proto-Germanic 'nahts', from Proto-Indo-European 'nokt-', the same root behind Latin 'nox', Greek 'nyx', and Sanskrit 'nakti'.
