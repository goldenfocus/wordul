GOOFY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈɡuː.fi/

GOOFY

What does "GOOFY" mean?

Silly, ridiculous, or foolish in a harmless, endearing way.

Meanings

  1. Comically silly or foolish; given to clowning around. He pulled a goofy face to make the baby laugh. informal
  2. In surfing and snowboarding, riding with the right foot forward. She rides goofy, so her left foot trails on the board. technical

Did you know?

  • Disney's Goofy debuted in 1932 under the name 'Dippy Dawg' - he wasn't actually called Goofy on screen until several years later.

Word origin

From 'goof', early-1900s American slang for a foolish person (probably from dialectal English 'goff', a simpleton), plus the adjectival '-y'.

Remember it

GOOFY: a 'goof' wearing a wide 'OO' grin and a floppy 'Y' for legs.

Wordplay

  • Why does the surfer ride goofy? Because the regular way just felt too left-footed.

What it teaches

A little willing foolishness is the cheapest way to put another person at ease.
