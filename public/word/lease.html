LEASE

noun · 1 syllable · /liːs/

LEASE

What does "LEASE" mean?

A contract granting use of property for a set period in exchange for payment.

Meanings

  1. A legal agreement to rent land, a building, or equipment for a fixed term. They signed a two-year lease on the downtown apartment.
  2. To grant or take property under such an agreement. The company leases its delivery vans rather than buying them.
  3. A renewed or extended period of vitality, in the phrase 'a new lease on life'. The surgery gave her grandfather a new lease on life. figurative

Word origin

From Anglo-French 'les', from the Old French verb 'lesser' (to let, leave), from Latin 'laxare' meaning to loosen or release.

Remember it

LEASE contains EASE: a good lease should let you live with ease, for a fixed time.

What it teaches

Everything you call yours is really a lease: held for a term, paid for in time, and due back in the end.
