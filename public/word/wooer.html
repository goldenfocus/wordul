WOOER

noun · 2 syllables · /'wu.ɚ/

WOOER

What does "WOOER" mean?

A person who courts or seeks the affection of another, especially with a view to marriage.

Meanings

  1. One who woos; a suitor seeking someone's love or favour. She had a dozen wooers at the ball, and turned every one of them away. archaic
  2. One who seeks to win over or attract something abstract, such as votes, support, or fortune. He was a tireless wooer of investors, charming money out of every room he entered. figurative

Word origin

From the verb 'woo', from Old English 'wogian' (to court, make love to), of uncertain earlier origin, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Remember it

A WOOER says 'woo!' and adds an 'er' - he's the one doing the wooing.
