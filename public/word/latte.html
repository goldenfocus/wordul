LATTE

noun · 2 syllables · /'lɑː.teɪ/

LATTE

What does "LATTE" mean?

A coffee drink made with espresso and a large amount of steamed milk.

Meanings

  1. A drink of espresso topped with steamed, lightly foamed milk. She ordered an oat-milk latte with an extra shot.
  2. A flavored milk-based hot drink modeled on the coffee version. The cafe's pumpkin-spice latte appears the moment leaves start turning. informal

Did you know?

  • Order a 'latte' in Italy and you may get a glass of plain milk - in Italian the word just means 'milk', and the coffee is properly a 'caffe latte'.

Word origin

Shortened from Italian 'caffe latte', literally 'milk coffee', from Latin 'lac' (milk); 'latte' alone means simply 'milk' in Italian.

Remember it

LATTE = milk in Italian; the double-T pours like the two streams of espresso and steamed milk meeting.
