BOULE

noun · 1 syllable · /buːl/

BOULE

What does "BOULE" mean?

A single rounded mass, such as a synthetic crystal ingot, a round loaf of bread, or a ball in the game of boules.

Meanings

  1. A pear- or sausage-shaped single-crystal ingot grown synthetically, as of sapphire or silicon. The furnace produced a flawless sapphire boule for the laser lenses. technical
  2. A round, crusty loaf of French bread, roughly the shape of a ball. She tore open a warm boule and spread it with butter.
  3. A heavy metal ball used in the French game of boules (pétanque). He lofted his boule to knock his rival's away from the jack.
  4. In ancient Greece, a council of citizens that prepared business for the assembly. The boule of Athens numbered five hundred members. technical

Word origin

From French 'boule' meaning 'ball', from Latin 'bulla' ('bubble, round object'). The Greek council sense comes separately from Ancient Greek 'boulē' meaning 'will, counsel'.

Remember it

BOULE looks like 'bowl' with the letters tumbled - and almost everything it names is round like a bowl turned over.
