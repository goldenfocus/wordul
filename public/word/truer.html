TRUER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'truː.ər/

TRUER

What does "TRUER" mean?

The comparative form of 'true', meaning more accurate, faithful, or genuine.

Meanings

  1. More in accordance with fact or reality. No statement could be truer of how the day went.
  2. More loyal, faithful, or steadfast. You will never find a truer friend than her.
  3. More accurately aligned, level, or fitted (of a wheel, edge, or note). After re-tensioning the spokes the wheel ran truer. technical

Word origin

Comparative of 'true', from Old English 'triewe' / 'treowe' (faithful, trustworthy), from a root meaning steadfast and firm.

TRUE + R: add one letter and the truth gets one notch closer.
