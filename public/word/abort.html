ABORT

verb · 2 syllables · /ə'bɔːrt/

ABORT

What does "ABORT" mean?

To stop or cancel a process before it is completed.

Meanings

  1. To halt an operation, mission, or plan prematurely. Mission control made the call to abort the launch.
  2. In computing, to terminate a program or transaction before it finishes. If a download is corrupted, the script will abort and retry. technical
  3. To end a pregnancy before term, or for a pregnancy to end naturally. The vet said the mare had aborted the foal early. technical
  4. An instance of aborting, especially a halted mission or flight. The first attempt was an abort at the four-second mark. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'aboriri' ('to miscarry, disappear'), combining 'ab-' (away, amiss) and 'oriri' (to rise, be born) - literally 'to fail to come into being'.

Remember it

ABORT shares its Latin root 'oriri' (to be born) with 'origin' - to abort is to undo the rising before it begins.
