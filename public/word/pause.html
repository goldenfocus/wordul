PAUSE

noun · 1 syllable · /pɔːz/

PAUSE

What does "PAUSE" mean?

A temporary stop in action or speech.

Meanings

  1. A short, temporary interruption of activity, sound, or movement. There was a long pause before she answered.
  2. To stop an action or process for a short time before resuming. He paused the film to grab a drink.
  3. In music, a mark indicating a note or rest held longer than its written value, a fermata. The conductor held the final chord on a pause. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Pause' and 'menopause' share a root: the Greek 'pauein' meant to stop, and menopause is literally the stopping of monthly cycles.

Word origin

From Greek 'pausis' (a stopping, ceasing), from 'pauein' (to stop), via Latin 'pausa' and Old French 'pause'.
