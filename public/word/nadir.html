NADIR

noun · 2 syllables · /'neɪ.dɪər/

NADIR

What does "NADIR" mean?

The lowest point of something; in astronomy, the point directly below an observer.

Meanings

  1. The lowest or worst point of a situation, fortune, or career. Losing the championship was the nadir of a long, painful season.
  2. The point of the celestial sphere directly beneath an observer, opposite the zenith. The star crossed the meridian at its nadir, hidden beneath the Earth. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Nadir' comes from Arabic 'nazir', meaning 'opposite' - it is literally defined as the point opposite the zenith, the spot in the sky directly under your feet.

Word origin

From Arabic 'nazir (as-samt)', 'opposite (the zenith)', via Medieval Latin into Middle English in the 14th century.

Remember it

NADIR sounds like 'NAY, dear' - the lowest, most discouraging point, a flat refusal of hope.
