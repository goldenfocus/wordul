adjective · 3 syllables · /ˈeɪviən/

AVIAN

What does "AVIAN" mean?

Of or relating to birds.

Meanings

  1. Relating to or characteristic of birds. Avian flu spreads quickly through dense poultry flocks.
  2. A bird (chiefly in technical or literary use). The reserve protects dozens of rare avians. technical

Did you know?

  • The word 'aviation' is built from the same Latin bird-root as 'avian': early flight pioneers literally named the dream of human flight after birds.
  • Every 'avian' alive is a living dinosaur - birds are the sole surviving branch of the theropod dinosaurs, kin to Tyrannosaurus rex.

Word origin

From Latin 'avis' ('bird') plus the suffix '-an'; the same 'avis' root gives 'aviary', 'aviation', and 'aviator'.

Remember it

AVIAN holds 'AVIation' - both from Latin 'avis', bird. Aviation was the dream of joining the avians.
