MODEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'mɒd.əl/

MODEL

What does "MODEL" mean?

A representation, often simplified or scaled, of a person, object, system, or idea.

Meanings

  1. A small-scale or simplified replica of something larger or more complex. He spent the winter building a model of the Cutty Sark from matchsticks.
  2. A person employed to display clothing or to pose for artists and photographers. The model held the pose under the studio lights for nearly an hour.
  3. A description or set of equations that captures how a system behaves. Their climate model predicted the warming a decade in advance. technical
  4. A particular version or design of a manufactured product. The new model of the car gets twice the mileage of the old one.
  5. An excellent example worthy of imitation. She was a model of patience throughout the ordeal. figurative
  6. To shape, design, or base one thing on another. The new constitution was modeled on the American one.

Word origin

From Middle French 'modelle', from Italian 'modello', from Vulgar Latin 'modellus', a diminutive of Latin 'modus', meaning 'measure, manner'.

Remember it

A MODEL is a small MODE of the real thing - both share the Latin 'modus', measure.
