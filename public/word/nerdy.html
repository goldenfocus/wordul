NERDY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'nɜːr.di/

NERDY

What does "NERDY" mean?

Characteristic of a nerd; intensely studious, socially awkward, or obsessively into niche interests.

Meanings

  1. Resembling or befitting a nerd, especially in being bookish or enthusiastically devoted to specialized hobbies. He gave a charmingly nerdy speech about train timetables. informal

Did you know?

  • The earliest known printed 'nerd' is in Dr. Seuss's 'If I Ran the Zoo' (1950), where it names an imaginary creature - the modern meaning came afterward.

Word origin

From 'nerd', which first appeared in print in Dr. Seuss's 1950 book 'If I Ran the Zoo', plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

NERDY = NERD + Y; the 'RD' in the middle could stand for 'really dedicated'.
