FLICK

verb · 1 syllable · /flɪk/

FLICK

What does "FLICK" mean?

To make a quick, light, sudden movement with a finger, wrist, or small object.

Meanings

  1. To strike or move something with a sudden light blow or jerk. She flicked the crumb off the table with one finger.
  2. A quick, light blow or sudden sharp movement. With a flick of the wrist he sent the frisbee spinning.
  3. A motion picture; a film. We caught a late-night flick downtown. informal

Word origin

Of imitative origin in Middle English, echoing the sharp light sound of a quick blow; the cinema sense comes from the early flickering of projected film.

Remember it

FLICK ends in -ICK, the same sharp click your wrist makes when you do it.
