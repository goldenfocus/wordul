RENEW

verb · 2 syllables · /rɪˈnjuː/

RENEW

What does "RENEW" mean?

To make new or valid again; to resume after an interruption.

Meanings

  1. To extend the period of validity of a contract, license, or membership. She forgot to renew her passport before the trip.
  2. To restore to a fresh or original condition; to revive. A week by the sea renewed his energy.
  3. To begin or take up again after a pause. The two nations renewed talks after years of silence.
  4. To replace something worn out with a fresh supply. The pharmacy will renew your prescription monthly.

Word origin

A Middle English compound of 're-' ('again') and 'new', modeled on Latin 'renovare'; 'new' descends from Old English 'niwe'.

Remember it

RE + NEW: make it NEW again. The word says exactly what it does.
