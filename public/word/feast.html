FEAST

noun · 1 syllable · /fiːst/

FEAST

What does "FEAST" mean?

A large, elaborate meal, often marking a celebration or holiday.

Meanings

  1. A lavish meal shared in celebration. The harvest feast stretched late into the night.
  2. A religious festival or holy day. The feast of the patron saint draws pilgrims every year.
  3. Something giving great pleasure to the senses or mind. The exhibition was a feast for the eyes. figurative
  4. To eat and drink sumptuously, or to enjoy something fully. They feasted on roast lamb and fresh bread.

Did you know?

  • 'Feast', 'festival', 'festive', and Spanish 'fiesta' are all the same word in disguise, each grown from the Latin 'festus', meaning 'joyful'.

Word origin

From Latin 'festus' meaning 'joyful or festive', via Old French 'feste'; the same root branches into 'festival', 'festive', and (via a different path) 'fair'.
