ELOPE

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪ'loʊp/

ELOPE

What does "ELOPE" mean?

To run away secretly in order to get married, usually without family approval.

Meanings

  1. To run off in secret to marry, typically against the wishes of others. Rather than plan a big wedding, they eloped to a courthouse in another state.
  2. To run away, especially of a patient leaving care without permission (clinical use). Staff sounded the alarm when the resident eloped from the ward. technical

Word origin

From Anglo-Norman 'aloper' (to run away from a husband with a lover), likely related to Middle Dutch 'ontlopen' (to run away) and to the root of 'leap'.

Remember it

ELOPE hides 'LOPE' - lovers LOPE off together to wed in a hurry.
