FLIER

noun · 2 syllables · /'flaɪ.ər/

FLIER

What does "FLIER" mean?

A person or thing that flies, or a small printed handbill distributed to many people.

Meanings

  1. A small printed sheet of paper advertising or announcing something. They stapled fliers for the lost cat to every pole on the street.
  2. A person who travels by aircraft, especially frequently. As a frequent flier she knew every gate in the terminal.
  3. Something that flies, such as a bird, aviator, or fast-moving object. The young pilot proved a natural flier.
  4. A risky or speculative venture or bet. He took a flier on the startup with his bonus. informal

Word origin

From the verb 'fly' (Old English 'fleogan') plus the agent suffix '-er'; spelled both 'flier' and 'flyer', the latter now more common for the leaflet sense.

Remember it

A FLIER is a fly-er: someone or something that flies, with the 'y' relaxed into 'i'.
