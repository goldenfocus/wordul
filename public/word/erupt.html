ERUPT

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪˈrʌpt/

ERUPT

What does "ERUPT" mean?

To burst out suddenly and violently, as a volcano, sound, or feeling.

Meanings

  1. To eject lava, ash, or gases suddenly, of a volcano. The volcano erupted without warning at dawn.
  2. To break out suddenly and noisily, of feeling, sound, or action. The crowd erupted when the goal went in.
  3. To break through a surface, as a tooth or a rash. Her first molar erupted just before her first birthday. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'eruptus', past participle of 'erumpere' (to burst out), from 'e-' (out) plus 'rumpere' (to break), the same root behind 'rupture' and 'interrupt'.

Remember it

ERUPT shares its root with RUPTURE and INTERRUPT - all come from Latin 'rumpere', to break; an eruption breaks out, an interruption breaks in.
