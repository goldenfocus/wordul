BEZEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'bɛz.əl/

BEZEL

What does "BEZEL" mean?

The grooved rim that holds a gem, watch face, or screen in place.

Meanings

  1. The rim or groove of metal that secures a gemstone in a setting or a glass cover in a watch or instrument. The diamond sat snug inside its gold bezel.
  2. The frame surrounding the display of a phone, monitor, or television. The new phone has almost no bezel around the screen.
  3. A sloping cut edge on a chisel, tool, or cut gem. He honed the bezel of the blade to a fine angle. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'bisel' or 'besel', meaning a sloping edge, probably from a root related to 'bis' ('twice') or to the same family as 'bevel'.

Remember it

BEZEL = the rim that 'embezzles' your gem - it grips the stone so tightly nothing falls out.
