noun · 1 syllable · /biːtʃ/

BEECH

What does "BEECH" mean?

A tall, smooth-barked tree with oval leaves and small edible nuts, common in temperate forests.

Meanings

  1. A deciduous tree of the genus Fagus, with grey bark, glossy leaves, and triangular nuts called beechmast. An old copper beech shaded the whole corner of the churchyard.
  2. The pale, hard, close-grained wood of this tree, prized for furniture and tool handles. The chair frame was turned from solid beech.

Did you know?

  • 'Beech' and 'book' grew from the same root: early Germanic peoples scratched runes onto beechwood tablets, so the tree that held the writing lent its name to the writing itself.

Word origin

From Old English 'bece' / 'boc', from the same Proto-Germanic root '*boko'; because early runes and writing were cut into beech bark and boards, the word for the tree is thought to be the source of the word 'book'.

Remember it

BEECH the tree has an extra E and ends in -CH like the wood you carve; the seaside BEACH swaps in an A for the sand.
