SAFER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'seɪ.fər/

SAFER

What does "SAFER" mean?

The comparative form of safe: more protected from or less exposed to danger.

Meanings

  1. More free from harm or risk than something else. Taking the side road felt safer than the icy highway.

Word origin

Comparative of 'safe', from Old French 'sauf', from Latin 'salvus' meaning 'uninjured, whole, healthy'; plus the comparative suffix '-er'.

Remember it

SAFER = SAFE + R: add an R to raise the guardrail one notch.
