DIARY

noun · 3 syllables · /'daɪ.ə.ri/

DIARY

What does "DIARY" mean?

A book in which one records daily events, experiences, or thoughts.

Meanings

  1. A personal daily record of events, thoughts, and feelings. She kept a diary all through the war and never missed a night.
  2. A book or app for noting future appointments and engagements (chiefly British). Let me check my diary - I think Thursday is free.

Did you know?

  • Samuel Pepys kept his famous diary from 1660 to 1669 in shorthand cipher, giving history its closest eyewitness account of the 1666 Great Fire of London.

Word origin

From Latin 'diarium', a daily allowance or daily record, from 'dies' meaning 'day'.

Remember it

A DIARY records your DAY - both trace back to the Latin 'dies', meaning day.
