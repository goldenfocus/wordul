noun · 2 syllables · /'keɪ.pər/

CAPER

What does "CAPER" mean?

A playful leap, a lighthearted escapade, or a small pickled flower bud used in cooking.

Meanings

  1. An illicit or daring escapade, especially a heist or scheme treated with playful flair. The film follows three thieves planning an elaborate jewel caper. informal
  2. A playful skip or leap. The lambs cut a few capers across the meadow.
  3. To skip or dance about in a lively, playful way. The children capered around the bonfire.
  4. The small green pickled flower bud of the caper bush, used as a savory seasoning. He scattered capers over the smoked salmon.

Did you know?

  • When you 'cut a caper', you're invoking a goat: the leaping sense comes from Latin 'caper' (goat) through Italian, the same root that gives us Capricorn the goat of the zodiac.

Word origin

The 'leap' sense comes from Italian 'capriola' ('a leap, a goat's jump'), from Latin 'caper' ('goat'). The culinary 'caper' bud is a separate word, from Greek 'kapparis' via Latin 'capparis'.

Remember it

Two CAPERS on one bush: the goat-leap (a CAPER like a CAPRICORN goat) and the briny bud. Same spelling, different roots.
