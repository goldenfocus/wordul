FLOOR

noun · 1 syllable · /flɔːr/

FLOOR

What does "FLOOR" mean?

The lower surface of a room, on which one stands and walks.

Meanings

  1. The flat surface of a room that one walks on. The dog stretched out on the cool kitchen floor.
  2. A whole storey or level of a building. Her office is on the fourteenth floor.
  3. A lower limit below which a value may not fall. The contract set a price floor of ten dollars a unit. technical
  4. The part of an assembly where members debate and vote. The senator finally yielded the floor to her colleague.
  5. To knock someone down, or to astonish or overwhelm them. The news completely floored her. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'flor', meaning ground or pavement, from a Germanic and ultimately Indo-European root meaning a flat or level surface.

Remember it

FLOOR has the same double-O as 'door' - the floor meets the door at the threshold.
