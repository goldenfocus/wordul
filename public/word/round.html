ROUND

adjective · 1 syllable · /raʊnd/

ROUND

What does "ROUND" mean?

Shaped like or approximately like a circle or sphere.

Meanings

  1. Circular or spherical in shape. The full moon hung round and bright.
  2. A single stage, cycle, or session in a sequence. She advanced to the final round of the tournament.
  3. A serving of drinks bought for a group at once. It's my turn to buy the next round. informal
  4. On every side of; surrounding (chiefly British). They gathered round the fire.
  5. To make or become circular, or to pass around something. The ship rounded the cape at dawn.

Did you know?

  • 'Round' traces back to Latin 'rotundus', built on 'rota' (wheel) - the same ancient root that gives us 'rotate', 'rotor', and 'rotund'.

Word origin

From Old French 'roont' (round), from Latin 'rotundus' (round), from 'rota' (wheel).

Remember it

ROUND contains a little O in the middle - the circle it describes is sitting right inside the word.
