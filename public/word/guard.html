GUARD

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡɑːrd/

GUARD

What does "GUARD" mean?

A person who watches over and protects someone or something; also to protect or defend.

Meanings

  1. A person assigned to protect, watch, or control access. A guard stood at the entrance to the vault.
  2. To protect or defend from harm or escape. Two dogs guard the yard at night.
  3. A defensive position or stance, as in boxing or fencing. Keep your guard up until the bell rings.
  4. A playing position in basketball or American football. She plays point guard for the city team.
  5. A protective device or fitting. Always fit the blade guard before storing the saw.

Did you know?

  • 'Guard' and 'ward' are the same ancient word twice: Norman French turned the Germanic 'w' into 'gu', so English ended up with both forms of one root.

Word origin

From Old French 'garde', from a Frankish word related to 'warden' and 'ward'; the 'gu-' spelling shows the Germanic 'w' reshaped through French.

Remember it

GUARD = 'guards U And R Door' - it stands between you and the way in.
