MINUS

preposition · 2 syllables · /'maɪ.nəs/

MINUS

What does "MINUS" mean?

Used to show subtraction or a negative quantity; reduced by, or without.

Meanings

  1. Reduced by a given amount; the operation of subtraction. Seven minus three is four.
  2. Lacking or without something. He returned from the trip minus his luggage. informal
  3. Negative; below zero or below a standard. The temperature dropped to minus ten.
  4. A disadvantage or drawback. The long commute is a definite minus. informal

Did you know?

  • The minus sign began as a merchant's mark: it first appeared in print in Johannes Widmann's 1489 arithmetic book, where '+' and '-' originally flagged surplus and shortfall in warehouse weights before becoming the operators of pure mathematics.

Word origin

From Latin 'minus' (less), the neuter comparative of 'parvus' (small); the same root behind 'minor', 'minimum', and 'minister'.

Remember it

MINUS makes things 'minus' - less. It shares its root with MINI and MINOR: all mean smaller.
