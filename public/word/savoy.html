noun · 2 syllables · /sə'vɔɪ/

SAVOY

What does "SAVOY" mean?

A variety of cabbage with crinkled, curly leaves, often used in cooking.

Meanings

  1. A hardy cabbage with wrinkled, deeply puckered leaves (savoy cabbage). She shredded the savoy and folded it into the soup.
  2. A historic region and former duchy in the western Alps, between France and Italy. The House of Savoy ruled the region for centuries before Italian unification.

Did you know?

  • The savoy cabbage is named after a real Alpine duchy: Savoy, whose ruling House of Savoy went on to supply the kings of a unified Italy from 1861 until 1946.

Word origin

From Savoy, the Alpine region (French 'Savoie', Latin 'Sapaudia'); the cabbage is named for the area where this crinkled variety was associated with cultivation.

Remember it

SAVOY ends in 'OY' - picture the crinkled cabbage and an Alpine duke saying 'oy, that's my region.'
