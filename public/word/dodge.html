verb · 1 syllable · /dɒdʒ/

DODGE

What does "DODGE" mean?

To move quickly aside to avoid being hit, caught, or noticed.

Meanings

  1. To avoid something by moving suddenly out of the way. He dodged the snowball and laughed.
  2. To evade a duty, question, or responsibility by cunning or trickery. The politician dodged every question about the budget.
  3. A sudden quick movement to one side, or a cunning trick to evade something. A clever dodge let him avoid the toll booth. informal

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, appearing in English in the 1560s with the sense 'to move to and fro'; the figurative meaning 'evade by trickery' developed by the 1600s.

Remember it

To DODGE, you DODGE left or right - picture the central D as you, ducking between two letters on each side.
