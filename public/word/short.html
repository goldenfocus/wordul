SHORT

adjective · 1 syllable · /ʃɔːrt/

SHORT

What does "SHORT" mean?

Measuring little in length, height, distance, or duration.

Meanings

  1. Of small length or height. He took the short route through the park.
  2. Lasting only a small amount of time. It was a short meeting, over in ten minutes.
  3. Not having enough of something; lacking. We came up short on rent that month.
  4. Curt or abrupt in manner. She was short with him after the long flight.
  5. To sell a borrowed asset hoping to buy it back cheaper; to bet against. The fund shorted the stock just before the crash. technical
  6. A short film, or a short circuit. The festival opened with a five-minute short.

Did you know?

  • A 'short circuit' is literally named for taking a shorter route: the electricity finds an unintended low-resistance shortcut that bypasses the device, which is why so much current floods through and things overheat.

Word origin

From Old English 'sceort' meaning short, from Proto-Germanic '*skurtaz' meaning cut off; it shares its root with 'shirt' and 'shear', all from the idea of being cut short.

Remember it

SHORT and SHIRT share four letters and one root meaning 'cut off' - a shirt was once just a short garment.
