ODDLY

adverb · 2 syllables · /'ɑd.li/

ODDLY

What does "ODDLY" mean?

In a strange or unusual way; surprisingly.

Meanings

  1. In a strange or peculiar manner. The machine was behaving oddly, humming when it should have been silent.
  2. Used to flag that what follows is unexpected, as in 'oddly enough'. Oddly, the quietest student gave the boldest answer.

Word origin

Formed from the adjective 'odd' plus the adverb-forming suffix '-ly'; 'odd' itself traces to Old Norse 'oddi', meaning a triangle or odd leftover point.

Remember it

ODDLY = ODD + LY; the suffix that turns 'quick' into 'quickly' turns the strange into a way of doing things.

What it teaches

We say 'oddly enough' to confess the world keeps a logic we never quite agreed to.
