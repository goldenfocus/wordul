FOCAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈfoʊ.kəl/

FOCAL

What does "FOCAL" mean?

Relating to or situated at a focus; central or pivotal.

Meanings

  1. Relating to the focus of a lens, mirror, or other optical system. A longer focal length compresses the background in a portrait. technical
  2. Forming the centre of interest or activity; central. The fireplace is the focal point of the whole room.

Did you know?

  • The 'focus' that 'focal' comes from is Latin for fireplace - Johannes Kepler borrowed the word for optics in 1604, picturing converging light rays like heat gathering at a hearth.

Word origin

From Latin 'focus' (hearth, fireplace), used in optics by Kepler for the point where rays converge, plus the adjective suffix '-al'.

Remember it

FOCAL = FOCus + AL; the focal point is where everything FOCuses.
