adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈlaʊ.zi/

LOUSY

What does "LOUSY" mean?

Very poor in quality; or, originally, infested with lice.

Meanings

  1. Of very bad quality; terrible. We got lousy weather the whole vacation. informal
  2. Feeling ill or unwell. I've felt lousy ever since I caught that cold. informal
  3. Abundantly supplied (often 'lousy with'). The beach town is lousy with tourists in August. informal
  4. Infested with lice. The crowded ship's quarters were filthy and lousy. archaic

Did you know?

  • When you call a meal 'lousy', you're literally calling it lice-ridden - the word started as 'infested with lice' before drifting into a general word for 'awful'.

Word origin

From 'louse' plus '-y', literally 'infested with lice'; the figurative sense of 'contemptible, bad' developed by extension, becoming the dominant modern meaning.

Remember it

LOUSY = full of LOUSE; something lousy is so bad it's crawling with problems.
