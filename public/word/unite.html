UNITE

verb · 2 syllables · /juːˈnaɪt/

UNITE

What does "UNITE" mean?

To come or bring together to form a single whole or to act as one.

Meanings

  1. To join two or more people or things together so they form a unit. The two companies decided to unite under a single brand.
  2. To join together in a shared purpose or cause. Workers across the country united to demand better pay.

Word origin

From Latin 'unire' ('to join, make one'), from 'unus' ('one'), reaching English via Late Latin 'unitus' and Old French in the 15th century.

Remember it

UNITE starts with U-N-I: 'U' and 'I' becoming one inside the same word.
