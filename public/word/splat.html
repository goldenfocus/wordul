SPLAT

noun · 1 syllable · /splæt/

SPLAT

What does "SPLAT" mean?

The sound or mark of something soft and wet striking a surface.

Meanings

  1. The dull, wet sound of a soft object hitting a surface, or the splash mark it leaves. The tomato hit the wall with a satisfying splat. informal
  2. To land or strike with such a sound; to splatter. The paintball splatted across his visor. informal
  3. The asterisk character ( * ), in programming and typesetting slang. Type 'rm star-dot-star' - the splat matches every file. technical

Word origin

An imitative (onomatopoeic) word coined to mimic the sound of a soft, wet impact, recorded from the early 20th century.
