EARLY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'ɜːr.li/

EARLY

What does "EARLY" mean?

Happening or done before the usual, expected, or appointed time.

Meanings

  1. Before the expected or usual time. We caught an early train to beat the crowds.
  2. Before the expected or usual time; near the beginning. He arrived early and waited in the empty lobby.
  3. Belonging to the first stage or period of something. Her early paintings are looser than the precise later work.

Word origin

From Old English 'aerlice', formed from 'aer' (before, soon) plus the adverbial '-lice'; 'aer' is also the ancestor of 'ere' and 'erst'.
