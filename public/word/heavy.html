HEAVY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'hev.i/

HEAVY

What does "HEAVY" mean?

Having great weight, or great force, intensity, or seriousness.

Meanings

  1. Of great weight; hard to lift or move. The suitcase was too heavy to carry alone.
  2. Great in amount, force, or intensity. Heavy rain flooded the streets.
  3. Serious, grave, or emotionally weighty. The conversation turned heavy. figurative
  4. Containing a large proportion of something, as heavy with sleep. Her eyes were heavy with fatigue.

Did you know?

  • The heavy elements in your body - the iron in your blood, the calcium in your bones - were forged inside dying stars long before the Sun was born.

Word origin

From Old English 'hefig' ('weighty, grievous'), from Proto-Germanic 'habigaz', literally 'having weight', from the same root as 'heave'.

Remember it

HEAVY is what you HEAVE - both come from the same Old English root for 'having weight'.
