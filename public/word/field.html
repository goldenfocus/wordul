FIELD

noun · 1 syllable · /fiːld/

FIELD

What does "FIELD" mean?

An open area of land, often used for crops, grazing, or play.

Meanings

  1. An expanse of open, often cultivated, land. The tractor crossed the field at dawn, turning the soil.
  2. A marked area where a sport is played. The striker raced the full length of the field.
  3. A branch of study, knowledge, or activity. She is a leading voice in the field of marine biology.
  4. A region of space where a force such as gravity or magnetism acts. The compass needle aligns with Earth's magnetic field. technical
  5. A space in a form or database that holds one item of data. Leave the middle-name field blank if you don't have one. technical
  6. To catch or stop a ball in play, or to handle questions. She fielded every reporter's question without missing a beat.

Did you know?

  • The same word for a farmer's open meadow became physics' word for the invisible web of force filling all space - Michael Faraday borrowed 'field' in the 1840s to picture how magnets pull without touching.

Word origin

From Old English 'feld' meaning open land or plain, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch 'veld' and German 'Feld'.
