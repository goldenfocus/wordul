SKATE

verb · 1 syllable · /skeɪt/

SKATE

What does "SKATE" mean?

To glide over ice or a surface on skates or a wheeled board.

Meanings

  1. To move along on ice skates or roller skates. They skated across the frozen pond at dusk.
  2. To ride a skateboard. He skates the rails behind the old library. informal
  3. To pass through something easily, or to avoid a difficulty. She skated through the exam without studying. figurative
  4. A boot fitted with a blade or wheels for gliding. He laced up his skates at the edge of the rink.
  5. A cartilaginous flatfish related to the rays, with broad winglike fins. The fishmonger laid a fresh skate on the ice.

Did you know?

  • The black leathery 'mermaid's purses' beachcombers find are the empty egg cases of skates and their shark relatives.

Word origin

The footwear sense comes from Dutch 'schaats' (borrowed singular, reanalyzed as plural 'skates'); the fish sense is a separate word from Old Norse 'skata'.

Remember it

SKATE = SK + ATE; picture a blade that 'ate' the ice as it glides, carving a trail behind it.
