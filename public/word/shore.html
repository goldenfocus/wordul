SHORE

What does "SHORE" mean?

The land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water.

Meanings

  1. The strip of land bordering a sea, lake, or wide river. They walked the shore until the lighthouse came into view.
  2. Land as opposed to water or the sea. The sailors were glad to be back on shore after the long voyage.
  3. To support or hold up something, typically with a prop (usually 'shore up'). They shored up the sagging roof with timber beams.
  4. To strengthen or reinforce something at risk of failing. The loan was meant to shore up the failing bank. figurative

Did you know?

  • The 'shore' of a beach and 'shoring up' a wall are unrelated twins: the coastline word and the propping word arrived in English from different sources and only later collided into the same spelling.

Word origin

The 'land by water' sense is from Middle English 'schore', probably from Old English or Middle Low German 'schore'; the verb 'shore up' (to prop) is a separate word from Middle Dutch 'schore', a prop or support.

Remember it

SHORE is where you're SURE of solid ground - both end in the same -ORE sound; the shore is the certain edge.
