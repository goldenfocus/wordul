FRONT

noun · 1 syllable · /frʌnt/

FRONT

What does "FRONT" mean?

The side or part of something that faces forward or is most prominent.

Meanings

  1. The forward-facing part or surface of something. A coat of paint brightened the front of the house.
  2. The line where opposing armies meet in battle. Letters from the front arrived weeks after they were written.
  3. The boundary between two masses of air of different temperatures. A cold front is expected to bring rain by evening. technical
  4. A deceptive outward appearance, or a cover for a secret activity. The laundromat was a front for the gang's money.
  5. To face toward, or to lead or represent publicly. She fronts a band and writes all its songs.

Did you know?

  • The 'cold front' on your weather app borrows its name from World War I: Norwegian meteorologists around 1918 saw colliding warm and cold air masses as battle lines and named them after the war's fighting fronts.

Word origin

From Latin 'frons' (genitive 'frontis') meaning forehead or brow, through Old French 'front'; the meteorological sense was coined during World War I by analogy with the military battle front.

Remember it

FRONT comes from the Latin for forehead - your face leads, your front goes first.
