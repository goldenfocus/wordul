BUDDY

noun · 2 syllables · /'bʌd.i/

BUDDY

What does "BUDDY" mean?

A close friend or companion; a pal.

Meanings

  1. A friend or comrade. He met his oldest buddy for a beer after work. informal
  2. A partner assigned to look out for you in a shared activity for safety. In scuba diving, you never leave your buddy underwater.
  3. A friendly or sometimes mock-friendly form of address to a stranger. Hey buddy, you dropped your wallet. informal
  4. To become friendly with someone (usually 'buddy up'). The new hires buddied up within the first week. informal

Word origin

American English, probably an alteration of 'brother', or a variant of British dialect 'butty' (companion); recorded from the early 19th century.

Remember it

BUDDY has a double-D in the middle - two friends standing side by side, shoulder to shoulder.
