SHIFT

verb · 1 syllable · /ʃɪft/

SHIFT

What does "SHIFT" mean?

To move or cause to move from one position, place, or direction to another.

Meanings

  1. To move or change position, often slightly. He shifted in his chair, unable to get comfortable.
  2. To change gear in a vehicle. She shifted into third as the road began to climb.
  3. A scheduled period of work. He picked up an extra night shift to cover the rent.
  4. A change or transition from one state, opinion, or trend to another. There has been a major shift in public opinion since the trial.
  5. A loose, straight, sleeveless dress; historically a woman's plain undergarment. She wore a simple linen shift to the garden party.

Did you know?

  • The 'Shift' key is named for a real movement: on early typewriters it physically lifted (shifted) the type bars or the carriage so each key could strike its capital letter instead of its lowercase one.

Word origin

From Old English 'sciftan' meaning to arrange, divide, or order, from Proto-Germanic '*skiftijan'; the sense of 'change position' developed in Middle English.

Remember it

SHIFT = SH + LIFT minus the L: pressing Shift 'lifts' your letters into capitals.
