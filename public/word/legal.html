LEGAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'li.gəl/

LEGAL

What does "LEGAL" mean?

Permitted by, conforming to, or relating to the law.

Meanings

  1. Allowed or required by law; lawful. It is perfectly legal to record a public meeting in this state.
  2. Relating to law, lawyers, or the courts. She joined the company's legal department after passing the bar.
  3. A document or matter pertaining to law; the legal aspects collectively. Marketing wants to launch Friday, but legal hasn't signed off yet. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Legal' and 'loyal' are doublets - both descend from the same Latin 'legalis', but 'loyal' arrived through French and drifted to mean faithful rather than lawful.

Word origin

From Latin 'legalis', 'pertaining to law', from 'lex' (genitive 'legis'), meaning law; entering English via Old French in the 15th century.

Remember it

LEGAL contains 'leg' - the law gives you a leg to stand on.
