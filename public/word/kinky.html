KINKY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'kɪŋ.ki/

KINKY

What does "KINKY" mean?

Having tight twists, curls, or bends; informally, unconventional in taste, especially sexually.

Meanings

  1. Full of tight twists, kinks, or curls. The garden hose was so kinky the water barely trickled out.
  2. Tightly and densely curled, as of hair. She loved the kinky texture of her natural curls.
  3. Unconventional or eccentric, especially in sexual taste. The film was praised for a plot that took some delightfully kinky turns. informal

Word origin

Formed in English from 'kink' (a twist or curl in rope, hair, or wire), probably from Dutch or Low German 'kinke' (a twist in a rope), plus the suffix '-y'.

Remember it

KINKY = KINK + Y: every kink is a tight bend, so a kinky line never runs straight.
