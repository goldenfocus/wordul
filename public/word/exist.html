EXIST

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪɡˈzɪst/

EXIST

What does "EXIST" mean?

To have actual being; to be real or present.

Meanings

  1. To be, to have objective reality or presence. Scientists debate whether life exists on other planets.
  2. To continue to live, especially under difficult conditions. For months they merely existed, scraping by on rations.
  3. To be present in a particular place or situation. A tension exists between the two departments.

Did you know?

  • 'Exist' comes from Latin 'exsistere', 'to step forth' or 'stand out' - so the original sense was less 'to be' and more 'to emerge into the open and become visible'.

Word origin

From Latin 'existere' (also 'exsistere') meaning 'to step out, stand forth, emerge', from 'ex-' (out) plus 'sistere' (to take a stand).

Remember it

EX-IST: to exist is to step EX (out) and IS - to simply be there.

What it teaches
