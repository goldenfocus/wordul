CLANG

verb · 1 syllable · /klæŋ/

CLANG

What does "CLANG" mean?

To make a loud, ringing metallic sound.

Meanings

  1. To produce a loud, resonant noise as of metal struck against metal. The iron gate clanged shut behind the prisoners.
  2. A loud, ringing metallic sound. Somewhere below, the clang of the blacksmith's hammer kept time.

Word origin

Imitative of the sound, reinforced by Latin 'clangere', 'to resound or clang', used of trumpets and the cries of birds.

Remember it

CLANG is CLAng + NG - the 'ng' is the metal still ringing after the strike.
