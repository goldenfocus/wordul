noun · 1 syllable · /tʃɛs/

CHESS

What does "CHESS" mean?

A two-player strategy board game played on a 64-square grid with sixteen pieces each.

Meanings

  1. The board game of strategy in which players move pieces to checkmate the opponent's king. They played chess by the window every Sunday afternoon.
  2. A situation of calculated, competing strategy, by metaphor. The negotiation became a slow game of chess between the two firms. figurative

Did you know?

  • Claude Shannon estimated in 1950 that the number of possible chess games is around 10^120 - far more than the roughly 10^80 atoms in the observable universe.

Word origin

From Old French 'esches', the plural of 'eschec' (check), from Persian 'shah' (king); the game's name traces back through the warning move 'check' to the word for 'king'.

Remember it

CHESS = the CHE of 'check' plus a double-S that hisses like two armies facing off.
