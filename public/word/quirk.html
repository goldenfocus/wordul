QUIRK

noun · 1 syllable · /kwɜːrk/

QUIRK

What does "QUIRK" mean?

A peculiar, distinctive habit or trait that sets a person or thing apart.

Meanings

  1. An odd or characteristic mannerism, habit, or feature. One of his quirks is alphabetizing the spice rack every Sunday.
  2. A strange chance or twist of circumstance. By a quirk of fate the two strangers shared the same birthday and name.
  3. A sharp groove or channel cut into a moulding, between mouldings. The carpenter ran a quirk along the edge of the cornice. technical

Word origin

Origin uncertain, recorded from the late 16th century, first meaning a verbal twist or subtle evasion before it came to mean an oddity of character.

Remember it

QUIRK sounds like a sudden 'jerk' of behavior - the little kink in someone's character.

Wordplay

What it teaches
