OPINE

verb · 2 syllables · /oʊˈpaɪn/

OPINE

What does "OPINE" mean?

To state or hold an opinion, usually on something debatable.

Meanings

  1. To express a view or judgment, especially without firm proof. The critic opined that the sequel had no business existing. formal

Word origin

From Latin 'opinari', to suppose or think, via Old French 'opiner'; the same root gives English 'opinion'.

Remember it

OPINE hides 'OPINion' minus its ending - to opine is to give an opinion out loud.

A little poem

He opined on the rain before a cloud had formed,
and called himself a prophet once the sky was stormed.

couplet

What it teaches

To opine costs nothing; to be right costs attention. Spend the second before the first.
