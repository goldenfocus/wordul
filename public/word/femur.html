FEMUR

noun · 2 syllables · /'fiː.mər/

FEMUR

What does "FEMUR" mean?

The thigh bone, the long bone of the upper leg between hip and knee.

Meanings

  1. The bone of the upper leg; the longest and strongest bone in the human body. He fractured his femur in the skiing accident. technical
  2. In insects and other arthropods, the third segment of the leg. The grasshopper's powerful femur drives its leap. technical

Did you know?

  • The femur is the longest and strongest bone you have - a healthy adult thigh bone can bear loads many times the body's own weight before it breaks.

Word origin

Directly from Latin 'femur', meaning 'thigh'; the word was adopted unchanged into anatomical Latin and from there into English.

Remember it

FEMUR sounds like 'fee-more' - the bone you'd pay more to keep, since it carries the whole body.
