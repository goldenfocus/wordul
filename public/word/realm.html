REALM

noun · 1 syllable · /relm/

REALM

What does "REALM" mean?

A kingdom or a domain of activity, interest, or knowledge.

Meanings

  1. A kingdom or the territory ruled by a monarch. The queen toured every corner of her realm. formal
  2. A field, sphere, or domain of activity or thought. That question belongs to the realm of philosophy, not science.

Word origin

From Old French 'reaume' (kingdom), ultimately from Latin 'regimen' (rule, government), influenced by 'regalis' (royal).

Remember it

REALM hides 'REAL' plus M - a realm is the REAL territory a Monarch rules.
