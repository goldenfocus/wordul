FAUNA

What does "FAUNA" mean?

The animals of a particular region, habitat, or geological period.

Meanings

  1. The collective animal life of a place or time. The island's fauna includes species found nowhere else on Earth.

Did you know?

  • 'Fauna' is named after a Roman nature goddess, and we owe its scientific use to Carl Linnaeus, who deliberately paired it with 'flora' so animals and plants would each have a matching collective name.

Word origin

From 'Fauna', a Roman goddess of nature and fertility and sister of the woodland god Faunus; the naturalist Carl Linnaeus borrowed the name in 1745 to pair with 'flora' for plant life.
