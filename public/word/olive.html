OLIVE

noun · 2 syllables · /'ɑ.lɪv/

OLIVE

What does "OLIVE" mean?

A small oval fruit of the olive tree, eaten or pressed for oil, and the tree that bears it.

Meanings

  1. The small bitter fruit of the Mediterranean olive tree, eaten cured or pressed for oil. She dropped two olives into the cocktail and saved one to eat.
  2. The evergreen tree, Olea europaea, that produces this fruit. Some olives in the grove were said to be over a thousand years old.
  3. A dull yellowish-green colour, named after the unripe fruit. He wore an olive jacket that blended into the brush.
  4. A branch of the olive tree as a symbol of peace, as in 'extend an olive branch'. After the argument she extended an olive branch and offered to talk. figurative

Did you know?

  • The word 'oil' is the olive's child: it descends from the Greek word for olive oil, because for the ancient Mediterranean world, olive oil simply was oil, with no other kind common enough to need its own name.

Word origin

From Latin 'oliva', borrowed from Greek 'elaia', the olive tree and its fruit; the same root gives us 'oil', since olive oil was the original 'oil'.

Remember it

OLIVE and OIL share their first two letters and their origin - the olive was the first oil.
