BATHE

verb · 1 syllable · /beɪð/

BATHE

What does "BATHE" mean?

To wash oneself or another in water, or to immerse something in liquid.

Meanings

  1. To wash the body by immersion in or with water. She bathed the baby in the warm, shallow tub.
  2. To swim or take a dip for pleasure, especially in the sea or a river. On hot afternoons they would bathe in the cool river below the bridge.
  3. To cover or suffuse something, as with light or warmth. The valley was bathed in the last gold of the setting sun. figurative
  4. To apply liquid gently to soothe or cleanse a wound or sore spot. The nurse bathed the cut with antiseptic before bandaging it.

Word origin

From Old English 'baþian', to wash by immersion, from the Proto-Germanic root that also gives 'bath'; the verb keeps the long vowel and voiced 'th' that the noun lost.

Remember it

BATHE = the noun BATH plus an E that stretches it into the long action: you take a BATH, but you BATHE in it.
