POLAR

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈpoʊ.lɚ/

POLAR

What does "POLAR" mean?

Relating to the North or South Pole, or to two directly opposite extremes.

Meanings

  1. Relating to or near the Earth's North or South Pole. Polar bears are perfectly suited to the frozen Arctic.
  2. Relating to a pole of a magnet, battery, or other system; having two opposite poles. Water is a polar molecule, with a positive and a negative end. technical
  3. Characterized by complete or extreme opposition. The two candidates held polar views on the issue. figurative

Did you know?

  • Polar bears are not actually white: their skin is black and their fur is made of hollow, transparent hairs that only scatter light to look white.

Word origin

From Medieval Latin 'polaris', from Latin 'polus' (the end of an axis, pole of the sky), from Greek 'polos' (pivot, axis).

What it teaches

Two extremes can share a single axis: opposites aren't unrelated, they're just the far ends of the same line.
