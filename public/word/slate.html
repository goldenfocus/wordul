SLATE

noun · 1 syllable · /sleɪt/

SLATE

What does "SLATE" mean?

A fine-grained grey rock that splits into thin sheets, used for roofing and writing.

Meanings

  1. A fine-grained metamorphic rock that cleaves into flat plates, traditionally used for roof tiles and writing tablets. The cottage had a roof of weathered grey slate.
  2. A writing tablet of slate, or by extension a record of debts or tasks. The pub let regulars run a tab on the slate.
  3. A list of candidates or items proposed for a particular purpose. The party announced its slate of candidates for the election.
  4. To schedule or plan something for a particular time. The launch is slated for early spring.
  5. To criticize harshly (chiefly British). Reviewers slated the play after opening night. informal

Did you know?

  • The idea of a 'clean slate' is literal: it comes from wiping a slate writing tablet bare so the day's debts or lessons vanished and you could begin again.

Word origin

From Old French 'esclate', a feminine form of 'esclat' (a splinter or fragment), reflecting how the rock splits into thin pieces.

Remember it

SLATE = S + LATE; if a roof tile breaks, your repair is always SLATE - and the launch you scheduled may run LATE.
