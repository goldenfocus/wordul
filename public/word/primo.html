PRIMO

adjective · 2 syllables · /'priː.moʊ/

PRIMO

What does "PRIMO" mean?

Of the highest quality; first-rate, excellent.

Meanings

  1. Top quality; the very best. These are primo seats, right behind home plate. informal
  2. In a piano duet, the upper or leading part, played by the player on the right. She took the primo while her teacher played the secondo. technical

Did you know?

  • In a piano duet the two players literally hold ranks: the higher part is the 'primo' (first) and the lower is the 'secondo' (second), straight from the Italian.

Word origin

From Italian and Spanish 'primo' (first), from Latin 'primus' (first); adopted into English slang in the 20th century as a term of high praise.

Remember it

PRIMO = 'prime' + O, the Italian 'first' - top of the line.

A little poem

Two hands climb high and call it primo;

Wordplay

  • My cousin keeps bragging his salsa is primo - in Spanish that just means he's calling himself 'cousin' and 'first-rate' at once.

What it teaches

Calling something 'first' is easy; earning the rank is the part that takes practice.
