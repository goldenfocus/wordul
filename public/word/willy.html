WILLY

noun · 2 syllables · /'wɪl.i/

WILLY

What does "WILLY" mean?

British informal and childish term for the penis.

Meanings

  1. A childish or jocular word for the penis, chiefly British. The toddler ran around the garden giggling about his willy. informal
  2. In the phrase 'the willies', a feeling of nervousness or unease. That abandoned house gives me the willies. informal

Word origin

A pet form of the name 'William', applied jocularly to the penis from the early 20th century; the unrelated phrase 'the willies' (nervous fear) appeared in late-19th-century American slang of uncertain origin.

Remember it

WILLY is a nickname for WILLIAM - the same way the slang sense is a nickname for something else.

Wordplay
