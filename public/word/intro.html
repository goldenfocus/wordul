INTRO

noun · 2 syllables · /'ɪn.troʊ/

INTRO

What does "INTRO" mean?

A short opening section that introduces something, such as a song, talk, or text.

Meanings

  1. An introduction; a brief preliminary part of a book, speech, or piece of music. The song's intro is just a single guitar before the whole band kicks in. informal
  2. An act of introducing one person to another. Thanks for the intro - she turned out to be a great contact. informal

Word origin

A clipped form of 'introduction', from Latin 'introducere' ('to lead in'), from 'intro-' (inward) + 'ducere' (to lead).

Remember it

INTRO is just INTROduction with the long tail lopped off - the word literally leads you IN.
