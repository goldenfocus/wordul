AFFIX

verb · 2 syllables · /əˈfɪks/

AFFIX

What does "AFFIX" mean?

To attach or fasten one thing to another, or a word-part added to a root.

Meanings

  1. To fasten, stick, or attach something to something else. Please affix a stamp to the envelope. formal
  2. To add in writing, such as a signature or seal. She affixed her name to the bottom of the contract. formal
  3. A letter or group of letters added to a word to change its meaning, such as a prefix or suffix. In 'unhappiness', 'un-' and '-ness' are both affixes. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'affixus', past participle of 'affigere' (to fasten to), from 'ad-' (to) plus 'figere' (to fix, fasten) - the same 'figere' that gives English 'fix' and 'fixture'.

Remember it

AFFIX is 'A' stuck onto 'FIX' - and that's exactly what an affix does: it sticks onto a word.
