SLICE

noun · 1 syllable · /slaɪs/

SLICE

What does "SLICE" mean?

A thin, broad piece cut from a larger object, especially food.

Meanings

  1. A thin flat portion cut from something larger. He had a single slice of toast for breakfast.
  2. A portion or share of something. Each investor wanted a bigger slice of the profits. figurative
  3. To cut into thin broad pieces, or to cut cleanly through. Slice the bread thinly for sandwiches.
  4. In golf and tennis, a stroke that sends the ball curving to one side. His drive ended in the trees thanks to a wicked slice. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'esclice' (a splinter, fragment), from the verb 'esclicier' (to splinter), of Germanic origin akin to 'slit' and 'split'.
