OVOID

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈoʊ.vɔɪd/

OVOID

What does "OVOID" mean?

Egg-shaped, as a three-dimensional solid.

Meanings

  1. Having the solid, rounded form of an egg. The sculpture was a smooth ovoid stone balanced on its narrow end.
  2. An egg-shaped object or solid. The artist carved a perfect ovoid from white marble.

Word origin

From Latin 'ovum' meaning egg plus the Greek-derived suffix '-oid' meaning 'resembling'; literally 'egg-like'.

Remember it

OVOID = 'OVum' + '-oid', literally 'egg-resembling'; the double O even looks like two eggs.
