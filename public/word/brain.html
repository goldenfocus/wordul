BRAIN

noun · 1 syllable · /breɪn/

BRAIN

What does "BRAIN" mean?

The organ inside the skull that controls thought, memory, emotion, and the body's actions.

Meanings

  1. The soft mass of nervous tissue in the skull that acts as the body's control center. A blow to the head can bruise the brain against the inside of the skull.
  2. Intelligence, cleverness, or mental capacity. It took real brains to wire the whole system in a single afternoon. informal
  3. The cleverest person in a group, or the one who plans a scheme. She was the brains behind the whole operation. informal
  4. To hit someone hard on the head. He threatened to brain me with a frying pan. informal

Did you know?

  • A 2009 cell count put the human brain at about 86 billion neurons - not the round 100 billion that textbooks repeated for decades.
  • Your brain is roughly 2% of your body weight but burns about 20% of your energy, making thinking one of the most expensive things your body does.

Word origin

From Old English 'brægen', related to Middle Low German 'bregen', from a Proto-Germanic root possibly tied to Greek 'bregma', the front of the head.
