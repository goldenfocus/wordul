INTER

verb · 2 syllables · /ɪn'tɜːr/

INTER

What does "INTER" mean?

To place a dead body in a grave or tomb; to bury with ceremony.

Meanings

  1. To deposit a corpse in the earth or a tomb, usually with funeral rites. The veterans were interred with full military honours at the national cemetery. formal

Word origin

From Latin 'in-' (into) + 'terra' (earth), via Old French 'enterrer', literally 'to put into the ground'.

Remember it

INTER hides 'in' + 'terra' (earth): to inter is to put someone IN the eaRth.
