FRAIL

adjective · 1 syllable · /freɪl/

FRAIL

What does "FRAIL" mean?

Physically weak, delicate, and easily broken or harmed.

Meanings

  1. Weak in body, especially through old age or illness. Her grandmother had grown frail, leaning on a cane to cross the room.
  2. Easily damaged or broken; flimsy in structure. The frail old bridge swayed under the weight of the truck.
  3. Morally weak; liable to give way to temptation. He blamed his frail resolve for breaking the diet. formal

Word origin

From Old French 'fraile', from Latin 'fragilis' meaning easily broken, from 'frangere' to break — the same root that gives English 'fragile'.
