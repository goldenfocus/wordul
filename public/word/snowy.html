SNOWY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈsnoʊ.i/

SNOWY

What does "SNOWY" mean?

Covered with snow, or full of falling snow.

Meanings

  1. Covered with, abounding in, or characterized by snow. They drove carefully along the snowy mountain road.
  2. Pure white, like snow. The old man stroked his snowy beard. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'snāwig', from 'snāw' (snow) plus the adjective-forming suffix '-ig' (-y), of common Germanic origin.

Remember it

SNOWY = SNOW + Y: just add a 'y' to the cold stuff and the whole landscape is buried.
