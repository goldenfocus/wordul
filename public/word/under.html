preposition · 2 syllables · /ˈʌn.dər/

UNDER

What does "UNDER" mean?

In or to a position below or beneath something.

Meanings

  1. Below or beneath something, in position. The cat was hiding under the bed during the storm.
  2. Less than a stated amount or age. Tickets are free for children under five.
  3. Subject to authority, control, or a condition. The whole project is under new management.
  4. In or to a lower place; beneath the surface. The swimmer went under and came up gasping.

Word origin

From Old English 'under', from a Proto-Germanic root; ultimately related to a Proto-Indo-European stem also behind Latin 'infra' (below) and Sanskrit 'adhah'.

Remember it

UNDER ends in 'der' and sits below: it is the opposite half of 'over', the two prepositions that map the whole vertical world.
