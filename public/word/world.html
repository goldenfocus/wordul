WORLD

noun · 1 syllable · /wɝld/

WORLD

What does "WORLD" mean?

The earth, together with all its countries, peoples, and natural features.

Meanings

  1. The earth and all the life and human activity upon it. News of the discovery spread around the world in a single day.
  2. A particular sphere, domain, or field of activity or experience. She moved easily in the world of high finance.
  3. A planet or celestial body considered as a place that could support life. The telescope found a distant world orbiting a dim red star.
  4. Human society and its concerns, especially as opposed to a spiritual or private life. After years in the monastery he found the world loud and bewildering.

Did you know?

  • WORLD literally meant 'the age of man': it began as a Germanic compound of 'wer' (man, as in werewolf) and a root for 'age', so the planet sense came much later than the human one.

Word origin

From Old English 'woruld', a Germanic compound of 'wer' (man) and a root meaning 'age'; its earliest sense was the age or life of man, not the physical planet.
