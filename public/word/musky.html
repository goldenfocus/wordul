MUSKY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'mʌs.ki/

MUSKY

What does "MUSKY" mean?

Having the strong, earthy, penetrating smell characteristic of musk.

Meanings

  1. Smelling of or resembling musk; heavy, warm, and animalic in scent. The old cellar had a musky, leathery smell that clung to his coat.

Did you know?

  • The root of 'musky' is the Sanskrit word for 'little scrotum' - named for the shape of the gland on the musk deer that produces the scent.

Word origin

From 'musk', via Late Latin 'muscus' and Greek 'moskhos', ultimately from Sanskrit 'muska', 'scrotum', for the gland-shaped pod of the male musk deer.

Remember it

MUSKY = MUSK + Y: the Y is the lingering trail of scent the word leaves behind.
