FLORA

noun · 2 syllables · /'flɔː.rə/

FLORA

What does "FLORA" mean?

The plants of a particular region, habitat, or period, considered as a group.

Meanings

  1. The plant life of a particular region, era, or environment. The island's flora includes dozens of species found nowhere else.
  2. The community of microorganisms living in or on the body, such as in the gut. Antibiotics can disrupt the gut flora for weeks. technical
  3. A descriptive scientific work cataloguing the plants of an area. He consulted the regional flora to identify the unknown fern. technical

Did you know?

  • The biology term comes straight from mythology: Flora was the Roman goddess of flowers, celebrated each spring at the Floralia festival, which is why the plants of a place are still collectively called its flora.

Word origin

From Latin 'Flora', the Roman goddess of flowers and spring, from 'flos, floris' meaning flower; adopted as a botanical term in the 18th century.

Remember it

FLORA contains 'FLOR-' as in 'floral' and 'florist' - all the flower words share the same Latin root.
