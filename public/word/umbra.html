UMBRA

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈʌm.brə/

UMBRA

What does "UMBRA" mean?

The darkest, central part of a shadow, where all light from a source is blocked.

Meanings

  1. The fully dark core of a shadow, where the light source is completely hidden. Only observers inside the Moon's umbra see a total solar eclipse. technical
  2. The dark central region of a sunspot, surrounded by a lighter penumbra. Through the filter, each sunspot showed a black umbra ringed in grey. technical

Word origin

Directly from Latin 'umbra' meaning 'shade' or 'shadow'; the same root gives English 'umbrella' (a little shade) and 'umbrage'.

Remember it

UMBRA is the dark center; an UMBRELLA makes you a little patch of it on a sunny day - same Latin 'umbra', shade.
