ERECT

adjective · 2 syllables · /ɪˈrɛkt/

ERECT

What does "ERECT" mean?

Upright in posture or position; also, to build or raise something upright.

Meanings

  1. Rigidly upright or vertical; not bent or leaning. He sat erect, shoulders squared, through the whole interview.
  2. To construct or raise a structure into an upright position. Workers erected the scaffolding overnight.
  3. To set up or establish, as a system or principle. They erected an elaborate set of rules around the contest. figurative

Word origin

From Latin 'erectus', past participle of 'erigere' (to set up, raise), from 'e-' (out, up) plus 'regere' (to direct, guide), the same root behind 'regular' and 'direct'.

Remember it

ERECT contains 'rect' - the same straight-line root as in 'rectangle' and 'correct'; all mean kept straight or upright.
