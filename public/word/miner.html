MINER

noun · 2 syllables · /'maɪ.nər/

MINER

What does "MINER" mean?

A person who works in a mine extracting coal, ore, or other minerals from the earth.

Meanings

  1. A worker who digs in a mine to extract coal, metal ore, or minerals. The miners descended in the cage at dawn.
  2. A computer or person that performs cryptocurrency mining by validating transactions. The bitcoin miner ran around the clock. technical

Did you know?

  • The phrase 'canary in a coal mine' is literal history: miners carried caged canaries underground because the birds collapsed from poison gas long before humans noticed it, giving the men time to escape.

Word origin

From Old French 'minier' / 'mineur' (one who mines), from 'mine' (an ore deposit or excavation), of Celtic or Gaulish origin; not related to 'minor' despite the identical sound.

Remember it

A MINER digs in a MINE; the extra R is the Rock they bring up. (A MINOR with an O is just young.)
