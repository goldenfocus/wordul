PURER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'pjʊr.ər/

PURER

What does "PURER" mean?

More free from contamination, mixture, or moral fault than something else.

Meanings

  1. The comparative form of 'pure': more unmixed, clean, or untainted. Spring water from the mountain is purer than what comes from the tap.

Word origin

The comparative of 'pure' (from Latin 'purus', 'clean, unmixed', via Old French 'pur'), with the standard English comparative suffix '-er'.

Remember it

PURER is just PURE with an -ER, the comparative tag that means 'more so'.
