noun · 2 syllables · /ˈtæroʊ/

TAROT

What does "TAROT" mean?

A deck of 78 illustrated cards used for games and for fortune-telling.

Meanings

  1. A pack of 78 cards, including 22 trumps (the Major Arcana), used for card games and, since the 18th century, for divination. She laid out the tarot in the shape of a cross.
  2. Any single card from such a deck. The Tower is one of the most striking tarots in the deck. informal

Did you know?

  • Tarot began as an ordinary card game in 1400s Italy, much like bridge - its association with fortune-telling didn't appear until the 1780s, over 300 years later.

Word origin

From French 'tarot', from Italian 'tarocchi', a card game of uncertain origin first recorded in 15th-century northern Italy; the divinatory use came centuries later.

Remember it

TAROT keeps a silent final 'T' from its French spelling - it's pronounced like 'TARO'.
