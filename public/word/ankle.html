ANKLE

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈæŋ.kəl/

ANKLE

What does "ANKLE" mean?

The joint connecting the foot to the leg.

Meanings

  1. The joint between the foot and the lower leg, or the slender part of the leg above it. He twisted his ankle stepping off the curb.

Did you know?

  • The human ankle is essentially a hinge of three bones - tibia, fibula, and talus - and the talus is one of the only bones in the body with no muscles attached to it.

Word origin

From Old English 'ancleow', reinforced by Old Norse 'ǫkkla'; the root traces to a Germanic word for a bend or joint, related to the idea of an angle.

Remember it

ANKLE has the letters of LEAN plus K - which is what you do on a bad one.
