AXIAL

adjective · 3 syllables · /ˈæksiəl/

AXIAL

What does "AXIAL" mean?

Relating to, forming, or arranged along an axis.

Meanings

  1. Of, on, around, or along an axis. The drill applies axial force straight down the bit. technical
  2. In anatomy, relating to the central axis of the body (the head, neck, and trunk). The skull and spine form the axial skeleton. technical

Did you know?

  • Your 'axial skeleton' - skull, spine, and rib cage - is exactly 80 bones, the central column distinct from the arms and legs of the appendicular skeleton.
  • Earth's roughly 23.4-degree axial tilt, not its changing distance from the Sun, is what gives us seasons - the planet is actually closest to the Sun during northern winter.

Word origin

From Latin 'axis' ('axle, pivot') plus the suffix '-al'; the same 'axis' root behind 'axle' and the geometric 'axis'.

Remember it

AXIAL = AXIS + AL. Anything axial runs along or around an axis.
