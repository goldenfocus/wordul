OUTGO

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈaʊt.ɡoʊ/

OUTGO

What does "OUTGO" mean?

Money that is spent or paid out; expenditure.

Meanings

  1. The total of what is paid out or expended, as opposed to income. When outgo exceeds income, the savings begin to vanish. formal
  2. To go faster than, to exceed, or to surpass. No runner could outgo him on the open straight. archaic

Word origin

A compound of 'out' and 'go', from Old English 'utgan', meaning to go out; the financial sense of money 'going out' parallels the older sense of physically departing.

Remember it

OUTGO is the opposite of income: money that goes OUT instead of coming in.
