ORDER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɔːr.dər/

ORDER

What does "ORDER" mean?

An arrangement of things in a sequence, or a command to do something.

Meanings

  1. The arrangement of things according to a sequence, pattern, or rank. List the names in alphabetical order.
  2. An authoritative command or instruction. The captain gave the order to abandon ship.
  3. A request for goods, food, or services to be supplied. Your order will ship within two business days.
  4. A state of tidiness, control, or proper functioning. She kept her workshop in perfect order.
  5. A religious, military, or honorific society bound by rules. He took vows and joined a monastic order.
  6. To command, request, or arrange something. I ordered the soup and a coffee.

Did you know?

  • In the Linnaean ranking of life, 'order' is a precise taxonomic rung between class and family - humans, for instance, belong to the order Primates.

Word origin

From Latin 'ordo' (a row, rank, arrangement), via Old French 'ordre'; originally referring to rows of threads in weaving.

Remember it

ORDER contains 'OR' twice over in spirit: you put things in order OR you give an order.
