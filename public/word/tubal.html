TUBAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'tuː.bəl/

TUBAL

What does "TUBAL" mean?

Relating to a tube, especially a fallopian tube in the body.

Meanings

  1. Of or relating to a bodily tube, most often a fallopian tube. A tubal pregnancy lodges in the fallopian tube rather than the uterus. technical
  2. Pertaining to a tube or tubular structure in general. The instrument measured tubal airflow in the duct. technical

Word origin

From Latin 'tubus' (pipe, tube) plus the adjectival suffix '-al', the same Latin root that gives English 'tube' and 'tubular'.

Remember it

TUBAL = TUBE made adjective: drop the 'e', add '-al', and the pipe becomes a description.
