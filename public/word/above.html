ABOVE

preposition · 2 syllables · /ə'bʌv/

ABOVE

What does "ABOVE" mean?

In or to a higher position than something else.

Meanings

  1. At a higher point or level than. The hawk circled high above the field.
  2. Greater in number, rank, or degree than. The role calls for experience above five years.
  3. Overhead; or earlier in a text. As noted above, the deadline has moved.
  4. Morally superior to; beyond. She is above petty revenge. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'abufan', a compound of 'a-' (on) and 'bufan' (over), the latter itself from 'be' (by) plus 'ufan' (above).

Remember it

ABOVE ends in '-bove'; picture a dove flying ABOVE you, swapping the d for a b.
