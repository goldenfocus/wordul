PROOF

What does "PROOF" mean?

Evidence or reasoning that establishes the truth of something beyond doubt.

Meanings

  1. Facts or arguments that establish that something is true. The receipt is proof that she paid in full.
  2. A logical or mathematical demonstration that a statement is necessarily true. His proof of the theorem ran to forty pages. technical
  3. A trial print of text or an image, made for checking before final printing. The author marked corrections on the galley proof. technical
  4. A measure of the alcoholic strength of a spirit. This whiskey is bottled at 100 proof.
  5. Able to resist or withstand something, as in 'waterproof'. The jacket is proof against the heaviest rain.

Did you know?

  • In the US, a spirit's 'proof' is simply twice its alcohol percentage - so 80-proof gin is 40% alcohol - a system born from old tests of whether gunpowder soaked in liquor would still ignite.

Word origin

From Old French 'preuve' (a test, proof), from Late Latin 'proba', from Latin 'probare' (to test, prove); related to 'probe' and 'probable'.

Remember it

PROOF shares its root with PROVE and PROBE - all from Latin 'probare', to test.
