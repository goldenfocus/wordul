FLUME

noun · 1 syllable · /fluːm/

FLUME

What does "FLUME" mean?

An artificial channel that carries water, or a narrow gorge with a stream running through it.

Meanings

  1. A constructed channel or chute that carries water, often to power machinery or float logs. Loggers sent timber rushing down the flume to the mill below.
  2. A deep narrow ravine or gorge with a stream flowing through it. The trail climbs beside a flume carved into the granite.
  3. A water-based amusement ride in which boats slide down a chute. We got soaked on the log flume at the fair. informal

Word origin

From Old French 'flum', meaning a river or stream, from Latin 'flumen' (river), itself from 'fluere', to flow.

Remember it

FLUME sounds like 'flu' plus 'me' - or picture water 'fluming' down a chute, all rush and spray.
