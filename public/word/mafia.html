noun · 3 syllables · /'mɑː.fi.ə/

MAFIA

What does "MAFIA" mean?

An organized secret society engaged in crime, originally Sicilian.

Meanings

  1. An organized international body engaged in racketeering and crime, originating in Sicily. Prosecutors spent years building a case against the local mafia.
  2. Any closed group seen as wielding power through influence and intimidation. He complained about the literary mafia who decided which novels got reviewed. figurative

Did you know?

  • The word reached a wide public through a play: the 1863 Sicilian drama 'I mafiusi di la Vicaria' set its mafiosi in a Palermo prison and helped fix the term in everyday speech.

Word origin

From Sicilian 'mafia', of uncertain origin but linked to a 19th-century sense of boldness or swagger; popularized after the 1863 Sicilian play 'I mafiusi di la Vicaria'.
