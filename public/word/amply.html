AMPLY

adverb · 2 syllables · /ˈæmpli/

AMPLY

What does "AMPLY" mean?

To a more than sufficient degree; generously or abundantly.

Meanings

  1. Sufficiently and then some; in a way that fully meets or exceeds need. The evidence amply demonstrated her innocence.
  2. Generously or richly in amount. The volunteers were amply rewarded with a feast.

Word origin

The adverb of 'ample', from Latin 'amplus' (large, abundant); 'amply' formed in English by adding the adverb suffix '-ly' to 'ample'.

Remember it

AMPLY is AMPLE with the E swapped for a Y - the adverb of 'plenty', spelled with plenty packed in.
