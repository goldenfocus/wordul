BEFIT

verb · 2 syllables · /bɪ'fɪt/

BEFIT

What does "BEFIT" mean?

To be appropriate or suitable for someone or something.

Meanings

  1. To be fitting, proper, or seemly for a person, role, or occasion. He greeted them with the calm dignity that befits a judge. formal

Word origin

Formed in Middle English from the prefix 'be-' (here intensifying or making transitive) plus the verb 'fit'; the 'be-' turns 'fit' into 'to suit thoroughly', as in 'become' or 'belong'.

Remember it

BEFIT = 'BE a good FIT' for the occasion - it is simply 'fit' with a dressed-up 'be-'.

Wordplay

What it teaches

What suits you is rarely what fits widest; the right size is measured in conduct, not cloth.
