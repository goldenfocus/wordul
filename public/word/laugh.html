LAUGH

verb · 1 syllable · /læf/

LAUGH

What does "LAUGH" mean?

To make the sounds and movements that express amusement, joy, or scorn.

Meanings

  1. To express mirth with vocal sounds and a smiling, often shaking, face. They laughed until their sides ached at the old joke.
  2. The act or sound of laughing. She had a warm, infectious laugh.
  3. To mock or deride, especially in the phrase 'laugh at'. Don't laugh at him for trying.

Word origin

From Old English 'hlæhhan', an imitative word echoing the sound of laughter, related to German 'lachen' and ultimately a Germanic root mimicking the noise.

Remember it

Spelling trap: LAUGH = 'Lots A U Get Happy' - and remember the silent GH that sounds like F.
