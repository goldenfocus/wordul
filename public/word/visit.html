VISIT

verb · 2 syllables · /'vɪz.ɪt/

VISIT

What does "VISIT" mean?

To go to see a person or place and spend time there.

Meanings

  1. To go and stay with a person or at a place for a time. We plan to visit my grandparents over the holidays.
  2. To go to a website or online location. Visit our site to claim the discount.
  3. An act of going to see someone or somewhere. Her visit lasted only an afternoon.
  4. To inflict or impose something on someone (often harm or punishment). May such suffering never be visited upon you. formal

Did you know?

  • At its root 'visit' simply means 'to go and see': it shares the Latin verb 'videre' with 'vision', 'video', and 'evident'.

Word origin

From Latin 'visitare' (to go to see), frequentative of 'visere' (to behold, go to see), from 'videre' (to see); via Old French 'visiter' into English.

Remember it

VISIT begins with 'VIS-' just like 'VISion' - you go to SEE.
