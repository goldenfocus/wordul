JUICE

noun · 1 syllable · /dʒuːs/

JUICE

What does "JUICE" mean?

The liquid naturally contained in fruit, vegetables, or other plant or animal tissue.

Meanings

  1. The drinkable liquid extracted from fruit or vegetables. She squeezed three oranges to make a single glass of juice.
  2. The natural fluid present in tissues, such as meat or the body. Let the steak rest so the juices redistribute before you slice it.
  3. Electrical power or fuel; energy or vitality. My phone's dead - it ran out of juice on the train. informal
  4. To extract the liquid from, or to add energy or force to. He juiced a whole bag of kale before breakfast.

Word origin

From Old French 'jus' (juice, broth), from Latin 'ius' (broth, sauce, soup), related to an ancient root meaning to mix or blend.
