ASKEW

adverb · 2 syllables · /ə'skjuː/

ASKEW

What does "ASKEW" mean?

Out of line, crooked, or tilted to one side.

Meanings

  1. At an angle; not straight or level. The painting hung askew after the door slammed.
  2. Crooked, awry, or out of proper order. His tie was askew and his hair was wild.

Did you know?

  • Type 'askew' into Google and the search results page tilts visibly to one side - a deliberate Easter egg the engineers built to match the word's meaning.

Word origin

Likely from the prefix 'a-' ('on') plus 'skew' ('to turn aside'), which came through Old North French 'eskiuwer' from a Germanic root meaning 'to shy away'.

Remember it

ASKEW = A + SKEW; the word itself ends in a wonky tail of letters (-KEW) that looks a little crooked.
