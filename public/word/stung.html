STUNG

verb · 1 syllable · /stʌŋ/

STUNG

What does "STUNG" mean?

Past tense and past participle of 'sting'; pierced or pained sharply.

Meanings

  1. Past tense of sting: wounded with a sharp-pointed organ, as a bee does. A wasp stung him on the wrist.
  2. Caused a sharp, smarting pain or sensation. The salt water stung her cut.
  3. Hurt emotionally; wounded with words or rejection. His criticism stung more than she let on. figurative
  4. Cheated or overcharged someone. They got stung paying double the fair price. informal

Did you know?

  • When a honeybee has stung a human, it usually dies: its barbed stinger lodges in the skin and tears loose from the bee's body as it pulls away.

Word origin

Past tense of 'sting', from Old English 'stingan', to prick or pierce, from a Proto-Germanic root meaning to be sharp or to stab.

Remember it

STUNG = STING with the I stung into a U - the vowel got pierced and changed shape.
