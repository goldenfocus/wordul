FLYER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈflaɪ.ər/

FLYER

What does "FLYER" mean?

A small printed sheet handed out or posted to advertise something.

Meanings

  1. A single printed leaflet distributed to promote an event, product, or cause. They stapled flyers for the concert to every lamppost.
  2. A person or thing that flies, such as an air passenger or a bird. Frequent flyers earn points toward free trips.
  3. A risky speculative venture or bet. He took a flyer on a tiny startup and it paid off. informal

Word origin

From the verb 'fly' (Old English 'fleogan') plus the agent suffix '-er'; the leaflet sense arose because such sheets were scattered or 'flown' through crowds.

Remember it

A FLYER is paper that seems to FLY from hand to hand - the word literally hides FLY.
