HEAVE

verb · 1 syllable · /hiːv/

HEAVE

What does "HEAVE" mean?

To lift, haul, or throw something heavy with great effort.

Meanings

  1. To lift or haul a heavy thing with effort. They heaved the crate onto the truck.
  2. To rise and fall rhythmically, as a chest or the sea. His chest heaved after the sprint.
  3. To utter a sound, especially a sigh, with effort. She heaved a sigh of relief.
  4. To retch or vomit. The rough crossing left half the passengers heaving over the rail. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'hebban' ('to lift, raise'), from Proto-Germanic 'habjaną', related to Latin 'capere' ('to take') via a shared Indo-European root.

Remember it

HEAVE shares its root with 'heavy' and 'have' - you grab hold and lift the weight.
