BONUS

noun · 2 syllables · /'boʊ.nəs/

BONUS

What does "BONUS" mean?

An extra payment or benefit given on top of what is normally due or expected.

Meanings

  1. A sum of money added to a person's wages as a reward for performance or as a share of profits. The whole team got a year-end bonus after the product launch.
  2. Any unexpected extra advantage that comes along with something. The flat was cheap, and the rooftop view was a real bonus. informal
  3. Extra; provided in addition to the usual amount. The album shipped with three bonus tracks.

Word origin

From Latin 'bonus' meaning 'good'; adopted in English in the late 1700s, reputedly from the slang of the London Stock Exchange for a dividend or premium.

Remember it
