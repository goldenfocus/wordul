SPREE

noun · 1 syllable · /spriː/

SPREE

What does "SPREE" mean?

A bout of unrestrained indulgence in an activity, especially spending or revelry.

Meanings

  1. A period of uninhibited activity, often involving excess such as spending, drinking, or celebration. After payday she went on a shopping spree and regretted it by Sunday. informal
  2. A rapid, intense burst of repeated actions, sometimes destructive. The team is on a winning spree, six games and counting. informal

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, first recorded in Scots and northern English around 1800; possibly related to 'spreath', a cattle raid, from Irish and Scottish Gaelic.
