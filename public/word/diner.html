DINER

noun · 2 syllables · /'daɪ.nər/

DINER

What does "DINER" mean?

A small, casual restaurant, often shaped like a railway car; also, a person who is eating.

Meanings

  1. A small, informal American restaurant serving simple meals, often open long hours. We pulled off the highway for pancakes at a roadside diner.
  2. A person who is eating, especially in a restaurant. The waiter checked on each diner before clearing the plates.
  3. A railway carriage in which meals are served; a dining car. On the long train route, the diner stayed open until midnight.

Word origin

From 'dine' (to eat dinner, via Old French 'disner') plus '-er'; the restaurant sense grew in America from eateries built inside or shaped like railway dining cars.

Remember it

A DINER is where a diner dines - the place and the person share one word.
