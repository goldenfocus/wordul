ENTRY

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɛn.tri/

ENTRY

What does "ENTRY" mean?

The act of going in, or a place or record through which one enters.

Meanings

  1. The act or right of coming or going into a place. No entry was permitted after the doors closed.
  2. A door, passage, or hall by which one enters. She left her boots by the entry.
  3. An item recorded in a list, diary, dictionary, or ledger. The dictionary added a new entry for the slang term.
  4. A person or thing submitted into a competition. Her photo was the winning entry.

Word origin

From Old French 'entree' (an entrance), the feminine past participle of 'entrer' (to enter), from Latin 'intrare' (to go in).

Remember it

ENTRY = ENTER minus the second E, plus a Y - the doorway shortened, as doorways are.

What it teaches

An entry can be a door or a record; both keep the truth of who passed through.
