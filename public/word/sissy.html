SISSY

noun · 2 syllables · /'sɪs.i/

SISSY

What does "SISSY" mean?

A derogatory term for a person, especially a boy, seen as timid or unmanly.

Meanings

  1. A person regarded as weak, cowardly, or effeminate; used as an insult, chiefly of boys or men. He was called a sissy for refusing to climb the tree. informal
  2. An affectionate diminutive for a sister. Come here, sissy, and help me set the table. informal
  3. Suited to or characteristic of a timid or effeminate person. He dismissed the pink wallpaper as too sissy. informal

Word origin

An American English diminutive of 'sis', itself a shortening of 'sister'; the weakling sense developed in the late 19th century from the 'little sister' connotation.

Remember it

SISSY hides SIS (sister) twice over - the same root that gave the word its 'little sister' put-down.
