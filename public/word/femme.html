FEMME

noun · 1 syllable · /fɛm/

FEMME

What does "FEMME" mean?

A person, especially in LGBTQ+ contexts, who expresses a feminine gender identity or style.

Meanings

  1. A person, often within queer culture, who presents in a markedly feminine way. She identifies as femme and dresses to match.
  2. Characterized by a feminine presentation or style. He has a soft, femme aesthetic in how he dresses.
  3. A woman, as in the borrowed French phrase 'femme fatale'. The film's femme fatale lured him into the scheme. formal

Did you know?

  • 'Femme' is just the French word for 'woman', and through its Latin source 'femina' it is a close cousin of 'feminine' and 'female' - though English 'female' actually arrived by a different, unrelated-looking route.

Word origin

Directly from French 'femme' meaning 'woman', from Latin 'femina'; English borrowed it whole, keeping the French spelling and a near-French pronunciation.

Remember it

FEMME doubles the M of 'feminine' and stops there - keep the French spelling, drop the rest.
