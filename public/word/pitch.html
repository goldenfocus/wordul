PITCH

noun · 1 syllable · /pɪtʃ/

PITCH

What does "PITCH" mean?

The highness or lowness of a sound, or a throw, a sales talk, or a sticky tar.

Meanings

  1. How high or low a sound is, set by the frequency of its vibration. She sang the note at exactly the right pitch.
  2. To throw or toss something, especially a baseball to a batter. He pitched the ball low and outside.
  3. A persuasive speech to sell a product, idea, or oneself. The founder gave a five-minute pitch to investors. informal
  4. A field or area marked out for an outdoor game, in British English. The teams walked onto the pitch as the rain stopped.
  5. A dark, sticky resin or tar obtained from petroleum or wood. They sealed the boat's hull with pitch.

Did you know?

  • Pitch the tar looks solid but flows like a liquid: a sample set up in 1927 at the University of Queensland has dripped fewer than ten times in almost a century - the world's longest-running lab experiment.

Word origin

Two roots converge: the tar sense is from Old English 'pic', from Latin 'pix'; the throw/sound/incline senses come from Middle English 'picchen' (to thrust, fix in place), of uncertain origin.

Remember it

PITCH covers throw, tune, sale, field, and tar - one slot, five jobs; remember it as the word that does the most pitching.
