GREAT

adjective · 1 syllable · /ɡreɪt/

GREAT

What does "GREAT" mean?

Very large in size, amount, or importance, or remarkably good in quality.

Meanings

  1. Of an extent, amount, or intensity considerably above average. A great crowd gathered in the square.
  2. Of ability, quality, or eminence considerably above the normal; excellent. She is a great novelist.
  3. Very good; enjoyable or satisfying. We had a great time at the party. informal
  4. Denoting one more generation removed in a family relationship. She visited her great-grandmother.

Word origin

From Old English 'grēat' meaning thick, coarse, or massive; from Proto-Germanic '*grautaz' — the sense shifted from physical bulk to importance and excellence.
