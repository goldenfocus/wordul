FIERY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'faɪ.ə.ri/

FIERY

What does "FIERY" mean?

Consisting of, resembling, or full of fire.

Meanings

  1. Burning, flaming, or glowing like fire. The sunset spread a fiery red across the whole horizon.
  2. Passionate, quick-tempered, or intensely spirited. Her fiery speech brought the delegates to their feet. figurative
  3. Producing a burning sensation, as of spicy food. The curry had a fiery kick that left them reaching for water.

Did you know?

  • 'Fiery' is the rare adjective that reshuffles its own root: it comes straight from 'fire', yet the 'r' and 'e' swap places, which is why so many people misspell it 'firey'.

Word origin

From Middle English 'firi', from 'fir' (fire), from Old English 'fyr', of Germanic origin; the unusual spelling reorders the letters of 'fire'.

Remember it

FIERY = FIRE with the R and E swapped, then add Y; remember 'fire' wears its letters out of order.
