SUITE

noun · 1 syllable · /swiːt/

SUITE

What does "SUITE" mean?

A connected set of things, such as rooms, musical movements, or software programs, grouped as a unit.

Meanings

  1. A set of connected rooms, especially in a hotel, used as a single unit. They booked the honeymoon suite overlooking the harbor.
  2. A musical composition made up of several loosely connected instrumental movements. Bach's cello suites are performed worldwide. technical
  3. A coordinated set of software applications sold or used together. The office suite includes a word processor and spreadsheet. technical
  4. A matching set of furniture for a single room. She ordered a three-piece living-room suite.

Did you know?

  • 'Suite' and 'suit' are the same word twice borrowed: both come from Latin 'sequi', to follow - which is why a suite of rooms, a suit of clothes, and a card suit all mean 'things that go together'.

Word origin

From French 'suite', meaning a following or sequence, from Old French 'sieute', ultimately from Latin 'sequi' (to follow); a doublet of the English word 'suit'.

Remember it

SUITE is SUIT with a silent E, and it sounds exactly like 'sweet' - a sweet suite at the hotel.
