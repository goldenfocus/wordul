TWIXT

What does "TWIXT" mean?

A shortened, poetic form of 'betwixt', meaning between two things.

Meanings

  1. Between; in the space or relationship separating two things (chiefly poetic or archaic). Twixt sea and sky there was no telling where one ended. archaic

Word origin

A clipped form of 'betwixt', from Old English 'betweox' / 'betwux', built on the root of 'two'; the dropped first syllable is marked by an apostrophe ('twixt) in older verse.

Remember it

TWIXT is BeTWIXT with the front bitten off - both start from 'two', the number a between-space always needs.

A little poem

Twixt the last lamp and the first cold star,
the road forgets how long roads are.

couplet

What it teaches

Most of life is lived twixt one thing and the next - the in-between is not the wait, it is the place.
