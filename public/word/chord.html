CHORD

What does "CHORD" mean?

A group of musical notes sounded together in harmony.

Meanings

  1. Three or more musical notes played or sung at the same time. She strummed a single minor chord and the whole room went quiet.
  2. A straight line joining two points on a curve. In geometry, a diameter is the longest possible chord of a circle. technical
  3. An emotional response, especially one of sympathy or recognition. Her speech struck a chord with every parent in the audience. figurative

Did you know?

  • The 'h' in 'chord' is a historical error: the music word is a clipping of 'accord', but writers later bolted on an 'h' to make it look like 'chorda', the unrelated Greek word for a string.

Word origin

A 16th-century shortening of 'accord' (harmony of sound); the spelling was later reshaped to resemble Latin 'chorda', a string.

Remember it

A musical CHORD has an H (Harmony); a rope CORD does not.
