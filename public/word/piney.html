PINEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'paɪ.ni/

PINEY

What does "PINEY" mean?

Of, relating to, smelling of, or covered with pine trees.

Meanings

  1. Having the scent, character, or growth of pine trees. The trail wound through cool, piney air.
  2. Abounding in or planted with pines. They built the cabin on a piney ridge.

Did you know?

  • In New Jersey's Pine Barrens, 'Piney' is not only an adjective but a name: locals of the vast pine forest have been called Pineys for generations.

Word origin

From 'pine' (the conifer), from Old English 'pin' via Latin 'pinus', plus the adjective suffix '-y'; the variant spelling 'piny' is also standard.

Remember it

PINEY is just PINE wearing a 'Y' - the tree turned into a description.
