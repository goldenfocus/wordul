adjective · 2 syllables · /'ɑd.ər/

ODDER

What does "ODDER" mean?

More strange or unusual; the comparative form of the adjective 'odd'.

Meanings

  1. More strange, peculiar, or unexpected than something else. The deeper she read the diary, the odder his story became.

Word origin

The comparative of 'odd', which comes from Old Norse 'oddi', a point of land or triangle, hence the leftover third that did not pair off; '-er' marks the comparative.

Remember it

ODDER is ODD plus -ER: take something already odd and turn the strangeness up a notch.

A little poem

Each answer that he gave was strange, and then
the silence after it was odder still again.

couplet

What it teaches

Strangeness is comparative; spend long enough somewhere and the odd becomes the ordinary.
