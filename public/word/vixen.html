VIXEN

noun · 2 syllables · /'vɪk.sən/

VIXEN

What does "VIXEN" mean?

A female fox.

Meanings

  1. A female fox. The vixen led her cubs out of the den at dusk.
  2. A woman regarded as fierce, quarrelsome, or seductive. The role cast her as a scheming vixen. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Vixen' is a linguistic fossil: it is the only standard English word in which a native Old English initial 'f' survived as 'v', preserving a southern dialect that voiced f-sounds (the same accent that once said 'vather' for father).

Word origin

From Old English 'fyxe', the feminine of 'fox'; the initial 'v' reflects a southern English dialect that voiced 'f' to 'v', which is why we say vixen, not 'fixen'.

Remember it

VIXEN = a fox that's a 'she'; the V is the southern accent's voiced version of fox's F.
