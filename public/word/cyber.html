CYBER

adjective · 2 syllables · /'saɪ.bər/

CYBER

What does "CYBER" mean?

Relating to computers, networks, or virtual reality, especially the culture and security of the internet.

Meanings

  1. Of or relating to computers, the internet, and virtual environments. The firm hired a team to handle cyber threats.
  2. Short for cybersecurity, the practice of protecting systems and data from digital attack. She works in cyber for a major bank. informal

Did you know?

  • Every 'cyber' word traces back to a steersman: the prefix comes from 'cybernetics', which Norbert Wiener built in 1948 from Greek 'kybernetes', the man who steers a ship.

Word origin

Back-formed from 'cybernetics', which Norbert Wiener coined in 1948 from Greek 'kybernetes', a steersman or governor.

Remember it

CYBER hides 'BE' in the middle - to BE online is to BE cyber.
