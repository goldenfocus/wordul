POUTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈpaʊ.ti/

POUTY

What does "POUTY" mean?

Showing displeasure or sulkiness by pushing out the lips; sulky.

Meanings

  1. Tending to sulk or look displeased, often with the lips pushed forward. The toddler grew pouty when his snack was taken away. informal
  2. Of lips: full and protruding, often considered attractive. The portrait emphasized her pouty lips and dark eyes. informal

Word origin

From 'pout' (to push out the lips, sulk), of uncertain origin, possibly imitative or from a Scandinavian source, plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

POUTY = POUT + Y; the 'ou' makes the very shape your mouth pushes into when you sulk.
