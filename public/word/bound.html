BOUND

verb · 1 syllable · /baʊnd/

BOUND

What does "BOUND" mean?

To leap or spring energetically, or to be tied, certain, or limited depending on sense.

Meanings

  1. To move forward with leaping strides. The dog bounded across the lawn to greet her.
  2. Certain or sure to do something. If you keep skipping practice, you're bound to fall behind.
  3. Tied up, fastened, or obliged by duty or law. They were bound by a strict confidentiality agreement.
  4. Going or intending to go toward a particular place. We caught the train bound for the coast.
  5. A limit or boundary of an area or of what is acceptable. Their generosity knew no bounds.
  6. To form the boundary of; to enclose or limit. The estate is bounded on the north by a river. formal

Word origin

The 'leap' sense comes from French 'bondir' ('to bounce, resound'); the 'limit' sense from Old French 'bonde' ('boundary'); and the 'tied' sense is the past participle of 'bind', from Old English 'bindan'.

Remember it

BOUND packs three words in one: BOUNCE (leap), BIND (tied), and BORDER (limit) - all share the 'b' and the idea of an edge or push.
