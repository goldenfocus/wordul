adjective · 1 syllable · /ʃɪər/

SHEER

What does "SHEER" mean?

Complete and utter; or very thin and translucent; or extremely steep.

Meanings

  1. Nothing other than; complete, utter, absolute. He won by sheer determination.
  2. Very thin, fine, and nearly transparent, as fabric. The window was hung with sheer white curtains.
  3. Extremely steep, almost vertical. The climbers faced a sheer cliff of bare rock.
  4. To swerve or change course suddenly, especially of a ship. The boat sheered away from the rocks. technical

Word origin

From Middle English 'schere', meaning clear or pure, probably from Old Norse 'skaerr' (bright, clean); the 'steep' and 'thin' senses developed from the idea of unbroken, pure surface.

Remember it

SHEER sounds like 'sheer' off a cliff - and you can see right through SHEER fabric: thin, steep, and pure all at once.
