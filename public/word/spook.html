SPOOK

noun · 1 syllable · /spuːk/

SPOOK

What does "SPOOK" mean?

A ghost or apparition, especially one imagined as frightening.

Meanings

  1. A ghost or specter. The old mill was said to be full of spooks after dark. informal
  2. To frighten or startle suddenly. A snapping twig spooked the deer and it bolted.
  3. A spy or intelligence agent. The novel follows a retired spook who can't quite leave the trade. informal

Word origin

From Dutch 'spook' meaning ghost or apparition, adopted into American English in the early 19th century.
