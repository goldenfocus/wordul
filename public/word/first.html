FIRST

adjective · 1 syllable · /fɜːrst/

FIRST

What does "FIRST" mean?

Coming before all others in order, time, rank, or importance.

Meanings

  1. Earliest in time or order; preceding all the rest. She was the first person to sign the petition.
  2. Before anything else; in the first place. First, we need to check whether the door is even locked.
  3. A person or thing that comes before all others, or an initial achievement. Landing the contract was a first for the young firm.

Did you know?

  • The German title 'Fürst' ('prince') and the English word 'first' grow from the same ancient root - a prince was simply 'the foremost one' of his people.

Word origin

From Old English 'fyrst' ('foremost'), a superlative of the root that also gives 'fore'; related to Old Norse 'fyrstr' and German 'Fürst' ('prince', literally 'the foremost one').

Remember it

FIRST hides 'FIR' - picture the tallest fir tree, standing first above the forest.

Wordplay

What it teaches

Being first proves you were early, not that you were right - the order of arrival is not the order of merit.
