LOCAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈloʊ.kəl/

LOCAL

What does "LOCAL" mean?

Belonging to or relating to a particular place or neighborhood.

Meanings

  1. Relating to or characteristic of a particular area, especially one's own neighborhood. We try to buy from local farmers whenever we can.
  2. A person who lives in a particular place, as opposed to a visitor. The locals knew a quiet beach the tourists never found. informal
  3. A nearby pub or bar that one frequents (British). He stopped by his local for a pint after work. informal
  4. Affecting only a part of the body, as anesthesia. The dentist gave her a local before the filling. technical

Word origin

From Late Latin 'localis' (pertaining to a place), from Latin 'locus' (place), via Old French 'local' into English.

Remember it

LOCAL contains 'LOC' from Latin 'locus' = place; what's local is what's tied to a place.
