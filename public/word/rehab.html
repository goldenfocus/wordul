REHAB

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈriːhæb/

REHAB

What does "REHAB" mean?

Treatment to restore health, function, or a property; short for rehabilitation.

Meanings

  1. A program or facility for treating addiction or restoring physical function after illness or injury. After the surgery he spent three weeks in rehab relearning to walk. informal
  2. The renovation or restoration of a building or property. The old factory rehab turned the warehouse into lofts. informal
  3. To restore to health, good condition, or function. They rehabbed the injured hawk and released it in spring. informal

Word origin

A 20th-century shortening of 'rehabilitation', from Latin 'rehabilitare', from 're-' ('again') plus 'habilitare' ('to make fit'), from 'habilis' ('able, fit').

Remember it

RE + HAB: get your 'hab'-ility back. 'Habilis' (Latin: able) is hiding in the middle.
