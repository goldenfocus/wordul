TOXIC

adjective · 2 syllables · /'tɒk.sɪk/

TOXIC

What does "TOXIC" mean?

Poisonous; capable of causing harm or death through chemical action, or figuratively harmful.

Meanings

  1. Containing or being poisonous material able to cause illness, injury, or death. The factory was fined for dumping toxic waste into the river.
  2. Deeply harmful, corrosive, or unhealthy in a relationship, workplace, or culture. She finally left the toxic job that had been wearing her down. figurative

Did you know?

  • 'Toxic' is rooted in archery: the Greek 'toxikon' meant 'poison for arrows', from 'toxon', a bow - the chemistry came later than the weapon.
  • Oxford named 'toxic' its 2018 Word of the Year as people stopped applying it mainly to chemicals and started applying it to bosses, exes, and online culture.

Word origin

From Late Latin 'toxicus', 'poisoned', from Latin 'toxicum', 'poison', from Greek 'toxikon (pharmakon)', literally 'poison for arrows', from 'toxon', meaning 'bow'.

Remember it

TOXIC starts like 'toxin' and 'toxon' (Greek for bow) - a toxic thing is poison fired straight at you, like a poisoned arrow.

A little poem

No skull-and-crossbones marks the worst of it;

Wordplay

  • My chemistry teacher said the relationship between sodium and water was toxic. I said no - it just had explosive chemistry.

What it teaches

Poison rarely announces itself; the most toxic things are the ones we keep drinking because we got used to the taste.
