BATTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'bæt.i/

BATTY

What does "BATTY" mean?

Crazy, eccentric, or slightly mad.

Meanings

  1. Mildly insane, foolish, or eccentric. Everyone thought the old man was batty for talking to his roses. informal

Word origin

From the phrase 'bats in the belfry', an early-1900s image of a head full of erratically swooping bats; the adjective 'batty' was clipped from it.

Remember it

BATTY = full of BATs (in the belfry), with a -TY tacked on for the condition.

A little poem

Wordplay
