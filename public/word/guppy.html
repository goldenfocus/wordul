GUPPY

What does "GUPPY" mean?

A small, brightly colored freshwater fish popular in home aquariums and famous for breeding rapidly.

Meanings

  1. A tiny tropical fish (Poecilia reticulata) native to South America, kept widely as a pet for its hardiness and color. The tank was full of guppies darting between the plastic plants.

Did you know?

  • The guppy is named after Robert John Lechmere Guppy, who shipped the little fish from Trinidad to the British Museum in the 1860s.
  • Guppies don't lay eggs - they are livebearers, giving birth to fully formed, swimming young.

Word origin

Named after Robert John Lechmere Guppy, a Trinidad-based naturalist who sent specimens to the British Museum in the 1860s, where the fish was named in his honor.
