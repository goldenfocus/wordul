CUBIC

What does "CUBIC" mean?

Of, relating to, or shaped like a cube; or measuring volume by a unit raised to the third power.

Meanings

  1. Denoting a unit of volume equal to that of a cube whose side is the stated length. The tank holds two cubic metres of water.
  2. Having the shape or properties of a cube. The mineral pyrite often grows in striking cubic crystals.
  3. Involving the third power of a quantity, as in an equation of degree three. Solving the cubic equation gave three real roots. technical

Did you know?

  • The formula for solving any cubic equation drove the discovery of imaginary numbers: when Gerolamo Cardano published it in 1545, the method sometimes forced you to take the square root of a negative number even to reach a real answer.

Word origin

From Latin 'cubicus', from Greek 'kybikos', from 'kybos' meaning a cube or die used in gaming.
