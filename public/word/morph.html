MORPH

What does "MORPH" mean?

To change smoothly and gradually from one form, shape, or state into another.

Meanings

  1. To transform gradually and seamlessly into something different. The startup morphed from a podcast app into a payments company.
  2. In film and graphics, to blend one image into another through computer animation. The effects team morphed the man's face into a wolf's. technical
  3. In linguistics, a minimal meaningful unit of a word as it is actually pronounced. The plural marker in 'cats' is a single morph. technical
  4. In biology, a distinct form or color variant within a single species. The snow leopard's pale coat is a morph suited to its mountains. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Morph' as a verb is surprisingly young: it was popularized around 1990 by Hollywood's new computer 'morphing' effects, clipped from the ancient Greek-rooted 'metamorphosis'.

Word origin

A clipping of 'metamorphosis', from Greek 'morphe', meaning 'form, shape'; popularized as a verb by computer-graphics 'morphing' in the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Remember it

MORPH shares its core with metaMORPHosis - both come from Greek 'morphe', shape.
