LOVER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈlʌv.ɚ/

LOVER

What does "LOVER" mean?

A person who is in a romantic or sexual relationship with another, or who deeply enjoys something.

Meanings

  1. A partner in a romantic or sexual relationship. The poem was written for a lover she never named.
  2. A person who greatly enjoys or is devoted to a specified thing. He's a lover of old jazz records and strong coffee.

Word origin

From the verb 'love' (Old English 'lufian', from a Proto-Germanic root meaning 'to cherish') plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Remember it

LOVER = LOVE + R; the R is the 'one who' does the loving.
