CREAM

noun · 1 syllable · /kriːm/

CREAM

What does "CREAM" mean?

The thick, fatty part of milk that rises to the top.

Meanings

  1. The rich, fatty layer that separates from milk. She poured cream into her coffee.
  2. A thick, smooth cosmetic or medicinal substance applied to the skin. Apply the cream twice a day to the rash.
  3. The very best part of something or a group. These recruits are the cream of the academy. figurative
  4. A pale yellowish-white color. The walls were painted a soft cream.
  5. To beat ingredients into a smooth, soft mixture; informally, to defeat decisively. Cream the butter and sugar before adding eggs.

Word origin

From Old French 'cresme', a blend of Late Latin 'chrisma' (holy oil, ointment) and Gaulish-derived 'cramum' (skin on milk); the two sources fused in French.

Remember it

CREAM rises to the top - and so does the 'cream of the crop'. The word literally floats above the milk.
