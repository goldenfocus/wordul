ADOBE

noun · 3 syllables · /əˈdoʊbi/

ADOBE

What does "ADOBE" mean?

A sun-dried brick of clay and straw, or the building material made from it.

Meanings

  1. A brick of clay mixed with straw and dried in the sun, used for building. The walls were thick adobe, cool even at noon.
  2. A house or structure built from such bricks. They restored an old adobe on the edge of the desert.
  3. Made of or resembling sun-dried clay brick. The town's adobe church has stood for three centuries.

Did you know?

  • 'Adobe' carries an ancient passport: scholars trace it from a hieroglyphic Egyptian word for brick into Coptic, then Arabic 'al-tub' (the brick), then Spanish, before it reached English.

Word origin

From Spanish 'adobe', borrowed from Arabic 'al-tub' (the brick), traced by scholars back through Coptic to an Ancient Egyptian word for brick - one of the oldest building words in any language.

Remember it

ADOBE = A Dwelling Of Baked Earth - a sun-baked mud house spelled out.
