ISLET

noun · 2 syllables · /'aɪ.lət/

ISLET

What does "ISLET" mean?

A very small island.

Meanings

  1. A tiny island, often just a rock or sandbar above the waterline. A single palm leaned over the bare islet in the lagoon.
  2. A small isolated cluster of cells, as in the islets of Langerhans that make insulin. Type 1 diabetes destroys the insulin-producing islets in the pancreas. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'islette', a diminutive of 'isle' (island), itself from Latin 'insula'; the silent 's' is a later spelling borrowed from Latin.

Remember it

ISLET is ISLE + T - a tiny island that even kept the silent 's' from its bigger cousin.
