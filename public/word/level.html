LEVEL

noun · 2 syllables · /'lɛv.əl/

LEVEL

What does "LEVEL" mean?

A horizontal position or height, or a tool used to check whether a surface is horizontal.

Meanings

  1. A position or height on a scale; an amount or degree. The water rose to a dangerous level overnight.
  2. A tool with a liquid-filled bubble vial used to check whether a surface is horizontal. He set the spirit level on the shelf to make sure it wasn't tilted.
  3. Flat, horizontal, and even; without slope. Find level ground before you pitch the tent.
  4. To make flat or even; to bring down to the ground. The demolition crew leveled the old factory in a single morning.
  5. To be honest and direct with someone. Let me level with you - we're behind schedule. informal

Word origin

From Old French 'livel', from Latin 'libella', a 'little balance' or carpenter's level, a diminutive of 'libra', meaning balance or scales.
