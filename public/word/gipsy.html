GIPSY

noun · 2 syllables · /'dʒɪp.si/

GIPSY

What does "GIPSY" mean?

An older British spelling of 'gypsy', a now-dated and often offensive term for a Romani person.

Meanings

  1. A dated and frequently offensive term for a member of the Romani people; many now consider it a slur and prefer 'Roma' or 'Romani'. Older novels use 'gipsy', a spelling and term modern writers avoid in favor of 'Romani'. archaic

Did you know?

  • The word rests on a centuries-old mistake: Europeans thought the Romani came from Egypt and called them 'Egyptians', but the Romani language is an Indo-Aryan tongue tracing back to northern India.

Word origin

A shortening of 'Egyptian', from the medieval mistaken belief that the Romani people came from Egypt; in fact their language traces to northern India.

What it teaches

A name born from a mistaken assumption can outlive the truth; what a people call themselves should outrank what strangers guessed.
