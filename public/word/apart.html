APART

adverb · 2 syllables · /əˈpɑːrt/

APART

What does "APART" mean?

Separated by distance, or into pieces.

Meanings

  1. At a distance from one another in space or time. The two villages sit only a mile apart.
  2. Into separate pieces or components. He took the engine apart to find the leak.
  3. Aside or excepted; set to one side. Joking apart, we really do need to leave. formal

Word origin

From Old French 'a part', meaning 'to the side', from Latin 'ad partem', 'to the part' or 'to one side'.

Remember it

A-PART means moved to A PART, set off on its own side.

Wordplay

  • 'Apart' and 'a part' are spelled almost the same but mean the opposite - one keeps you separate, the other makes you belong.

What it teaches
