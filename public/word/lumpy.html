LUMPY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'lʌm.pi/

LUMPY

What does "LUMPY" mean?

Covered with or full of lumps; uneven in texture.

Meanings

  1. Full of or covered with lumps; not smooth. The gravy went lumpy the moment she stopped stirring.
  2. Uneven, awkward, or coming in irregular bursts. Sales were lumpy this quarter - flat for weeks, then a sudden spike. informal

Word origin

From 'lump' (a compact mass, of uncertain origin, possibly imitative or from a Germanic source) plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

LUMPY = LUMP + Y. The Y is the last bump sticking up out of the mash.

A little poem

She swore the porridge would be smooth as glass-
it surfaced lumpy, proud, and would not pass.

couplet

Wordplay

  • My mattress is so lumpy I sleep on a topographic map. Last night I summited a spring.

What it teaches

Smoothness is just lumps you bothered to stir; most progress is lumpy before it pours.
