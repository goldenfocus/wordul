BLUER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈbluːər/

BLUER

What does "BLUER" mean?

The comparative form of 'blue', meaning more blue in color or mood.

Meanings

  1. More blue in hue than something else. The afternoon sky was bluer over the mountains.
  2. More sad or melancholy than before. Every rainy Sunday left him bluer than the last. figurative

Word origin

The comparative of 'blue', from Old French 'bleu' of Germanic origin, with the standard English '-er' suffix marking greater degree.

Remember it

BLUER is BLUE plus R - add the R and you push the color one shade further along.

A little poem
