LOWER

verb · 2 syllables · /ˈloʊ.ɚ/

LOWER

What does "LOWER" mean?

To move something down to a lesser height or level; also the comparative of 'low'.

Meanings

  1. To let or bring something down to a lower position. They lowered the coffin slowly into the grave.
  2. To reduce in amount, degree, or intensity. Please lower your voice in the library.
  3. The comparative of 'low'; situated below something else or less in amount. The books are on the lower shelf.
  4. Of the sky or someone's expression, to look dark and threatening (pronounced 'LOW-er', rhyming with 'flower'). Storm clouds lowered over the hills all afternoon. formal

Did you know?

  • 'Lower' is two unrelated words sharing one spelling: the everyday 'bring down' (rhymes with 'mower') and a literary 'lower', meaning to scowl or darken like a storm sky (rhymes with 'flower').

Word origin

The 'move down' and comparative senses come from 'low' (from Old Norse 'lagr') plus '-er'. The 'look threatening' sense is a separate word from Middle English 'louren' (to frown), a homograph pronounced differently.

Remember it

To LOWER is to make more LOW; the '-er' just turns 'low' into the action of going down.
