CURLY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'kɜːr.li/

CURLY

What does "CURLY" mean?

Having or arranged in curls; forming or full of spirals or curves.

Meanings

  1. Of hair, naturally forming coils or ringlets rather than lying straight. Her curly hair sprang back the moment she let go of it.
  2. Twisted, coiled, or wavy in form generally. The pig had a tight, curly tail.

Word origin

From Middle English 'curl', a metathesized form of earlier 'crul', plus the adjectival suffix '-y'.

Remember it

CURLY hides 'URL' in the middle - even a web address can be a curly link.
