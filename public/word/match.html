noun · 1 syllable · /mætʃ/

MATCH

What does "MATCH" mean?

A short stick tipped with chemicals that ignites when struck against a rough surface.

Meanings

  1. A small fire-starting stick whose coated head bursts into flame when scraped. She struck a match and the candle caught.
  2. A contest or game between opponents. The tennis match went to five sets.
  3. A person or thing that corresponds to or equals another. These two socks aren't a match.
  4. To correspond, agree with, or be equal to something else. Your story doesn't match the receipt.
  5. To pair compatible people or things together. The app matches travelers heading the same way.

Did you know?

  • The first friction match was an accident: English chemist John Walker noticed a coated stick burst into flame when he scraped it on the hearth in 1826 - and he never bothered to patent it.

Word origin

Two separate roots fused: the fire sense from Old French 'mesche' (a lamp wick), via Latin and Greek for wick; the 'equal/pair' sense from Old English 'gemæcca' (mate, companion).

Remember it

One word, two stories: a striking MATCH for a fire and a winning MATCH for a game - both about who or what meets the moment.
