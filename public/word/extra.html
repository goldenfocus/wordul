EXTRA

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈɛk.strə/

EXTRA

What does "EXTRA" mean?

More than is usual, expected, or needed; additional.

Meanings

  1. Added to what is normal or required. We need an extra chair for the guest.
  2. A person hired to appear in the background of a film or play. She started in Hollywood as an extra in crowd scenes.
  3. To an unusual degree; especially. Be extra careful on the icy steps. informal
  4. Excessive, dramatic, or over the top in behaviour. Renting a limo for a coffee date is so extra. informal

Word origin

A shortening of Latin 'extra' meaning 'outside, beyond', itself probably from 'exterus' (outward); the slang sense of 'over-the-top' arose in late-20th-century English.
