VIRUS

noun · 2 syllables · /'vaɪ.rəs/

VIRUS

What does "VIRUS" mean?

A tiny infectious agent that replicates only inside the living cells of a host.

Meanings

  1. A submicroscopic infectious particle that can reproduce only inside a living host cell. The flu virus mutates enough each year to need a new vaccine.
  2. A malicious program that copies itself into other files or systems. The email attachment carried a virus that locked his files. technical
  3. A corrupting or rapidly spreading harmful influence. Cynicism spread through the office like a virus. figurative

Did you know?

  • Biologists still argue whether a virus is even alive: it has no metabolism and cannot copy itself without commandeering a host cell, sitting on the fuzzy border between chemistry and life.
  • Before it meant a microbe, 'virus' meant plain poison in Latin - English used the word for snake venom and toxic fluids long before germ theory existed.

Word origin

From Latin 'virus' (poison, venom, slimy liquid); applied in the 1890s to the newly discovered submicroscopic infectious agents.

Remember it

VIRUS keeps the Latin 'poison' inside it; think 'VI' (six) tiny invaders 'RUSh' your cells.
