WEEDY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'wiː.di/

WEEDY

What does "WEEDY" mean?

Full of or overgrown with weeds, or (of a person) thin and weak-looking.

Meanings

  1. Overgrown with or characterized by weeds. The abandoned lot was a weedy tangle by July.
  2. Thin, frail, or feeble in appearance, especially of a person. The weedy recruit struggled with the heavy pack. informal

Word origin

From 'weed' (Old English 'weod', a wild plant) plus the adjective suffix '-y'; the 'thin person' sense draws on the image of a spindly, straggling weed.

Remember it

WEEDY has a double-E like a long stretch of overgrown field - two weeds standing side by side in the middle of the word.
