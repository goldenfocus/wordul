FLUNK

verb · 1 syllable · /flʌŋk/

FLUNK

What does "FLUNK" mean?

To fail an exam, course, or test, or to give someone a failing grade.

Meanings

  1. To fail an examination, course, or assessment. He flunked chemistry and had to retake it over the summer. informal
  2. To give a student a failing grade. The professor flunked half the class on the final. informal
  3. To give up or back out of something. He flunked out of the program after one semester. informal

Word origin

An American college slang term from the early 19th century, possibly a blend of 'flinch' and 'funk' (meaning to shrink back in fear), capturing the idea of failing or backing out.

Remember it

FLUNK rhymes with 'sunk' - flunk a test and your grade is sunk.
