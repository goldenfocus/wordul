PAPAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'peɪ.pəl/

PAPAL

What does "PAPAL" mean?

Relating to the Pope or to the office and authority of the Pope.

Meanings

  1. Of or relating to the Pope, the papacy, or the Roman Catholic Church's leadership. The crowd waited for the papal address from the balcony.

Did you know?

  • The lofty word 'papal' traces back to one of a baby's first sounds: the title 'pope' comes from Latin 'papa', meaning 'father'.

Word origin

From Medieval Latin 'papalis', from Late Latin 'papa' (pope, literally 'father'), ultimately an affectionate child's word for father echoed across many languages.

Remember it

PAPAL keeps PAPA inside it - the Pope is the 'papa' (father) of the Church.
