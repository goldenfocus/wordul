KEBAB

noun · 2 syllables · /kɪ'bæb/

KEBAB

What does "KEBAB" mean?

A dish of small pieces of meat, and often vegetables, grilled or roasted on a skewer.

Meanings

  1. Pieces of meat, fish, or vegetables cooked on a skewer or spit, often over a flame. He threaded the marinated lamb and peppers onto a kebab.
  2. A doner kebab; spiced meat shaved from a vertical rotisserie, usually served in flatbread. After the match they grabbed a kebab from the late-night stall. informal

Word origin

From Arabic 'kabab' (roasted or fried meat), by way of Persian, Urdu, and Turkish 'kebap'; entered English in the 17th century.

Remember it

KEBAB is a near-palindrome: KEB-AB; picture the skewer running straight through the middle B.
