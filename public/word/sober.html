SOBER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈsoʊ.bər/

SOBER

What does "SOBER" mean?

Not affected by alcohol; serious, calm, and self-controlled.

Meanings

  1. Not drunk; not under the influence of alcohol. She stayed sober so she could drive everyone home.
  2. Serious, solemn, sensible, or free from excess. After the news, the mood turned sober.
  3. Plain and subdued, especially of color. He wore a sober grey suit to the funeral.
  4. To make or become serious or solemn; to bring back to a calmer state (often with 'up'). The accident sobered them up at once.

Word origin

From Latin 'sobrius' (not drunk, moderate), formed from 'se-' (without) and a root related to 'ebrius' (drunk); into Old French 'sobre', then Middle English.

Remember it

SOBER literally means 'without the ebri-' — Latin 'sobrius' is 'se' (without) plus the root of 'inebriated'.
