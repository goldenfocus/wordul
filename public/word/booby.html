BOOBY

noun · 2 syllables · /'buː.bi/

BOOBY

What does "BOOBY" mean?

A large tropical seabird of the gannet family, known for its clumsy gait on land.

Meanings

  1. A diving seabird of the genus Sula, found in tropical oceans, several species having brightly coloured feet. The blue-footed booby lifts each bright foot in turn during its mating dance.
  2. A foolish or stupid person; a dunce. Don't be a booby - read the instructions first. informal

Did you know?

  • A blue-footed booby's feet really are a health report: the colour comes from carotenoid pigments in its fish diet, and a well-fed bird flashes brighter blue feet to attract a mate.

Word origin

From Spanish 'bobo' meaning 'stupid' or 'clown', from Latin 'balbus' ('stammering'); sailors named the bird for its apparent foolishness in letting itself be caught.

Remember it

BOOBY ends in BY a 'Y' shape - picture the bird's webbed feet splayed like a Y.
