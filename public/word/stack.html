STACK

What does "STACK" mean?

A neat pile of objects arranged one on top of another.

Meanings

  1. An orderly heap of items placed one above another. A stack of unread books leaned beside her bed.
  2. To arrange or pile in a stack. He stacked the chairs after the meeting.
  3. A data structure where the last item added is the first removed (last-in, first-out). The function call pushes a new frame onto the stack. technical
  4. A tall industrial chimney. Smoke curled from the factory's twin stacks.

Did you know?

  • In computer science, a stack is 'last-in, first-out': the last plate you put on a pile is the first you take off, which is exactly how a program's call stack unwinds function calls.

Word origin

From Old Norse 'stakkr', meaning a haystack or pile, entering Middle English in the 13th century.
