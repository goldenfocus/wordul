AFTER

What does "AFTER" mean?

Following in time, place, or order; later than something else.

Meanings

  1. Later in time than; following. We'll talk after lunch.
  2. Behind in place or order; following in position. Z comes after Y.
  3. In pursuit of or in search of. The dog tore off after the squirrel.
  4. In imitation of or in honour of. She was named after her grandmother.
  5. Following the time when. Call me after you land.
  6. Later; subsequent, especially of years or parts of a ship. In after years she rarely spoke of it. archaic

Did you know?

  • A ship's 'aft' (the rear) and the everyday word 'after' grow from the same Old English root meaning 'behind, away' - one sense stayed at sea, the other moved into time.

Word origin

From Old English 'æfter', built on a Germanic comparative of 'af' (off, away) - so 'after' originally meant 'further off, behind', a sense it still carries in space and time.

Remember it

AFTER starts with AFT - the back of a ship - because 'after' originally meant 'behind'.
