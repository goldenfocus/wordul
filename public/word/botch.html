BOTCH

What does "BOTCH" mean?

To carry out a task badly or clumsily, ruining it through carelessness or lack of skill.

Meanings

  1. To spoil something by doing it clumsily or incompetently. They botched the repair and the leak got worse.
  2. A piece of clumsy or failed work; a mess. The whole launch was a complete botch. informal

Word origin

From Middle English 'bocchen' meaning 'to repair', of uncertain origin; the sense shifted over time from patching clumsily to spoiling outright.

Remember it

BOTCH rhymes with 'blotch' - a botched job leaves a blotch you can't wipe clean.
