FAITH

noun · 1 syllable · /feɪθ/

FAITH

What does "FAITH" mean?

Complete trust or confidence in someone or something.

Meanings

  1. Strong belief or trust, often without proof. She kept faith in her team even when they were losing.
  2. Belief in and devotion to a religion or its doctrines. He was raised in the Catholic faith.
  3. A particular religion or system of religious belief. People of every faith attended the vigil.
  4. Loyalty, sincerity, or fidelity to a promise. They acted in good faith throughout the deal.

Did you know?

  • 'Faith', 'fidelity', 'confide' and even 'fiancee' all grow from one Latin word, 'fides' (trust) - the same root in the Marine motto 'Semper Fidelis', 'always faithful'.

Word origin

From Latin 'fides' (trust, confidence) via Old French 'feid/feit'; 'fides' also gives English 'fidelity', 'confide', and 'fiance'.

Remember it

FAITH: Full Assurance In Trusting Hearts - or just note it ends in 'ith', the quiet sound of holding on.
