FORTH

adverb · 1 syllable · /fɔːrθ/

FORTH

What does "FORTH" mean?

Out into view, or onward in place or time.

Meanings

  1. Out from a starting point; away or forward. The knights rode forth at first light.
  2. Out into view or from concealment. She brought forth a letter she had hidden for years. formal
  3. Onward in time; from this point on. From that day forth they were inseparable. formal

Word origin

From Old English 'forth' (forward, onward), from the Proto-Germanic root meaning 'forward', related to 'for' and 'fore'.

Remember it

FORTH is FOR + TH, pushing forward; think of striding ForTH like the fourTH step that takes you out the door.

A little poem

Wordplay

What it teaches

Forth is the smallest brave word: it only asks you to leave where you are standing.
