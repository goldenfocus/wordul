ALONE

adjective · 2 syllables · /əˈloʊn/

ALONE

What does "ALONE" mean?

Having no one else present; on one's own.

Meanings

  1. Without other people; by oneself. She lived alone but was rarely lonely.
  2. Without help from anyone or anything else. He built the whole shed alone over one summer.
  3. Considered on its own, to the exclusion of others; only. You alone can decide what happens next.

Word origin

From Middle English 'al one', literally 'all one' — Old English 'eall' (all, wholly) plus 'ān' (one). The same fusion gave us 'lone' by clipping off the 'a'.

Remember it

ALONE is 'all one' worn smooth — all-one, one all by itself.
