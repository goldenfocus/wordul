DUSTY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈdʌsti/

DUSTY

What does "DUSTY" mean?

Covered with or full of dust; also a muted, faded shade of a color.

Meanings

  1. Covered with or full of fine dry particles of dust. The attic shelves were thick and dusty after years untouched.
  2. Of a muted, grayish, faded shade. She painted the room a soft dusty rose.
  3. Outdated, neglected, or long unused. He pulled a dusty old theory out of the archive. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'dust' (fine dry powder) plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

DUSTY = DUST + Y: a thin gray Y-coat over everything left alone.
