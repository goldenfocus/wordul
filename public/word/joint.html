JOINT

noun · 1 syllable · /dʒɔɪnt/

JOINT

What does "JOINT" mean?

A point where two parts are joined, especially where bones meet in the body.

Meanings

  1. A structure in the body where two bones connect, allowing movement. Years of running wore down the cartilage in his knee joint.
  2. A place or device where two parts are fitted or fastened together. The plumber sealed the leaking pipe joint with fresh tape.
  3. Shared by or involving two or more parties together. They opened a joint bank account after the wedding.
  4. A large cut of meat for roasting (chiefly British). She put the Sunday joint in the oven before church.
  5. A place or establishment, often informal or disreputable. It's a cheap little joint, but the noodles are the best in town. informal

Word origin

From Old French 'joint', past participle of 'joindre' ('to join'), from Latin 'jungere' ('to yoke, join together') - the same root as 'junction' and 'yoke'.

Remember it

JOINT comes from Latin 'jungere', to join - everywhere two things connect, from knees to pipes to accounts.
