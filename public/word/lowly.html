LOWLY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'loʊ.li/

LOWLY

What does "LOWLY" mean?

Low in rank, status, or importance; humble.

Meanings

  1. Low in rank, position, or social standing. She started as a lowly clerk and rose to run the firm.
  2. Humble or modest in manner and spirit. He kept a lowly heart even after the award. formal
  3. In a humble or modest manner. They bore themselves lowly before the king. archaic

Word origin

From Middle English 'lowely', from 'low' (of Old Norse 'lagr', 'low') plus the suffix '-ly'; the sense of humble grew from the literal sense of being low to the ground.

Remember it

LOWLY = LOW + LY: something kept low, all the way down low.
