INPUT

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈɪn.pʊt/

INPUT

What does "INPUT" mean?

What is put into a system, process, or device, or one's contribution of ideas.

Meanings

  1. Data or signals fed into a computer or device. The program crashed when it received unexpected input. technical
  2. Advice, ideas, or contributions offered to a decision or project. Thanks for your input - it changed how we framed the launch.
  3. Resources, energy, or material fed into a process or economic activity. Fertilizer and labor are the farm's main inputs. technical
  4. To enter data into a computer or device. She inputted the figures by hand from the old ledger. technical

Word origin

A compound of 'in' plus 'put'; an old dialectal and accounting term revived and popularized in the computer era to pair with 'output'.

Remember it

INPUT is what you PUT IN; OUTPUT is what comes out - the system's two doors.

A little poem

Feed it kindness and it answers kind;
the output is only the input refined.

couplet

Wordplay

  • I asked the computer for its honest input. It said, 'Garbage in, garbage out - and you've been very generous.'

What it teaches

Watch what you feed any system, mind or machine; the output never rises above its input.
