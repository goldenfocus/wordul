FRIED

verb · 1 syllable · /fraɪd/

FRIED

What does "FRIED" mean?

Cooked in hot oil or fat; the past tense and past participle of fry.

Meanings

  1. Cooked in hot fat or oil over direct heat. She fried the eggs until the edges turned crisp.
  2. Prepared by frying. A plate of fried chicken sat steaming on the table.
  3. Damaged by excessive heat or electricity; ruined. The power surge fried the motherboard. informal
  4. Mentally exhausted or overwhelmed. After three exams in one day, my brain was completely fried. informal

Word origin

Past tense of 'fry', from Old French 'frire', from Latin 'frigere' to roast or fry.

Remember it

FRIED contains the letters of 'fire' plus a D - heat is baked into the spelling.
