DICEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'daɪ.si/

DICEY

What does "DICEY" mean?

Risky, uncertain, or unpredictable; likely to go wrong.

Meanings

  1. Involving risk or unpredictability; not safe or certain. Driving over that old bridge in the storm felt a bit dicey. informal

Word origin

An informal 20th-century English coinage from 'dice', the plural of 'die', evoking the gamble of a throw; first recorded around the 1940s.

Remember it

DICEY = like rolling DICE; the outcome is left to chance.

A little poem

The plan was sound on paper, clean and dry-
but every road worth taking turns out dicey.

couplet

Wordplay

  • I asked the gambler if his shortcut was safe. He said it was a bit dicey - then he wasn't kidding.

What it teaches

Call a risk by its name before you take it; 'dicey' is honest in a way 'fine' never is.
