BOOTH

noun · 1 syllable · /buːθ/

BOOTH

What does "BOOTH" mean?

A small enclosed or partly enclosed compartment used for a single purpose or person.

Meanings

  1. A small partitioned space for a particular activity, such as voting, phoning, or being photographed. She stepped into the voting booth and drew the curtain.
  2. A stall or stand where goods are sold or services offered, as at a market or fair. We handed out flyers from a booth at the trade show.
  3. A partly enclosed seating area with a table, typically in a diner or restaurant. Let's grab the corner booth by the window.

Word origin

From Middle English 'bothe', borrowed from Old Norse 'búð' meaning 'dwelling' or 'stall', related to 'búa' ('to dwell').

Remember it

BOOTH = 'be both' enclosed: the double O are the two walls you sit between.
