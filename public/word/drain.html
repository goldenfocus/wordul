DRAIN

verb · 1 syllable · /dreɪn/

DRAIN

What does "DRAIN" mean?

To cause liquid to flow away gradually.

Meanings

  1. To make liquid flow off or out, leaving something dry or empty. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
  2. To deplete the strength, resources, or emotion of someone or something. The long argument drained him completely. figurative
  3. A channel or pipe carrying off surplus liquid. Leaves clogged the drain and the street flooded.

Word origin

From Old English 'dreahnian' (to strain, filter, drain off), related to 'dry'.

Remember it

DRAIN ends in RAIN - water flowing away is exactly what a drain is for.
