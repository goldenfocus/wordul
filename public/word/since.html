SINCE

preposition · 1 syllable · /sɪns/

SINCE

What does "SINCE" mean?

From a specified past time until now; also, because.

Meanings

  1. In or during the period after a stated past time up to the present. She has lived here since 2010.
  2. From the time when. It's been years since we last spoke.
  3. Because; for the reason that. Since you're already up, could you get the door?
  4. From then until now; ago. He left town and hasn't been seen since.

Word origin

A contraction of Middle English 'sithen', from Old English 'siththan' (after that, from the time that), built from 'sith' (after) and 'than' (then).

Remember it

SINCE points two ways: back in time ('since Monday') or to a cause ('since you asked').

What it teaches

The same word can mark a cause or a moment; meaning depends on which way you look.
