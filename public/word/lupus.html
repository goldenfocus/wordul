LUPUS

noun · 2 syllables · /'luː.pəs/

LUPUS

What does "LUPUS" mean?

A chronic autoimmune disease in which the body's immune system attacks its own tissues.

Meanings

  1. An autoimmune disorder (chiefly systemic lupus erythematosus) that can inflame skin, joints, kidneys, and other organs. Her lupus flared after a week in the summer sun. technical
  2. Latin for 'wolf'; also the name of a southern constellation, the Wolf. The constellation Lupus crouches beside Centaurus in the southern sky. technical

Did you know?

  • Lupus is Latin for 'wolf': medieval physicians thought the disease's facial rash resembled a wolf's bite, and the classic 'butterfly' rash across the cheeks was once likened to a wolf's mask.

Word origin

From Latin 'lupus', 'wolf'; the disease was named in medieval times because the facial skin lesions were thought to resemble a wolf's bite or a wolf-like mask across the face.

Remember it

LUPUS is Latin for wolf - picture an immune system that turns wolf and bites the body it was meant to guard.
