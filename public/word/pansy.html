PANSY

noun · 2 syllables · /'pæn.zi/

PANSY

What does "PANSY" mean?

A small garden flower with broad, brightly colored petals and a face-like marking.

Meanings

  1. A cultivated violet with large, velvety, often multicoloured flowers and a dark central blotch. She planted pansies along the path for winter colour.

Did you know?

  • The cheerful pansy is named for a thought: its name comes from the French 'pensee', 'a thought', because the flower stood for remembering an absent loved one.

Word origin

From Middle French 'pensee', meaning 'thought' or 'remembrance' (from 'penser', to think), because the flower was a token of thinking of someone.

Remember it

PANSY hides 'pense' (French 'think') - the flower of thought, given to mean 'thinking of you'.

Wordplay

  • I gave my friend a pansy and told her it came with a thought. She said that's the most I'd ever put into a gift.

What it teaches

The smallest tokens carry the largest meanings; a flower can stand in for a thought you couldn't say.
