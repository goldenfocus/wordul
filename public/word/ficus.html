noun · 2 syllables · /'faɪ.kəs/

FICUS

What does "FICUS" mean?

A genus of tropical trees and shrubs including figs and many houseplants.

Meanings

  1. Any plant of the genus Ficus, which includes fig trees, the rubber plant, and the weeping fig. The lobby's tall ficus dropped half its leaves the week the heating broke.

Did you know?

  • Most Ficus species are locked into a partnership with a single matching kind of fig wasp - the wasp pollinates the fig from the inside, and neither can reproduce without the other.

Word origin

Directly from Latin 'ficus' meaning fig tree or fig, the same root that gives English 'fig'.

Remember it

FICUS = FIg + cUS; the fig tree is its most famous member.
