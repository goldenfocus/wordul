PROSE

noun · 1 syllable · /proʊz/

PROSE

What does "PROSE" mean?

Ordinary written or spoken language without the structured rhythm or line breaks of verse.

Meanings

  1. Written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure. She wrote the report in plain prose so anyone could follow it.
  2. Dull, matter-of-fact, or commonplace quality or expression. The grand ceremony soon dissolved into the prose of cleanup and paperwork. figurative
  3. To write or speak in a tedious, drawn-out way. He prosed on about his garden until the guests grew restless. archaic

Word origin

From Latin 'prosa', short for 'prosa oratio' meaning 'straightforward speech', from 'prorsus' ('straight ahead'); via Old French 'prose' into English.

Remember it

PROSE reads straight across the page; its Latin parent 'prorsus' literally means 'straight ahead'.
