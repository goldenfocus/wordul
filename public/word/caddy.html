CADDY

noun · 2 syllables · /'kæd.i/

CADDY

What does "CADDY" mean?

A person who carries a golfer's clubs and offers advice during a round.

Meanings

  1. An attendant who carries a golfer's bag of clubs and helps read the course. His caddy talked him out of the driver and handed him a three-wood.
  2. A small container or rack for holding and organizing items. She kept the sponges and soap in a caddy by the sink.
  3. To act as a caddy for a golfer. He caddied at the club every summer through college.

Word origin

From French 'cadet' ('younger son, junior'), which in Scots became 'cadie' or 'caddie' meaning an errand boy or porter, later specialized to the golf attendant; the 'tea caddy' container sense is unrelated, coming from Malay 'kati', a unit of weight.
