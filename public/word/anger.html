ANGER

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈæŋ.ɡər/

ANGER

What does "ANGER" mean?

A strong feeling of displeasure or hostility provoked by a perceived wrong.

Meanings

  1. A strong feeling of annoyance, displeasure, or hostility. She could hear the anger rising in his voice.
  2. To provoke such a feeling in someone. It angered him that no one had asked his opinion.

Word origin

From Old Norse 'angr' meaning grief or distress, related to a Germanic root for narrowness and constriction; the sense narrowed from sorrow to wrath in Middle English.
