CLEAR

adjective · 1 syllable · /klɪər/

CLEAR

What does "CLEAR" mean?

Easy to perceive, understand, or see through; free from obstruction or cloudiness.

Meanings

  1. Transparent; allowing light through without distortion. The water was so clear you could see the pebbles below.
  2. Easy to understand or perceive; unambiguous. She gave clear instructions to the new staff.
  3. Free of obstacles, clouds, or anything blocking the way. The road ahead was clear for miles.
  4. To remove obstructions or unwanted things from a place. Please clear the table after dinner.
  5. To pass over or through without touching. The horse cleared the final fence.

Word origin

From Latin 'clarus' meaning bright, clear, or famous; into Old French 'cler' and then Middle English 'clere'.

Remember it

CLEAR has an EAR inside - when something is clear, you hear it plainly the first time.
