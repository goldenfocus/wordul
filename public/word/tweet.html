noun · 1 syllable · /twiːt/

TWEET

What does "TWEET" mean?

A short high chirping sound made by a small bird.

Meanings

  1. A weak, thin chirping note of a small bird. A single tweet came from the hedge before the whole flock answered.
  2. To make such a chirping sound. Sparrows tweeted in the eaves at first light.
  3. A short message posted on the social platform formerly called Twitter (now X). Her tweet about the typo got more replies than the article itself. informal
  4. To post such a message online. He tweeted the score before the final whistle had even blown. informal

Did you know?

  • The bird-chirp word 'tweet' was so reshaped by social media that the Oxford English Dictionary added its 'post online' verb sense in 2013, only seven years after Twitter launched.

Word origin

Imitative (onomatopoeic) of the thin sound of a small bird, recorded in English from the early 1800s; the social-media sense was coined for Twitter in 2006.

Remember it

TWEET is what a bird does and what a Twitter user does - both are short bursts you can't take back.
