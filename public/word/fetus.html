noun · 2 syllables · /'fiː.təs/

FETUS

What does "FETUS" mean?

An unborn mammal in the later stages of development before birth.

Meanings

  1. A developing human or animal offspring after the embryonic stage, when major structures have formed. By the second trimester the fetus can already hear muffled sounds from outside. technical

Did you know?

  • The word only applies after a deadline: a human is an embryo until the end of week 8 of pregnancy, and only then officially becomes a fetus.

Word origin

From Latin 'fetus' meaning offspring, bringing forth, or hatching of young, related to 'fecundus' (fruitful).

Remember it

FETUS keeps the Latin spelling - one 'e', no British 'oe' like the older 'foetus'.
