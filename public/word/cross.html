CROSS

noun · 1 syllable · /krɒs/

CROSS

What does "CROSS" mean?

A figure or structure of two lines or beams intersecting at right angles.

Meanings

  1. A mark or shape formed by two intersecting lines. She signed the form with a cross because she could not write.
  2. The wooden structure on which Jesus was crucified, used as the central symbol of Christianity. A small silver cross hung at her throat.
  3. To go from one side of something to the other. Look both ways before you cross the street.
  4. To breed two different varieties or species together. They crossed a donkey with a horse to get a mule.
  5. Annoyed or mildly angry. Don't be cross with me - it was an honest mistake. informal
  6. A mixture or blend of two different things. The dog was a cross between a poodle and a terrier.

Word origin

From Latin 'crux' (cross, gallows), borrowed via Old Irish 'cros' into Old English; it replaced the native word 'rood'.

Remember it

CROSS has a double S at its center like the X where two roads cross - and crossing the street is exactly the verb.
