FECAL

What does "FECAL" mean?

Relating to or consisting of feces, the body's solid waste.

Meanings

  1. Of, relating to, or composed of excrement. Water samples were tested for fecal contamination. technical

Did you know?

  • Before it meant bodily waste, the Latin root of 'fecal' - 'faex' - simply meant the dregs or sediment that settle at the bottom of a barrel of wine.

Word origin

From Latin 'faex' (plural 'faeces') meaning 'dregs or sediment', the residue left at the bottom of a liquid; the medical sense narrowed to bodily waste over time.

Remember it

FECAL sounds like 'feces' with an L - same Latin dregs, just dressed as an adjective.
