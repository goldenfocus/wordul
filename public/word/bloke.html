BLOKE

noun · 1 syllable · /bloʊk/

BLOKE

What does "BLOKE" mean?

An informal British and Australian term for a man or guy.

Meanings

  1. A man, especially an ordinary or down-to-earth one. He's a decent bloke who'd help anyone out. informal

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, first recorded in British slang around the mid-19th century; possibly from Shelta, the cant of Irish Travellers, or from Romani.

Remember it

BLOKE rhymes with 'oak' - picture a sturdy, ordinary bloke leaning on an oak tree, pint in hand.

A little poem

No title, no grand story to be told -
just a bloke who shows up when the nights turn cold.

couplet

Wordplay

What it teaches

Being called an ordinary good bloke is a higher honour than most fancy titles.
