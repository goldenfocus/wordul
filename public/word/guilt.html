GUILT

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡɪlt/

GUILT

What does "GUILT" mean?

The fact of having done wrong, or the painful feeling of having done so.

Meanings

  1. The state of having committed an offense or wrong. The jury found no evidence of his guilt.
  2. A feeling of having done something wrong or failed an obligation. She felt a pang of guilt for skipping the call.

Word origin

From Old English 'gylt' meaning a crime, sin, or fault, of uncertain origin and not clearly related to any word outside English.

Remember it

GUILT is GUILD with the D swapped for a T - a guild builds together; guilt you carry alone.
