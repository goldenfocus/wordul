BASAL

adjective · 2 syllables · /'beɪ.səl/

BASAL

What does "BASAL" mean?

Forming or relating to a base; fundamental or at the lowest level.

Meanings

  1. Relating to, situated at, or forming a base. The plant's leaves spread in a basal rosette at ground level. technical
  2. Of physiology: pertaining to the minimum level of activity, as in basal metabolic rate. Doctors measure basal metabolic rate while the body is fully at rest. technical
  3. Fundamental; constituting a foundation or starting point. These are the basal skills every later lesson builds on. formal

Word origin

From 'base' (the bottom or foundation), from Latin 'basis' via Greek 'basis' (a stepping, foundation), plus the adjective suffix '-al'.

Remember it

BASAL = BASE + AL: it's the adjective form of 'base'. Picture the BASe of a column, the lowest stone.
