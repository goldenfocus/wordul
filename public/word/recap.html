noun · 2 syllables · /'riː.kæp/

RECAP

What does "RECAP" mean?

A short summary of the main points of something already said or shown.

Meanings

  1. A concise restatement of the key facts or events. Here's a quick recap of what we decided in the meeting. informal
  2. To state again briefly; to summarize. Let me recap the plan before we move on. informal
  3. To put a new tread on a worn tire by bonding fresh rubber to the casing. The fleet recaps its truck tires instead of buying new ones. technical

Did you know?

  • To 'recap' comes from 'recapitulate' - and its Latin root 'capitulum' means a little head or chapter heading, the same root that gives us 'capital', 'chapter', and 'captain'.

Word origin

A 20th-century shortening of 'recapitulate', from Latin 're-' (again) plus 'capitulum' (a little head or chapter heading), so to recap is to run through the main headings again.

Remember it

RECAP = put a 'cap' back on it: you re-CAP the story by sealing it into a short summary.
