DRAPE

verb · 1 syllable · /dreɪp/

DRAPE

What does "DRAPE" mean?

To arrange cloth loosely over or around something.

Meanings

  1. To hang or arrange fabric in loose folds over something. She draped a shawl over her shoulders against the chill.
  2. To let a part of the body hang loosely and gracefully. He draped an arm across the back of the couch.
  3. The way fabric hangs or falls in folds. The dress had a beautiful drape that caught the light.
  4. A long curtain (often plural). Close the drapes before the film starts.

Word origin

From Old French 'draper' (to weave, full cloth), from 'drap' (cloth), from Late Latin 'drappus'.

Remember it

DRAPE is GRAPE with a D - imagine cloth hanging in soft, rounded folds like a bunch of grapes.
