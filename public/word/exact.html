EXACT

adjective · 2 syllables · /ɪɡˈzækt/

EXACT

What does "EXACT" mean?

Correct in every detail; precise and accurate.

Meanings

  1. Strictly accurate and complete, without approximation. Give me the exact time the train left.
  2. Marked by strict rigour or carefulness. She is exact in everything she does.
  3. To demand and obtain something by force or authority. The regime exacted heavy taxes from the farmers. formal

Did you know?

  • The 'exact' that means precise and the 'exact' that means to demand by force share a root: Latin 'exigere', 'to weigh out' - which also produced 'exigent' and even 'essay' (a weighing of ideas).

Word origin

From Latin 'exactus', past participle of 'exigere' meaning 'to drive out, weigh, measure precisely', from 'ex-' (out) plus 'agere' (to drive).

Remember it

EX-ACT: an exact answer is the act done EXactly right, nothing extra.
