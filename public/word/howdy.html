HOWDY

interjection · 2 syllables · /'haʊ.di/

HOWDY

What does "HOWDY" mean?

An informal, friendly greeting meaning hello, associated with the American South and West.

Meanings

  1. A casual greeting equivalent to 'hello', especially in the southern and western United States. Howdy, folks - take a seat and stay a while. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Howdy' is a fossilized question: it's worn down from 'How do ye?', so every cowboy greeting is technically asking how you are.
  • At Texas A&M, 'Howdy' is the official school greeting, used in everything from class welcomes to university events.

Word origin

A contraction of the older greeting 'How do ye?' or 'How do you do?', worn down over time into a single friendly word.

Remember it

HOWDY = 'How do ye?' squeezed flat. The 'HOW D' at the front is the question still hiding inside the hello.

Wordplay

What it teaches
