BUNCH

noun · 1 syllable · /bʌntʃ/

BUNCH

What does "BUNCH" mean?

A number of things growing or grouped closely together.

Meanings

  1. A cluster of things of the same kind fastened or growing together. She carried a bunch of grapes and a bunch of keys in the same hand.
  2. An informal group of people. The whole bunch of us crammed into one booth. informal
  3. A large amount or number (informal). There's a bunch of work left before Friday. informal
  4. To gather or draw together into a cluster or fold. The fabric bunched at the elbow whenever she bent her arm.

Word origin

From Middle English 'bunche' (a swelling, hump, or cluster), of uncertain origin, possibly imitative of a lump or knob.

Remember it

BUNCH ends in -UNCH like CRUNCH and MUNCH: a bunch of grapes you munch.
