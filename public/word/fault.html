FAULT

noun · 1 syllable · /fɔːlt/

FAULT

What does "FAULT" mean?

A defect or imperfection, or responsibility for something wrong.

Meanings

  1. Responsibility for an error or wrongdoing. The collision was entirely the driver's fault.
  2. A defect, flaw, or imperfection. The diamond had a tiny fault near its base.
  3. A fracture in rock where blocks of the Earth's crust have moved relative to each other. The San Andreas Fault runs hundreds of miles through California. technical
  4. In tennis, a serve that fails to land in the correct area. A double fault handed her opponent the point. technical
  5. To criticize or find error in. You can't fault her for trying so hard.

Did you know?

  • The 'l' in 'fault' is a learned forgery: medieval scholars added it to make the word look more like its Latin ancestor 'fallere', and for centuries the letter sat there completely silent before people started actually saying it.

Word origin

From Latin 'fallere' meaning 'to deceive or fail', via Old French 'faute'; the silent then re-spoken 'l' was added later to mirror the Latin, which is why pronunciation has wandered over the centuries.

Remember it

FAULT hides 'vault' minus the V - an earthquake fault is where the ground vaults past itself.
