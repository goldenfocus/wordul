CRAWL

verb · 1 syllable · /krɔːl/

CRAWL

What does "CRAWL" mean?

To move slowly on hands and knees or with the body close to the ground.

Meanings

  1. To move along on hands and knees or with the body near the ground. The baby learned to crawl before she could walk.
  2. To move or progress very slowly. Traffic crawled along the highway after the accident.
  3. A fast swimming stroke performed face-down with alternating arm strokes. She won the race doing the front crawl.
  4. Of software, to systematically visit web pages to index them. Search engines crawl billions of pages every day. technical
  5. A round of visiting several pubs or bars in one outing. We organized a pub crawl for his birthday. informal

Word origin

From Old Norse 'krafla', meaning 'to claw or scrabble with the hands'; entered Middle English as 'craulen'.

Remember it

CRAWL ends in -AWL like a tool that scratches the floor; picture clawing forward, low and slow.
