GUILD

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡɪld/

GUILD

What does "GUILD" mean?

An association of people in the same trade or craft, formed for mutual support and shared standards.

Meanings

  1. A medieval or later organization of craftsmen or merchants regulating their trade. Only a member of the weavers' guild could sell cloth in the town.
  2. Any modern association of people sharing a profession or interest. The writers' guild negotiated new contract terms.
  3. In online games, an organized group of players who cooperate. Her guild cleared the raid for the first time on Friday. informal

Did you know?

  • 'Guild' comes from an old word for a payment or contribution: members literally paid into a shared pot, and that same root grew into the modern word 'yield'.

Word origin

From Old Norse 'gildi' meaning payment, guild, or fellowship, related to the verb 'yield' and to Old English 'gild' meaning a contribution or tax paid into a common fund.
