BEEFY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'biː.fi/

BEEFY

What does "BEEFY" mean?

Muscular, solid, and powerfully built.

Meanings

  1. Heavily muscled, thickset, or burly. Two beefy bouncers stood at the club's entrance. informal
  2. Substantial, powerful, or impressively capable, of a machine or component. The truck has a beefy engine that hauls without complaint. informal
  3. Tasting strongly of beef. The stew had a rich, beefy broth.

Word origin

From 'beef' plus '-y'; 'beef' comes through Old French 'boef' from Latin 'bos' (ox), the same root as 'bovine' - so a 'beefy' person is, etymologically, ox-like.

Remember it

BEEFY = built like BEEF, with the muscle and the -Y to match; double-E for the double effort it took.
