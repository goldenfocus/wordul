adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈpɜːr.ki/

PERKY

What does "PERKY" mean?

Cheerful, lively, and full of brisk energy.

Meanings

  1. Briskly cheerful, lively, and self-confident in manner. The waiter greeted us with a perky good morning.
  2. Pleasantly firm and upturned in appearance. The puppy had two perky little ears.

Word origin

From the verb 'perk' (to raise briskly, as in 'perk up'), of Middle English origin and uncertain root, plus the adjective suffix '-y'.

Remember it

PERKY: when you PERK up, you go from droopy to bright-eyed.
