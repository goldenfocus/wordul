SWEEP

verb · 1 syllable · /swiːp/

SWEEP

What does "SWEEP" mean?

To clean a surface by brushing away dust or debris with a broom.

Meanings

  1. To clear dust or litter from a floor or surface using a broom or brush. She swept the kitchen before the guests arrived.
  2. To move swiftly and smoothly over or across an area. The searchlight swept across the dark harbour.
  3. To win every game, contest, or seat in a series. The home team swept the series in four straight games. informal
  4. A long, smooth, curving movement or extent. From the cliff you could see the whole sweep of the bay.
  5. A person whose trade is cleaning soot from chimneys. A boy sweep would climb inside the flue itself.

Word origin

From Middle English 'swepen', related to Old English 'swapan' (to sweep, drive) and the same Germanic root that gives 'swoop'.
