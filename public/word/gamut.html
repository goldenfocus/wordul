noun · 2 syllables · /'ɡæm.ət/

What does "GAMUT" mean?

The complete range or scope of something.

Meanings

  1. The full extent or range of something, from one end to the other. Her emotions ran the gamut from joy to despair in a single afternoon.
  2. In music, the entire range of notes a voice or instrument can produce, or the whole musical scale. The aria demands the full gamut of a soprano's range. technical

Did you know?

  • 'Gamut' is squeezed from two medieval music terms - 'gamma' (the lowest note) and 'ut' (the first solfege syllable, ancestor of 'do') - both from the 11th-century monk Guido of Arezzo, the same teacher who gave us do-re-mi.

Word origin

A contraction of 'gamma ut' from medieval music: 'gamma' was the lowest note of the scale and 'ut' the first of the six syllables (ut, re, mi, fa, sol, la) named by Guido of Arezzo, so 'gamut' came to mean the whole range.

Remember it

GAMUT = GAMma + UT, the bottom note plus the first syllable - hold the whole musical scale in five letters.
