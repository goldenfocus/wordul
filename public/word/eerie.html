adjective · 2 syllables · /'ɪə.ri/

EERIE

What does "EERIE" mean?

Strange and frightening in a way that suggests the supernatural.

Meanings

  1. Uncanny, weird, and unsettling, often hinting at something supernatural. An eerie silence fell over the fog-bound harbor.

Word origin

From Middle English 'eri', meaning fearful or timid, from Old English 'earg' (cowardly); the sense flipped from feeling fear to inspiring it.
