ALPHA

What does "ALPHA" mean?

The first letter of the Greek alphabet, often meaning the first or foremost.

Meanings

  1. The first letter of the Greek alphabet (Α, α). In the equation, alpha stands for the angle of incidence.
  2. The dominant individual in a social group of animals or, informally, people. The pack's alpha ate before the others approached the kill.
  3. An early, internal release of software, before the more stable beta stage. The game is still in alpha, so expect crashes. technical
  4. First, principal, or earliest in a series. The alpha version shipped to a handful of testers.

Did you know?

  • The letter A you are reading began life as an ox: 'alpha' comes from Phoenician 'aleph' ('ox'), and the original glyph was an ox-head that later got flipped on its side into our A.
  • The word 'alphabet' is literally 'alpha' plus 'beta' bolted together - the first two Greek letters standing in for the whole set.

Word origin

From Greek 'alpha', the name of the first letter, itself borrowed from Phoenician 'ʾalp' meaning 'ox' — the letter's shape descends from a pictograph of an ox's head.

Remember it

ALPHA = A, the alphabet's first letter; both words start with the same A for a reason.
