LEAST

adjective · 1 syllable · /liːst/

LEAST

What does "LEAST" mean?

Smallest in size, amount, or degree; the superlative of 'little'.

Meanings

  1. Smallest or lowest in importance, amount, or degree. He chose the least expensive option on the menu.
  2. To the smallest extent or degree. She was the least surprised of anyone in the room.
  3. The smallest amount or thing, often in 'at least' or 'the least'. The least you could do is apologize.

Word origin

From Old English 'læst', a contraction of 'læsest', the superlative of 'læs' meaning less; the irregular series little-less-least has no shared root with 'little'.

Remember it

LEAST is the end of the line LESS-LEAST: smaller, then smallest. It even hides 'EAT' - the least you can eat.
