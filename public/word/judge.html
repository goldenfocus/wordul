JUDGE

noun · 1 syllable · /dʒʌdʒ/

JUDGE

What does "JUDGE" mean?

A public official with authority to hear and decide cases in a court of law.

Meanings

  1. An officer who presides over a court and decides questions of law and fact. The judge instructed the jury before they retired to deliberate.
  2. A person who decides the winner of a competition or who assesses merit. A panel of three judges scored each dish on taste and presentation.
  3. To form an opinion about, or to decide officially. Don't judge a book by its cover.

Word origin

From Latin 'iudex' (one who points out the law), combining 'ius' (law, right) and a root of 'dicere' (to say); via Old French 'juge' into English.
