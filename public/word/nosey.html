NOSEY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'noʊ.zi/

NOSEY

What does "NOSEY" mean?

Prying into other people's affairs; inquisitive in an intrusive way.

Meanings

  1. Too interested in other people's private business. Our nosey neighbour somehow knows everyone's business on the street. informal

Word origin

A variant spelling of 'nosy', from 'nose' plus '-y'; the image is of someone sticking their nose into matters that are not their own.

Remember it

NOSEY literally has NOSE in it - a nosey person leads with their nose into your business.

A little poem

She knew the wedding date before the groom-
her nose arrived ahead of her in every room.

couplet

What it teaches

Curiosity becomes intrusion the moment it stops being about understanding and starts being about ownership.
