TOTAL

noun · 2 syllables · /'toʊ.təl/

TOTAL

What does "TOTAL" mean?

The whole amount or sum of things added together.

Meanings

  1. The complete sum reached by adding everything together. The total at the bottom of the receipt made him wince.
  2. Complete; absolute; comprising the whole. The plan was a total disaster from the first day.
  3. To add up to a given sum, or to compute that sum. The damages totalled nearly a million dollars.
  4. To wreck a vehicle so badly that repair costs exceed its value. She walked away unhurt, but the car was totaled. informal

Word origin

From Medieval Latin 'totalis', 'entire', from Latin 'totus', meaning 'whole' or 'all'; into Old French 'total', then English.

Remember it

TOTAL keeps 'to all' inside it - a total gathers everything 'to all' of it into one whole.

A little poem

Wordplay

What it teaches

A total is just many small things refusing to be ignored; nothing is too minor to count.
