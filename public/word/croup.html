CROUP

What does "CROUP" mean?

A childhood illness that causes a harsh barking cough and difficulty breathing.

Meanings

  1. An infection of the upper airway in young children, marked by a barking cough, hoarseness, and a noisy strained breath. The toddler woke at midnight with the barking cough of croup. technical
  2. The rump or hindquarters of a horse, behind the saddle. He patted the mare on the croup before mounting.

Word origin

The illness sense is from Scots dialect 'croup' (to croak or cry hoarsely), of imitative origin; the horse sense is a separate word from Old French 'croupe' (rump).

Remember it

CROUP sounds like the hoarse CROAK-cough it names - and the same letters sit in 'group', the kids most likely to catch it.
