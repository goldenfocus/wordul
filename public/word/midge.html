noun · 1 syllable · /mɪdʒ/

MIDGE

What does "MIDGE" mean?

A tiny two-winged fly, often found in swarms near water, some species of which bite.

Meanings

  1. Any of various very small flies, especially the non-biting and biting midges that gather in swarms near water. Clouds of midges hovered over the still pond at dusk.

Word origin

From Old English 'mycg' (gnat, midge), from a Germanic root shared with German 'Mucke' (midge); ultimately echoic or related to words for buzzing insects.

Remember it

MIDGE = 'midget' with the 't' bitten off: a midge is a tiny midget of an insect.
