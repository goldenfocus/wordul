GLOOM

noun · 1 syllable · /ɡluːm/

GLOOM

What does "GLOOM" mean?

Partial darkness, or a state of low spirits and despondency.

Meanings

  1. Darkness or near-darkness; deep shade. Figures moved in the gloom of the cellar.
  2. A feeling of sadness, hopelessness, or low spirits. A gloom settled over the team after the loss.
  3. To look or be sullen, or to grow dark (chiefly literary). The sky gloomed before the storm. archaic

Word origin

From Middle English 'gloumen' meaning 'to look sullen, become dark'; the noun sense of 'darkness' was reinforced by Milton's use in 'Paradise Lost' in the 17th century.

Remember it
