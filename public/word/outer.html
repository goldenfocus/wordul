OUTER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈaʊ.tər/

OUTER

What does "OUTER" mean?

Situated on the outside or farther from the centre.

Meanings

  1. Located on or toward the outside; external or more distant from the middle. The outer ring of seats had the cheapest tickets.
  2. Relating to the world beyond Earth's atmosphere, as in 'outer space'. The satellite drifted into the silence of outer space.

Did you know?

  • The word 'outer' anchors the name of real international law: the 1967 Outer Space Treaty bans any nation from claiming the Moon or planets as its own territory.

Word origin

From Old English 'utera', a comparative form of 'ut' meaning 'out'; literally 'more out', formed the same way as 'inner' is built from 'in'.

Remember it

OUTER is just 'OUT' with '-er' on the end - the more 'out' something is, the more outer it gets.
