ABATE

verb · 2 syllables · /ə'beɪt/

ABATE

What does "ABATE" mean?

To become less intense or widespread; to diminish or reduce in force.

Meanings

  1. To grow less; to subside in strength or amount. We waited on the porch until the storm began to abate.
  2. To lessen, lower, or reduce something deliberately. The new filters abate the pollution leaving the plant.
  3. In law, to make void, end, or suppress (a nuisance or legal action). The court ordered the company to abate the nuisance within thirty days. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'abatre' ('to beat down'), from Vulgar Latin 'abbattere', from Latin 'ad-' (to) plus 'battere' (to beat).

Remember it

A-BATE: think of a storm that 'ate' less and less of the sky until it died down - it abated.
