SPOUT

noun · 1 syllable · /spaʊt/

SPOUT

What does "SPOUT" mean?

A tube or lip through which liquid is poured or discharged in a stream.

Meanings

  1. A projecting nozzle or channel that directs a flow of liquid. Tea trickled from the cracked spout onto the saucer.
  2. To eject liquid forcefully in a jet or stream. The whale surfaced and spouted a misty plume.
  3. To talk at length, often pompously or without much thought. He spouted statistics nobody had asked for. informal

Word origin

From Middle English 'spouten', probably from Middle Dutch 'spouten' (to gush or spurt), of imitative origin echoing the sound of jetting liquid.
