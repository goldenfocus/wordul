HYENA

noun · 3 syllables · /haɪ'i.nə/

HYENA

What does "HYENA" mean?

A dog-like carnivorous mammal of Africa and Asia, known for its powerful jaws and eerie call.

Meanings

  1. A carnivorous mammal of the family Hyaenidae, with a sloping back and bone-crushing jaws. A clan of hyenas circled the lion's kill at dusk.
  2. A cruel, greedy, or contemptible person, used as an insult. The tabloids descended on the scandal like hyenas. figurative

Did you know?

  • Spotted hyena society is run by females, who outrank and outweigh the males - and the female's anatomy so closely mimics the male's that early naturalists wrongly believed every hyena could switch sex.

Word origin

From Greek 'hyaina', formed from 'hys' (pig) because the animal's bristly mane reminded the Greeks of a hog's; via Latin 'hyaena' into English.

Remember it

HYENA opens with a 'HY' like a high, eerie laugh - and the laughing hyena is exactly the one you picture.
