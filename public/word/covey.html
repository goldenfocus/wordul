COVEY

noun · 2 syllables · /'kʌv.i/

COVEY

What does "COVEY" mean?

A small flock of game birds, especially partridges or quail.

Meanings

  1. A brood or small flock of partridges, quail, or similar game birds. A covey of quail burst from the brush as the dog approached.
  2. A small group of people or things gathered together. A covey of reporters waited at the courthouse steps. figurative

Did you know?

  • A 'covey' literally means 'a brood that was sat on': it comes from Old French for hatching, from Latin 'cubare', to lie down - the same root behind 'incubate'.

Word origin

From Old French 'covee' (a brood), from 'cover' (to sit on, to brood, to hatch), from Latin 'cubare' (to lie down) - the covey is, literally, what was brooded.
