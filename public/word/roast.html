ROAST

verb · 1 syllable · /roʊst/

ROAST

What does "ROAST" mean?

To cook food, especially meat, by prolonged exposure to dry heat in an oven or over a fire.

Meanings

  1. To cook by dry radiant heat, as in an oven or over flame. We'll roast the chicken for ninety minutes.
  2. A cut of meat cooked or intended to be cooked by roasting. She pulled the Sunday roast from the oven.
  3. To mock or criticize someone harshly, often affectionately at an event. His friends roasted him mercilessly at the retirement party. informal
  4. To dry and brown coffee beans, nuts, or peppers with heat. They roast the beans in small batches each morning.

Did you know?

  • The comedic 'roast' - turning the heat on a guest of honour - grew out of the New York Friars Club's testimonial dinners, where affectionate insults replaced praise from the 1950s onward.

Word origin

From Old French 'rostir', of Germanic origin, related to the notion of cooking on a grate or gridiron.
