LOOPY

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈluː.pi/

LOOPY

What does "LOOPY" mean?

Slightly crazy, dazed, or eccentric; or full of loops.

Meanings

  1. Mildly crazy, silly, or muddled in the head. The anesthetic left her feeling loopy for an hour. informal
  2. Having or forming many loops or curls. She signed her name with a loopy, flourishing scrawl.

Word origin

From 'loop' (a curve crossing itself), of uncertain origin possibly Celtic, plus '-y'; the 'slightly crazy' sense arose in early 20th-century American slang, evoking a mind that goes in circles.

Remember it
