HINGE

noun · 1 syllable · /hɪndʒ/

HINGE

What does "HINGE" mean?

A jointed device on which a door or lid turns, or a central point on which something depends.

Meanings

  1. A movable joint or mechanism by which a door, gate, or lid swings. The gate squeaked on its rusty hinge.
  2. To depend on or be contingent upon something. The whole plan hinges on the weather. figurative
  3. A natural joint that allows movement in one plane, as in the body. The knee works like a hinge. technical

Did you know?

  • Your knees and elbows are officially 'hinge joints': anatomists use the same word as the hardware store because, like a door, they bend mainly one way.

Word origin

From Middle English 'henge', related to 'hang'; the word names the thing on which a door hangs and turns.

Remember it

A HINGE lets a door swing because it can HANG and swING - the word holds both halves of the motion.
