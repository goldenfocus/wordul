NOOSE

noun · 1 syllable · /nuːs/

NOOSE

What does "NOOSE" mean?

A loop in a rope with a sliding knot that tightens as the rope is pulled.

Meanings

  1. A running loop of rope, cord, or wire that closes when pulled, used for snaring, trapping, or hanging. The trapper set a wire noose along the rabbit run.
  2. A tightening trap or constraint from which it is hard to escape. Each new loan pulled the noose of debt a little tighter. figurative
  3. To catch or secure with a loop that tightens. He noosed the post with one practised throw of the lariat.

Word origin

Of uncertain origin, recorded from the 15th century; often traced to Old French 'nous' or Old Occitan 'nous' meaning knot, from Latin 'nodus' (knot).

Remember it

NOOSE keeps a loop right in its spelling - the double 'O' is the open eye of the knot before it closes.
