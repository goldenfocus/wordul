SHINY

adjective · 2 syllables · /'ʃaɪ.ni/

SHINY

What does "SHINY" mean?

Reflecting light because of a bright, polished, or glossy surface.

Meanings

  1. Having a smooth surface that reflects light; bright and gleaming. He drove up in a shiny new car still smelling of the showroom.
  2. Worn smooth and glossy through long use, especially of cloth. His old jacket had gone shiny at the elbows.
  3. New, exciting, and attention-grabbing, often superficially so. The team kept chasing every shiny new feature instead of fixing the basics. figurative

Word origin

From the noun 'shine' (Old English 'scinan', to shine) plus the adjective-forming suffix '-y'; in use since the late 16th century.

Remember it

SHINY is SHINE with the E swapped for Y - the light keeps going, the spelling just changes its tail.
