FREER

adjective · 2 syllables · /ˈfriɚ/

FREER

What does "FREER" mean?

Comparative form of free: more at liberty or less restricted than before.

Meanings

  1. More free; enjoying greater liberty or fewer restrictions. After paying off the loan, she felt far freer than ever.
  2. A person who frees or liberates others. History remembers him as a freer of the enslaved. archaic

Word origin

Comparative of 'free', from Old English 'freo', from a Proto-Indo-European root meaning beloved.

Remember it

FREER is FREE plus the eR of compaRison — one more E, one more degree of liberty.

A little poem

Each chain I dropped, the road grew wide;
freer, I found fewer places to hide.

couplet

What it teaches

Freer is a direction, not a destination; there is always one more rule you didn't know you were obeying.
