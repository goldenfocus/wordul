RUPEE

noun · 2 syllables · /ruːˈpiː/

RUPEE

What does "RUPEE" mean?

The basic monetary unit of India, Pakistan, and several other South Asian countries.

Meanings

  1. The standard currency of India and several neighboring nations, divided into smaller units such as paise. The street vendor charged twenty rupees for the chai.

Did you know?

  • The word rupee comes from the Sanskrit 'rupya', meaning 'wrought silver'-a reminder that the coin was once literally a measure of silver, not paper.

Word origin

From Hindi/Urdu 'rupaya', from Sanskrit 'rupya' meaning 'wrought silver' or 'coin of silver'; the term was standardized as a silver coin in 16th-century India.

Remember it

RUPEE sounds like 'roo-pee'-picture a kangaroo paying with a silver coin.
