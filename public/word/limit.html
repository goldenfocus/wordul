LIMIT

noun · 2 syllables · /'lɪm.ɪt/

LIMIT

What does "LIMIT" mean?

A point or boundary beyond which something may not pass.

Meanings

  1. The greatest or smallest amount, extent, or degree that is allowed or possible. The speed limit on the highway is 100 kilometres an hour.
  2. A boundary or edge of a region or area. The new houses lie just within the city limits.
  3. In mathematics, the value a function or sequence approaches as the input approaches some point. The limit of 1/n as n grows without bound is zero. technical
  4. To restrict or keep within bounds. We need to limit screen time before bed.

Did you know?

  • The word shares its Latin root 'limes' with the Roman frontier defenses also called the 'limes' - the fortified borderlines that marked the empire's edge.

Word origin

From Latin 'limes' (genitive 'limitis'), meaning a boundary path, border, or frontier; the same root that named the fortified frontiers of the Roman Empire, the 'limes'.

Remember it

LIMIT shares 'lim' with 'limes', the Roman frontier - a limit is a line you don't cross.
