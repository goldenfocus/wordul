FACET

noun · 2 syllables · /ˈfæs.ɪt/

FACET

What does "FACET" mean?

One of the flat polished surfaces of a gem, or one aspect of something.

Meanings

  1. A flat cut surface on a gemstone. Each facet of the diamond caught the light differently.
  2. A particular aspect or feature of a thing. Patience is just one facet of good teaching.
  3. One of the units of a compound eye in insects. A dragonfly's eye has thousands of tiny facets. technical

Did you know?

  • A dragonfly's eye is built from up to about 30,000 facets, each a tiny lens - which is why the word 'facet' literally means 'little face', from the French 'facette'.

Word origin

From French 'facette', a diminutive of 'face' meaning 'little face', from Latin 'facies' (face, appearance).

Remember it

FACET = a little FACE-T: every facet is one small face the object turns toward the light.
