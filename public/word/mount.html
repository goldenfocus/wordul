MOUNT

verb · 1 syllable · /maʊnt/

MOUNT

What does "MOUNT" mean?

To climb up onto, fix in place, or steadily increase, depending on context.

Meanings

  1. To climb onto or get up on something, such as a horse or platform. She put a foot in the stirrup and mounted in one smooth motion.
  2. To fix or attach something onto a support or backing. They mounted the television flush against the wall.
  3. To increase gradually in amount or intensity. As the deadline neared, the pressure mounted.
  4. To organize and launch an effort, such as a campaign or attack. The defense mounted a vigorous rebuttal.
  5. A mountain or hill, especially in names. Mount Everest draws hundreds of climbers each season.
  6. To make a storage device accessible to a computer's filesystem. You'll need to mount the drive before the backup can run. technical

Word origin

From Old French 'monter', 'to go up', from Latin 'mons, montis', 'mountain'; the noun and verb senses both trace to the idea of rising.

Remember it

MOUNT contains 'UP' if you read the U and the climb: to mount is always to go up - up a horse, up a wall, up in number.
