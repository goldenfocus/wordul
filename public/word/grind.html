GRIND

verb · 1 syllable · /ɡraɪnd/

GRIND

What does "GRIND" mean?

To crush or reduce something to small particles or powder by friction.

Meanings

  1. To reduce to small particles by crushing between hard surfaces. She ground the coffee beans fresh each morning.
  2. To sharpen, smooth, or shape by friction against an abrasive surface. The knife was ground to a fine edge.
  3. To rub harshly together, producing a harsh sound. He ground his teeth in his sleep.
  4. Tedious, exhausting, relentless routine or work. The daily commute was a real grind. informal
  5. To work hard and persistently, often at something repetitive. They ground away at the project for months. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'grindan' (to rub together, crush small); from Proto-Germanic, with the past tense 'ground' preserving the old strong-verb pattern.

Remember it
