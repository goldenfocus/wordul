CRUMB

noun · 1 syllable · /krʌm/

CRUMB

What does "CRUMB" mean?

A tiny fragment of bread, cake, or other food.

Meanings

  1. A small fragment broken off bread, cake, or similar food. She swept the crumbs off the table into her hand.
  2. A very small amount or scrap of something. He offered not even a crumb of comfort. figurative
  3. The soft inner part of a loaf, as opposed to the crust. A good sourdough has an open, airy crumb. technical
  4. To coat food with breadcrumbs before cooking. Crumb the fillets, then fry them golden.

Did you know?

  • The 'b' in 'crumb' is a fake: Old English 'cruma' had no b at all, and the letter was tacked on later to match the look of words like 'dumb' and 'thumb'.

Word origin

From Old English 'cruma' (fragment, bit); the silent 'b' was added by analogy with words like 'dumb' and 'thumb' and was never pronounced.

Remember it

CRUMB ends in a silent B, like its cousins thumB, dumB, and numB - the bread's tiny bit hides a tiny mute.
