FRAME

noun · 1 syllable · /freɪm/

FRAME

What does "FRAME" mean?

A rigid structure that surrounds, supports, or encloses something.

Meanings

  1. A border or case that surrounds and holds a picture, window, or door. She slid the photograph into a silver frame.
  2. The rigid supporting structure of an object, building, or vehicle. The car's steel frame absorbed the impact of the crash.
  3. A single still image in a sequence of film or video. He paused the movie on the exact frame where the clue appears. technical
  4. To put a border around, or to express something in a particular way. Politicians frame the same policy as either a cut or a saving.
  5. To produce false evidence so an innocent person seems guilty. He swore the planted gun was meant to frame him. informal

Word origin

From Old English 'framian' meaning to be helpful or to make progress, related to 'fram' (forward); the sense of a fitted structure developed in Middle English.

What it teaches
