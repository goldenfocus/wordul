DEATH

noun · 1 syllable · /dɛθ/

DEATH

What does "DEATH" mean?

The permanent ending of all vital functions and life in an organism.

Meanings

  1. The irreversible cessation of life in a living being. The doctors recorded the time of death as just after midnight.
  2. The personification of mortality, often shown as a robed skeleton. In the painting, Death plays chess with a weary knight. figurative
  3. The end, destruction, or ruin of something abstract. The new highway spelled the death of the old roadside diners. figurative

Word origin

From Old English 'dēaþ', from Proto-Germanic 'dauþuz', from the verbal root meaning 'to die'; related to German 'Tod' and Old Norse 'dauði'.

Remember it

DEATH contains 'eat' in the middle - even time consumes everything in the end.
